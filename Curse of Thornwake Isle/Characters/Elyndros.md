## **Elyndros Origin**

Elyndros was forged in the soaring halls of Aurethyl Spire, a sanctuary within the second layer of Mount Celestia where lantern-bearers were crafted to guide souls through their final dusk. His task was simple; be present, not involved. A quiet companion in the last moments of mortal life, nothing more.

But Elyndros listened too closely, stayed a little longer than he should and offered comforts not written in the doctrines of his order. He lingered beside grieving widows, calmed frightened childen and sometimes delayed a passing long enough for a family to say their last goodbyes. Mercy, as it turned out, was not always compatible with fate.

When Elyndros broke his oath, it wasn't out of defiance but out of mercy. He was assigned to ease the final moments of Amara Telys, an elderly woman whose son was due to return from a distant war but had not yet arrives. She clutched Elyndros' hand with trembling fingers and begged to let her see her son come home.

Her soul was marked for departure, her fate already sealed. But Elyndros saw terror in her eyes, he felt a surge of compassion and intervened, holding her life aloft for a few hours more.

When amara's son finally reached her bedside and she passes peacefully, Elyndros understood what he had done. His brethren did not punish him; they mourned him. Intervention threatened the balance they were sworn to uphold. And so, by mutual agreement, he surrendered his radiance. His memories sealed and he fell toward the mortal world.
## **A new life**
I don't remember the heavens people claim I'm from.
I remember only light, then earth.

I woke in a smoking crater in the foothills near the village of Rosehollow, half buried in soil and ash while startled farmers circled from a distance. They said the sunlight seemed strangely bright around me, even though the sky was overcast. They gave me a name, "Dawn", because the first thing I saw when I opened my eyes was the rising sun behind them.

The village took me in. fed me. Taught me their language. I learned quickly, too quickly, some whispered. Though my body looked human there were small things they noticed. My pulse is like a warm stone, not as erratic as humans. Two large scars on my back glow when exerting myself and I never seem cold.

Despite these differences, the village worried, they cared anyway. When little Tomas fell ill, I was only trying to help. His mother had stepped out, and the boy was trembling with fever. I touched his forehead, and something inside me flickered. Warmth surged from my chest to my fingertips. My scars lit like the sun breaking through the clouds. The boy's breathing eased and his eyes opened.

Word spread, as it always does in villages. This gift was comforting to some, unsettling to others. But it was undeniable.

## **Calling the Dawn**

Unsure of the source of this power, I took to quiet morning prayers on the hill outside Rosehollow, seeking whatever being had placed this warmth inside me. Days passed. Weeks.

Then, one morning as the first rays of light crested the horizon, I heard a voide like the world exhaling after a long night.

A voice that said simply:
"Rise, child. The day welcomes you"

Lathander

The Morninglord, bearer of renewal and fierce foe of undeath. I finally felt seen.

I dedicated myself to his path, not because he demanded it, but because his ideals matched the instinctive itch inside me to shelter others from darkness. He granted me strength, sharpened my healing gift, and named me a Dawnbringer, a herald of first light.

The title doesn't quite fit the name the villagers gave me, so I kept it only for official purposes. The name "Dawn" reminds me that before i served any god, I was saved by the kindness of mortals.

## **Purpose**

Now I walk with purpose, carrying a warmth I barely understand. I mend the wounded, guide those lost in shadow and, when needed, stand between evil and the dawn.

I don't chase my forgotten past, but it follows me nonetheless. I feel it in dreams, in the pull of distant starlight, in the strange calm that settles over me before danger.

Whatever I once was, whatever I am becoming, my purpose is now clear:

*If the world must face darkness, then let me be the light that reaches it first*
