
The [[Book of Eternity]] is one of the Nine Books of Nagash.  
It embodies the severance of souls from the natural cycle of life and death — not in death’s embrace, but in hollow, stagnant existence.  

It is the first known Book recovered in [[Tharion]], discovered within the ruins of [[Hollowfall]].

---

## Appearance

- Bound in grey-black hide, seemingly breathing when unwatched.
- Glyphs etched in silver and violet shimmer faintly and rearrange themselves over time.
- The spine bears Nagash’s rune: a broken hourglass fused with a crowned skull.
- Opening the Book causes a brief silence in a 30-foot radius — all sound mutes for 6 seconds.

---

## Passive Effects While Carried

- Healing spells within 500 feet are halved in effect.
- Resurrection spells automatically fail unless backed by a divine artifact.
- Animals refuse to approach within 300 feet.
- Dreams within a mile become fragmented, cold, and prophetic of endings.
- Carrying the Book for over 3 continuous days increases corruption accumulation.

---

## Deciphering Procedure

**Requirements**:
- Warded location (e.g., a consecrated ruin, sealed sanctum).
- Daily investment of 2 hours focused study.
- Arcana or Religion skill checks (DC 18) after each study session.

**Progression**:
- 1 success → Basic lore unlocked (theories on death severance).
- 2 more successes → Minor rituals and hexes appear.
- 3 more successes → Major rituals (portal creation) revealed.
- 5 cumulative successes → Partial vision of Nagash’s original immortality process.

**Failure Consequences**:
- Nightmares.
- Minor psychic damage (2d6) from glyph backlash.
- Disadvantage on saving throws vs. corruption for the next 24 hours.

---

## Contents and Lore Fragments

The Book is not purely instructional — it is also a philosophical treatise on death and eternity.

**Sample Excerpts**:
- "In the twilight of existence, the gods forget their creations. We must not."
- "Eternity is not life without death; it is the death of purpose, the grave of time."
- "Those who bind themselves beyond the cycle are not spared from suffering — they are made its architects."

**Unveiled Secrets**:
- Nagash’s early failures at soul-binding led to countless fragmented echoes in the Shadowfell.
- True severance requires not just killing, but **unwinding the memory** of the soul.
- Immortality without renewal leads to entropy — an existence of unchanging decay.

## Rituals Contained Within

---

### Ritual of Dusk Crossing (Mandatory for Act II)

**Purpose**: Open a portal between the Material Plane and the Shadowfell, or vice versa.

**Components**:
- Circle of pure shadow-ink sigils drawn with soul ash (obtained from a willing volunteer’s relic, not the person themselves).
- A reflective surface (large still water basin, mirror) as the focal anchor.
- 10 minutes uninterrupted chanting of the Rite of Sundering.

**Performance**:
- DC 17 Arcana or Religion check during casting.
- If successful, opens a portal lasting **6 hours**.
- Can be used both to enter Shadowfell and later to escape.

**Risks**:
- Upon closure, the area gains a permanent **Shadow Scar** (approx. 60-foot radius where natural light is dimmed and plant life dies).
- Entities from the Shadowfell sense the portal opening and may attempt to cross through if precautions are not taken.

---

### The Hollow Covenant (Optional)

**Purpose**: Sever a creature’s divine connection, making them "Eclipsed" — immune to divine spells, forsaken by their gods.

**Components**:
- Broken relic or defiled symbol of the target’s faith.
- Two silver chains blessed under a new moon.

**Performance**:
- 1-hour invocation.
- DC 18 Religion check.

**Consequences**:
- Target is immune to healing spells and resurrection, but gains advantage on death saving throws.
- Deity of the severed individual notices immediately.
- Possible transformation into an "Eclipsed Paladin" or "Forsaken Cleric" archetype.

---

### The Hourglass Reversal (Optional, Immortality Ritual)

**Purpose**: Delay death indefinitely by absorbing the destined lifeline of others.

**Components**:
- A soulgem crafted from three willingly given lives (symbolic sacrifices — a child, a warrior, and a priest).
- Binding Circle of Final Sundering — requiring 12 uninterrupted hours to draw.
- Blood of an ancient lineage (king, hero, or demi-god bloodline).

**Performance**:
- 1-night ritual under full moons.
- Each night requires a separate DC 20 Arcana check.

**Consequences**:
- The individual becomes *severed* from natural death but **draws endless shadow toward themselves**.
- Their emotions, memories, and desires slowly erode.
- Nearby souls weaken; allies begin aging faster or suffering hallucinations.
- **Ultimate Cost**: Over time, their presence alone blackens the land — replicating a miniature version of the Blackening event.

> **Note**: Even Nagash's early immortality experiments failed multiple times before achieving stability through further Books and rituals.

## Defensive Reactions (If Improperly Tampered)

- Necrotic Explosion (8d10 necrotic damage; DC 18 Dexterity save halves).
- Wraith Echo Summoning (manifestations of forgotten scholars, 2–4 depending on tampering intensity).
- Memory Scorch: Reader forgets the last hour of real time (minor disorientation, flavor consequence).

---

## Deity and Alignment Responses

- **Clerics and Paladins**: Immediate sense of revulsion.  
  Continued study risks losing divine blessings temporarily (DM call).
- **Warlocks, Wizards, Sorcerers**: Feel fascination and an urge to delve deeper.
- **Good-aligned characters**: Save against passive despair effects daily if carrying the Book.
