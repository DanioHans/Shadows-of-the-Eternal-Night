# Session – The Dead Frequency

## Overview

**Lin** contacts Ivor with a retrieval job. A smuggler named **Ossa Dren** — Lin's supplier and one of the few people who moved goods through the deep infrastructure beneath **Smokestack Row** — is dead. Killed in a tunnel collapse five days ago. Ossa kept an insurance policy against her most dangerous client: a **sealed metal case** containing a set of **Arcanum-stamped transit permits** and a single **wax-sealed construction schematic** that she was never meant to see. She stole it from a shipment she was transporting and hid it in her vault as leverage.

The case is still down there, locked behind Ossa's mechanical security in a converted pump room beneath the old **Sulfur Baths** in Smokestack Row. Lin wants it before **Caelan Dross** gets it — because Dross is already looking.

**Why Lin cares:** Ossa was Lin's primary source for underworld logistics. Through Ossa, Lin had indirect visibility into shipments moving beneath the city — who was paying, what was being moved, and where. Ossa's death blinds Lin. The insurance case doesn't just contain evidence — it contains the only remaining proof of who Ossa's most dangerous client was. If Dross retrieves and destroys it, that thread dies with Ossa, and whoever is running operations beneath the city becomes untouchable. Lin doesn't know what the construction project is. She just knows someone powerful is paying to keep it invisible, and that makes the case valuable.

**Why Dross cares:** Dross was hired to manage the logistics of Verrath's lattice construction — procuring materials, paying smugglers, keeping the supply chain off official Arcanum books. Ossa was one of his couriers. She was reliable until she wasn't — she copied a schematic from a shipment and kept it as insurance. Dross learned about it after her death and sent a team to retrieve it. He cannot allow physical evidence of the lattice project to surface.

**Why this adventure works for Ivor and Scrap:** The vault is beneath the deep Smokestack Row infrastructure, in an area where the lattice's energy makes spellcasting dangerous. The security is entirely mechanical — built by a woman who hated magic. The approach requires stealth, lockpicking, trap navigation, and physical problem-solving. Ivor's rogue skills and Scrap's construct nature are not just useful — they are essential.

**Party Level:** 5 **Estimated Duration:** 2–3 sessions **Location:** Smokestack Row → Sulfur Baths ruins → Ossa's Vault **Quest Type:** Contact Job (Lin → Ivor) **Tone:** Heist. Tense, physical, low-magic. Ivor leads, Scrap navigates.

## Hook

Lin finds Ivor when the party is in Smokestack Row or the Slums — ideally during downtime or travel between locations. She does not approach the full group. She pulls Ivor aside, speaks quietly, and makes the pitch to him directly.

> _"Ossa Dren is dead. Tunnel collapse, five days ago. You wouldn't know her name but you've eaten food she moved and walked streets she kept clear. She was mine."_

> _"She had something locked in her vault — a case she took from a shipment as insurance. The man who hired her wants it back. His name is Dross, and if he gets that case first, I lose every thread I have left in this city."_

> _"I need someone who can get in without making noise. Her vault is mechanical — no wards, no glyphs, just iron and gears. That's your language, not theirs."_

She nods toward where the rest of the party is.

> _"Bring them if you want. You'll need the muscle. But understand — down there, magic draws attention. The ground hums. Something under the district has been feeding energy into the stone for weeks and it makes spellwork loud. Your wizard casts a cantrip and every rat and lookout in the Row hears it. So keep him on a leash."_

**What Lin offers:** 150 gold. She'll negotiate down to 120 if Ivor pushes, but no lower. She also offers a smaller, more personal incentive — not the wizard's name, but a detail:

> _"The experiment that took your memories. You think it was an accident. I've heard differently. Bring me the case and I'll tell you what I heard."_

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** Lin is offering a rumour, not a revelation. What she knows (or claims to know) is that the experiment may have been deliberate rather than accidental. She does not know the wizard's name. She does not have proof. This is a thread to pull on later — enough to motivate Ivor without resolving his arc. If he presses her for more now, she shuts it down: _"Case first. Then we talk."_

**Bringing the party:** Lin expects Ivor to bring the others. She frames the anti-magic warning as tactical advice, not a suggestion to go solo. The party goes together. Ivor leads because Lin gave the job to him, not because anyone stays behind.

## Ossa Dren — The Dead Smuggler

Ossa was a mid-tier smuggler operating beneath Smokestack Row for roughly eight years. She moved restricted alchemical components, unlicensed enchanted goods, and building materials for clients who paid for discretion. She was not violent, not ambitious, and not stupid.

**Defining trait:** Absolute distrust of magic. Ossa grew up in Smokestack Row when the old smelting operations were shutting down. She watched magical "improvements" destroy livelihoods. She never used magical locks, magical traps, or magical communication. Everything in her operation was physical, mechanical, and manually maintained.

**How she died:** A tunnel adjacent to her usual route collapsed five days ago. The tunnel ran close to one of the lattice's radial channels — the same kind of construction the party found beneath Corvin's tenements if they completed the Rat King's Ransom. The collapse was caused by structural stress from the channel's energy draw, which has been destabilizing foundations across the district. Whether Dross engineered the specific collapse or simply took advantage of it is left to the DM. Either way, Ossa is dead and her vault is sealed.

**Relationship to Lin:** Professional, not personal. Ossa was Lin's best logistics contact — through her, Lin could track what was being moved beneath the city and by whom. With Ossa dead and the vault's contents at risk, Lin loses both her source and the only physical evidence of who Ossa's most dangerous client was.

## The Volatile Magic — How to Communicate It

The lattice's radial channels have destabilized the Weave in this part of Smokestack Row. Spellcasting near the vault is dangerous — not suppressed, but volatile.

**How to tell the players before they go down:**

Lin warns Ivor directly during the hook. This is the primary delivery method. Beyond that, use environmental cues on the approach:

- As the party enters Smokestack Row, Kyron or Samson (whoever is most attuned to magic) feels a low pressure behind their eyes — like the air before a thunderstorm. No check needed, just narrate it.
- If anyone casts Detect Magic or any other spell while still above ground in the area, describe the spell behaving strangely — flickering, pulling in a direction, feeling _taut_ rather than flowing. This is a free warning with no consequence yet.
- A local NPC (a squatter, a child, a beggar — someone who lives here) can mention it in passing if the party talks to anyone: _"Don't light magic down there. Ground's been angry for weeks. Old Teff tried a light cantrip in his cellar and blew out every window on the street."_

**Mechanical rules once underground near the vault:**

- **Any spell of 1st level or higher** requires a DC 13 Constitution saving throw from the caster. On failure: the spell still works, but triggers an **arcane surge** — a visible flash and a low boom. The caster takes 2d6 force damage. The surge is audible throughout the tunnel system and alerts anyone else down there.
- **Cantrips** trigger a DC 10 Constitution save with the same consequences on failure but only 1d6 damage.
- **Detection spells (Detect Magic, etc.)** automatically surge without a save. They interact directly with the lattice energy. 3d6 force damage, audible across the district.
- **Magic items function normally** — they are self-contained. Enchanted weapons, potions, Samson's staff — all fine.
- **Kyron's innate sorcery** — see Kyron's Moment below for how this interacts differently.

## Kyron's Moment — The Resonance

Kyron's Clockwork Soul sorcery is not learned magic imposed on the Weave — it is innate order written into his blood. When the party enters the volatile zone, everyone with magical ability feels the instability. But Kyron feels something different.

**Narrate this directly to Kyron's player when they descend:**

> _"The others feel the Weave straining — like walking on thin ice. You feel that too. But underneath it, beneath the instability, you feel something else. A rhythm. The energy down here isn't chaotic — it's structured. It pulses in a pattern, geometric and deliberate. Your clockwork glyphs flicker in response. Not painfully. Sympathetically. Like two instruments tuned to the same key."_

**The mechanical payoff:** At one point during the vault sequence (the DM chooses when), give Kyron a unique interaction. When the party faces a challenge that seems unsolvable without magic — a sealed mechanism that requires energy to activate, a collapsed section that needs force to clear — Kyron can attempt to channel his sorcery _with_ the lattice's frequency rather than against it.

**How it works:** Kyron makes a straight Charisma check (not a spell, not a save — this is his innate connection, not spellcraft). DC 13. On success: his clockwork glyphs align with the lattice's pulse and he can push a small amount of energy through the local Weave without triggering a surge. The effect is minor — equivalent to a cantrip's force — but it accomplishes what the party needs in that moment. On failure: standard surge, but Kyron learns something important — the lattice _almost_ responded to him. The connection is real even if he couldn't hold it.

**What this means for Kyron's arc:** This is the first concrete evidence that his Clockwork Soul nature interacts with structured arcane constructions in a way that nobody else's magic does. Samson can analyse the lattice. Kyron can _feel_ it. This plants a seed for his post-Blackening relevance without resolving anything now.

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** This moment should be brief and quiet. Don't make a speech about it. Narrate what Kyron feels, let his player react, move on. The weight comes from the table realising what happened, not from the DM underlining it.

## The Location — The Sulfur Baths

The **Sulfur Baths** are already established in your Smokestack Row notes as _"formerly heated by furnaces, now a fog-dense ruin rumoured to house something... watching."_ This adventure gives the Baths a concrete purpose.

### Above Ground

The Baths are a large, squat stone building with a partially collapsed roof. Dense fog clings to the interior — condensation from old pipes that still leak warm air from somewhere below. The fog is natural, not magical, but it limits visibility to about twenty feet inside the building.

**Exterior approach:**

- The main entrance is visible from the street. A rusted iron gate, partially open. Going in this way is easy but exposed — anyone watching from the street sees the party enter.
- A side entrance through a collapsed wall requires squeezing through rubble. DC 12 Acrobatics for anyone in heavy armour. Scrap and Ivor pass without a check. This approach is hidden from the street.
- **Dross's watchers:** Two men rotate shifts in a gutted building across the street, watching the main entrance. DC 13 Perception to spot them on approach. If Ivor specifically scouts ahead (encourage this), give advantage. If spotted, the party can choose to deal with them (distraction, misdirection, using the side entrance instead) or ignore them and move fast.

> 💡 **Ivor Note:** Scouting the approach is a classic rogue move. If Ivor's player doesn't think of it, have Lin mention it: _"Dross will have eyes on the place. Find them before they find you."_ This gives Ivor an immediate task that's entirely his.

**Interior — ground floor:**

- Three connected rooms: a former reception area, the main bathing hall (large, tiled, the central pool now empty and cracked), and a boiler room at the rear.
- The boiler room contains the descent point — a maintenance hatch in the floor behind the old furnace. The hatch is not locked or hidden. Ossa didn't bother — the security is below, not above.
- The fog makes the interior atmospheric but not mechanically dangerous. Use it for tension: shapes in the mist, dripping sounds, the party's own footsteps echoing off tile.

> 💡 **Scrap Note:** Scrap's darkvision and constructed resilience (no breathing, no body heat) make the fog irrelevant to him. While the others are wiping condensation from their faces and squinting, Scrap sees clearly. Narrate this: _"The fog doesn't touch you the way it touches them. You see the room for what it is — tile, stone, rust. Simple."_ This is a small moment but it establishes Scrap as comfortable in a space where the others aren't.

## Below Ground — The Tunnels

The maintenance hatch opens onto an iron ladder descending into old utility tunnels — part of Smokestack Row's original industrial infrastructure. These are not sewers. They are dry, narrow, stone-walled service corridors built for pipe maintenance and coal transport.

### Tunnel Layout

The tunnels form a rough T-shape:

- The **main corridor** runs north-south, roughly 200 feet long. The party enters from the north (the hatch).
- A **branch corridor** splits east about halfway down. This leads to Ossa's vault (the converted pump room).
- The **south end** of the main corridor connects to deeper infrastructure — older tunnels that eventually link to the city sewer network. This is how Dross's team enters.

### Dross's Team — Already Here

**Critical change from draft 1:** Dross's team is not a conditional encounter. They are **already in the tunnels** when the party arrives. They entered from the south roughly an hour ago and are working their way toward the vault. They do not know the party is coming. The party does not know they are here — yet.

**Evidence of Dross's team (main corridor):**

- DC 12 Perception (Scrap advantage — his tracking skills apply underground): fresh boot prints in the dust, heading south to north. Three sets. Recent.
- DC 13 Survival: the prints entered from the south end of the main corridor. They paused at the T-junction, then turned east toward the vault branch.
- A faint smell of lamp oil — Dross's people are carrying hooded lanterns.

**The timing pressure:** Dross's team is ahead of the party but moving slowly. They reached the vault door roughly 30 minutes before the party arrives at the T-junction. They are currently trying to get through the door and failing — the triple-tumbler lock is beyond their lockpick's skill, and they are debating whether to force it. The party will hear them before they see them: low voices and the scrape of tools on iron, echoing from the east branch.

This creates a genuine decision point for the whole party:

- **Move fast and confront them at the door.** Ivor's territory — intimidation, negotiation, or ambush.
- **Wait and let them fail, then move in after they leave.** Risky — they might succeed, or they might call for reinforcements.
- **Find an alternative entry to the vault.** This requires exploration (see Alternative Entry below).
- **Set up an ambush in the main corridor.** Queek and Merios territory — if it comes to a fight, the narrow tunnels favor the party.

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** The party should hear Dross's team before they reach the vault. This turns the adventure from "get in, solve traps, get out" into "get in, deal with competition, solve traps, get out." The presence of another team creates urgency that carries the entire session.

### Dross's Team

- **Hael** — the lead. Former city guard turned private contractor. Calm, professional, armed with shortsword and hand crossbow. Will talk before fighting. Not loyal to Dross — loyal to the payment. Carries a sealed note from Dross authorising "recovery of materials from the Dren property." The note mentions no names beyond Dross.
    - Use **Veteran stat block, HP reduced to 45.**
    - Hael knows he was hired to retrieve a sealed case from a dead smuggler's vault. He does not know what's in it. He does not care. If the party offers him more money than Dross is paying (200 gold — more than the party likely wants to spend), he will walk away. If they threaten him, he assesses the odds honestly and will back down if outmatched. He is not dying for someone else's secrets.
- **Pol** — the lockpick. Young, nervous, clearly out of his depth. He has been working on the vault door for 20 minutes and has jammed one tumbler. His Thieves' Tools modifier is +3 — not enough for a DC 16 lock. He knows it. He is visibly frustrated.
    - Use **Spy stat block, HP 20.**
- **Grenn** — the muscle. Large, quiet, follows Hael's lead. Carries a maul.
    - Use **Thug stat block, HP 32.**

**If the party confronts them at the vault door:**

Hael speaks first. He is not hostile — he is assessing. His opening line depends on how the party approaches:

- If the party is aggressive: _"We have a contract. You have weapons. Neither of us needs this to get ugly."_
- If the party is diplomatic: _"You're here for the same thing. That's inconvenient. Let's talk about how we solve that."_
- If Ivor takes the lead (encourage this): Hael recognises a professional and responds accordingly. He might offer a deal — split the contents, or let the party open the door since Pol clearly can't, in exchange for a cut.

**If the party avoids them entirely (alternative entry or ambush):** Dross's team remains a lingering presence. They might hear the party inside the vault and come investigating. They might leave and report to Dross. Either way, Dross learns someone else was there.

## The Vault — Ossa's Pump Room

### Alternative Entry

The vault has a second access point that Ossa used as an emergency exit: a narrow **drainage conduit** that connects the pump room to a parallel maintenance shaft. It is too narrow for a person in armour — but a fox can fit through easily.

> 💡 **Scrap Note:** Vixen (the fox companion) can scout through the conduit. This is a perfect Beastmaster moment. Scrap sends Vixen through, and describes (through Scrap's bond with the fox) what the fox sees and smells on the other side. This gives the party information about the interior — trap positions, layout, the location of the case — before they enter.

The conduit is also passable for a Small creature or a Medium creature willing to squeeze. DC 14 Acrobatics to get through without getting stuck. On failure: stuck for one round, requiring a DC 12 Strength check from the stuck character or an ally to pull free. Not dangerous, but noisy and time-consuming.

If Vixen scouts successfully, **all Perception DCs for traps inside the vault are reduced by 2** — the party knows roughly where to look.

---

### The Door — Ivor's Lock

A **triple-tumbler mechanical lock** built into a reinforced iron door. No magic, no wards, just excellent craftsmanship.

- **DC 16 Thieves' Tools** to pick. This is genuinely difficult.
- On a failure by 5 or more, one tumbler jams — DC 12 Thieves' Tools to reset before trying again.
- Ivor can use **Steady Hands** (describe him taking a breath, slowing down, feeling the mechanism) — if his player narrates his approach, consider giving him advantage or a +2 circumstantial bonus. Reward engagement.

> 💡 **Ivor Note:** Give this moment time. Describe the mechanism — the clicks, the resistance, the satisfying give when a tumbler catches. This is where being a rogue matters. Let it breathe.

**Alternative:** The door hinges are on the tunnel side. DC 14 Investigation to notice. Removing pins requires DC 13 Strength and makes noise — not arcane noise, just metal-on-metal. If Dross's team is still in the tunnels, they hear this.

### The Interior — Challenge Sequence

The vault is roughly 30 feet by 20 feet. A converted pump room — stone walls, exposed pipe brackets, a single desk against the far wall. Between the door and the desk, Ossa built a sequence of mechanical defences. These are not all traps in the traditional sense — some are obstacles, some are choices, some require cooperation.

**The room is dark.** No torches, no lanterns. Scrap's darkvision functions perfectly. Anyone else needs a light source — and carrying a torch while navigating traps is itself a complication (one hand occupied).

#### Challenge 1 — The Weighted Floor

The first ten feet of floor beyond the door are a **counterweight system**. The floor is a single balanced stone slab — stable when undisturbed, but responsive to weight. If more than 150 pounds stands on it at once, the far edge rises and tilts the slab, creating a ramp that slides everything toward the doorway. Not lethal — Ossa wanted intruders pushed out, not killed.

- **DC 13 Perception** (Scrap advantage) to notice the seam lines and the slight flex underfoot.
- **Solutions:**
    - Cross one at a time, light characters first. Characters under 150 lbs cross freely. Heavier characters (Scrap in full gear, Queek with weapons) must cross carefully — DC 12 Acrobatics to maintain balance.
    - Jam the counterweight. DC 14 Investigation to find the fulcrum point beneath the slab edge. A piton, an iron bar, or Scrap's hand wedged into the mechanism locks it in place.
    - Vixen crossed during the scout and is already on the other side. She can stand on the far edge — not enough weight to matter, but if Scrap directs her to the fulcrum she can scratch at it to show the party where to jam it.

> 💡 **Group challenge:** This obstacle requires the party to communicate and coordinate, not just one person to roll a check. Ivor might spot the seam, Scrap might identify the mechanism, Queek might provide the muscle to jam it.

#### Challenge 2 — The Pipe Maze

The middle section of the room is cluttered with old pump infrastructure — thick iron pipes running floor to ceiling, some horizontal at waist or chest height. Ossa deliberately left this section as-is because the pipe maze restricts movement and creates natural choke points. She added one enhancement: **two spring-loaded bolt launchers** hidden in pipe brackets, triggered by tripwires strung between pipes at shin height.

- **DC 14 Perception** to spot the tripwires (Scrap advantage — his eyes process fine details in low light that biological vision misses).
- **DC 13 Thieves' Tools** to disarm each tripwire.
- If triggered: bolt fires from a pipe bracket. +5 to hit, 1d8+2 piercing. The bolt is loud — metal on metal, ringing through the pipes. Anyone in the tunnels hears it.

**The navigation challenge:** Even with tripwires disarmed, moving through the pipe maze quietly requires care. **DC 11 Stealth** for anyone in medium or heavy armour. Failure doesn't trigger a trap — it just makes noise. Pipes ring when bumped. In the silence of the vault, every clang is amplified.

> 💡 **Scrap Note:** Scrap can navigate the pipes in a way nobody else can. His body is metal — when he bumps a pipe, the sound is different. Duller. His weight distribution is mechanical, predictable. Narrate this: _"You move through the pipes like they were built for you. Your metal frame doesn't catch or snag — it slides against the iron with a sound like recognition. The pipes don't ring when you touch them. They hum."_ No check for Scrap. The pipes are his element.

#### Challenge 3 — The Gear Lock (Scrap's Centrepiece)

The desk sits behind a final barrier: a **gear-driven floor mechanism** that Ossa installed over the pump room's original valve controls. When weight is placed on the trigger section (the final five feet of floor before the desk), the gears engage and two things happen simultaneously: a heavy iron plate drops from the ceiling to block retreat, and a mechanical arm swings the desk backward into a recessed wall cavity — taking the case with it.

This is not a trap that hurts. It is a trap that **takes the prize away.** If triggered, the case disappears into the wall and the retrieval becomes significantly harder (requiring the party to disassemble the wall mechanism from the outside, which takes an hour of noisy work).

- **DC 15 Perception** to spot the trigger section. Scrap gets advantage. Additionally, if Vixen scouted, the DC drops to 13.
- **Disarming the mechanism:**
    - **Ivor — Thieves' Tools DC 15:** Can jam the trigger from above, preventing engagement.
    - **Scrap — No check required, but a choice:** Scrap can see the gears through a floor grate. He understands the mechanism intuitively — it is a machine, and he is a machine. He has two options:
        - **Hold the drive gear.** He reaches through the grate and physically holds the gear still while someone else crosses to the desk. He must keep his hand in the mechanism for the entire crossing — roughly 30 seconds. During this time he cannot move or act. If something interrupts him (noise from the tunnels, an unexpected complication), he must choose: hold the gear or let go and respond.
        - **Reverse the mechanism.** He can rotate the drive gear backward, which disengages the trigger permanently but causes a grinding metallic shriek that echoes through the tunnels. Anyone in the tunnel system knows exactly where the party is.
    - **Kyron — The Resonance Moment.** If the mechanism has been triggered and the desk has retreated into the wall, or if the party cannot disarm it through conventional means, this is where Kyron's moment (described above) can occur. The mechanism requires energy to reset — a small pulse is enough. Kyron can try to channel his innate clockwork magic with the lattice's frequency to push the gears backward.

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** Design the encounter so that the party likely uses two or three of these options across the challenges — not all of them. If Ivor handles the door and the tripwires, Scrap handles the weighted floor and the gear lock, and Kyron contributes the resonance moment, each has had a clear turn in the spotlight.

## The Case — What It Contains

The sealed case is a flat metal box, approximately the size of a thick book. It is locked with a simple mechanical clasp — DC 10 Thieves' Tools, trivial for Ivor after the triple-tumbler door.

Inside:

- **Three Arcanum transit permits** — official documents authorising transport of "arcane infrastructure materials" through city sub-channels. Each is stamped with the **Royal Arcanum Infrastructure Office** seal and signed with an indecipherable initial. They are dated within the last two months.
- **A single folded construction schematic** — hand-drawn, precise, showing a cross-section of a **crystalline node** embedded in a chamber beneath the city. The node matches the one the party found in the Rat King adventure (if completed) or is new information (if not). Annotations in Arcanum shorthand describe "ley convergence amplification" and "radial channel alignment." The schematic is not a complete map — it shows one node and its immediate connections. But it is unmistakably a piece of a larger design.

**What this is NOT:** It is not a ledger. It does not have "more entries" the players can ask about. It is three permits and one schematic. The information is finite, concrete, and immediately useful. The permits prove institutional involvement. The schematic proves the construction is deliberate and sophisticated. Together with evidence from previous adventures, the picture tightens.

> 💡 **Samson Note:** This is Samson's contribution moment — after the heist, not during it. He is the one who looks at the schematic and understands what it means. If the party has the Rat King chit or the Silverward "infrastructure assessment" notice, he can connect the dots. Let him have this analytical payoff. His value was not in the vault — it is in what the vault's contents mean.

## Resolution

### Returning to Lin

Lin meets Ivor at a pre-arranged location (she does not come to the Baths). She examines the case, takes one of the transit permits for herself (her payment for the information network she's rebuilding), and lets the party keep the rest including the schematic.

She pays the agreed gold. Then:

> _"The experiment. You asked."_

She pauses.

> _"Ossa moved goods for a lot of people. One of her clients — years ago, before the city work started — transported research materials for a wizard working out of the Arcanum annex in the Third Kingdom. Experimental magic. Memory displacement. Ossa said the wizard wasn't trying to erase anyone's mind. He was trying to copy it. The erasure was a side effect."_

> _"That's all I have. Ossa never gave me the name. She might not have known it."_

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** This gives Ivor something real but incomplete. His memory loss may not have been an accident — it was a side effect of an experiment in memory _duplication_, not destruction. This raises new questions without answering old ones: who wanted to copy his memories? Why? Is the copy still out there? These are threads for later, not now.

### What the Players Learn

Combined with previous adventures, the evidence chain now looks like this:

- **Silverward Dissonance** (completed): Something subterranean is feeding energy into the city's wards. The Arcanum called it "infrastructure assessment activity."
- **The Dead Frequency** (this adventure): Official Arcanum transit permits and a construction schematic prove the subterranean project is institutional, deliberate, and large-scale. Materials were moved through smuggling channels to avoid oversight.
- **Rat King's Ransom** (if completed): A lattice node beneath Smokestack Row, built using Arcanum notation, connected to a network pointing toward the Scholar's Descent.
- **The Missing Inspector** (if completed later): A city-wide geometric pattern converging on the Scholar's Descent, actively suppressed by Dross.

The party does not need to have completed all of these. Any two provide enough to know that something large, arcane, and hidden is being built beneath Caer Hollow by someone with Arcanum connections. The Dead Frequency's schematic is the most concrete piece of physical evidence — it shows _what_ is being built, not just that something is wrong.

## Spotlight Tracking — DM Reminders

**Ivor owns:** The hook (Lin talks to him), the approach (scouting watchers), the lock (triple-tumbler door), social/tactical decisions with Dross's team. He is the party's leader for this adventure.

**Scrap owns:** Environmental navigation (fog, darkness, pipes), mechanical understanding (weighted floor, gear lock), Vixen scouting, and the feeling of being _at home_ in a place built from machines.

**Kyron gets:** The resonance moment — one scene where his innate magic connects with the lattice in a way Samson's can't. Brief, quiet, significant.

**Samson gets:** The analytical payoff after the heist — reading the schematic, connecting the evidence. His contribution is cerebral, not physical. Frame it as essential but distinct from the vault work.

**Merios and Queek:** Muscle, distraction, support. If Dross's team becomes a fight, it's their fight. Merios's wings and Queek's ferocity matter in the narrow tunnels. They are not sidelined — they are in support, which is a healthy shift.
