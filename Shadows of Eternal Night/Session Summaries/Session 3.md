1. Session 3 summary: Day 3. Night settled poorly over the Severed Mug. No one trusted the establishment enough to sleep soundly, so Scrap, untiring and ever-watchful, took the night’s vigil. Yet even the Warforged’s mechanical patience wasn’t immune to distraction. His gaze wandered to the curious happenings of slum life beyond the tavern windows—people moving in shadows, laughter that didn’t belong, alleyways that whispered. When the dawn crawled in, Scrap had failed to notice anything useful. Jaspin never came. Samsons, his back aching from a night on a bed unworthy of the term, approached the barkeep Gareth that morning, asking if Jaspin had appeared. The answer was a blunt “No.” Further probing about Jaspin’s whereabouts earned only a stony glance and a curt suggestion to "ask anyone but me." Back in their cramped room, the party gathered to strategize. Ideas flew and fell until Queek, ever the wildcard, made a proposal—sell him. As a Skaven, alien to these lands, perhaps his appearance would lure Jaspin or someone connected to him. The plan was simple: bait a trap with Queek as the prize. Scrap took the task of binding Queek, ensuring the ropes looked secure but could be slipped in a moment's notice. (A fine knot indeed, with a 21 on his Survival check.) Merios hoisted Queek over his shoulder, a “caged beast” ready for market. When the group passed Gareth again, asking for directions to a marketplace, the barkeep raised an eyebrow. “Didn’t that rat sit with you last night? Ate soup, if I recall.” But no further questions were asked as the party reclaimed their weapons and left. The slum market reeked of stale meats and desperation.
    
2. A butcher’s stall caught their attention. Merios took the lead, offering Queek for sale. Aaron, the butcher, looked unimpressed. “Rat meat ain’t worth the salt to cure it,” he said. But he knew of someone who might be interested—an eccentric with a penchant for animal experiments, found down the road at the old tannery. “Smells like piss. Can’t miss it.” Samsons, distracted by the sight of rotting meat barrels, couldn’t resist purchasing a particularly long, questionable sausage. He was far too pleased with himself. The party followed Aaron’s directions into a dead-end alley choked with foul air. Two men in grey cloaks stood guard. Ivor, confident in his rogue’s tongue, approached to sell Queek. But it was Merios who stepped forward with a pitch that would make even a crooked merchant blush: “Listen, fellas, this rat? This is the finest rat you’ll ever lay eyes on. Never been a better rat. People are talking about it. Big people. You won’t find a better deal, believe me.” The men exchanged glances. Maybe amused, maybe curious. They didn’t bite, but they hinted at someone who might. Only two of the party could enter. Ivor and Merios, Queek still bound, followed them into the tannery’s dim interior. The air thickened with chemical rot, but both men steeled themselves against it. Inside, another figure greeted them. Queek waved with bound hands, trying to appear exotic. “What’s your name?” he chirped.
    
3. “Theo,” the man replied, eyeing the Skaven. He mentioned Jaspin was downstairs, working in the basement—accessible only from outside through a trapdoor. Outside, the party scaled a low wall into a yard. The trapdoor lay before them, secured by a heavy chain and padlock. Ivor knocked. A hissing sound responded. Kyron followed suit, the hiss growing louder. The air soured. Ivor, undeterred, picked the lock (25 Sleight of Hand), catching the chain before it clattered to the ground. As the doors creaked open, a cloud of poison gas puffed into his face, burning him for 4 damage. Blinking away the sting, he assured the party it was safe. It was not. The moment they descended, their nostrils burned with the foul stench. Kyron, ever eager, opened the next door without a care. He was wrong. A crude trap triggered, releasing three oversized rats. One lunged at Kyron. Samsons, in a moment of bizarre strategy, hurled his prized sausage at a distant rat, hitting it squarely for a mighty 1 damage. The rat was not impressed. Queek, freed from his ropes by Merios, sprang into action. Vaulting onto a crate, he skewered one of the rats with a perfect strike (natural 20), impaling it in a single brutal thrust. The sausage-eating rat advanced on Queek but found only his shield. Kyron retaliated, incinerating another rat with a blast of scorching flame. Merios, not to be outdone, unleashed an Eldritch Blast, dispatching the third. The room fell silent, save for Samsons, who eyed a surprisingly clean rug. Naturally, he wanted it. As he bent to roll it up, the rug unrolled itself—teeth bared, flesh twisting—a mimic! The mimic lashed out, wrapping Samsons in suffocating folds.
    
4. The party hacked, stabbed, and blasted at the creature, but their strikes risked hitting Samsons. Scrap wedged his blade beneath the mimic’s grip, prying it off Samsons just long enough for Kyron to attempt a pin—he missed, but the grip loosened. The mimic retaliated, striking at Scrap. Samsons, freed at last, sought revenge. Magic Missiles shot from his hand, slamming into the mimic for 11 forceful points. The combined assault finally felled the creature. Samsons was not pleased with how close he’d come to being a rug burrito, though his spirits lifted when he found a basilisk skull stashed in a nearby barrel. Ivor’s attention shifted to a door in the corner—no handle, but no challenge either. With a swift flick of his tools, it opened to reveal Jaspin, hunched over a cluttered desk. The smuggler had no chance. The party surged forward, tied him up, and in a bizarre twist of irony, Samsons retrieved his sausage from the fallen rat and used it to gag Jaspin. With four of them carrying the struggling smuggler, the group prepared to make their exit. Queek and Samsons lingered, searching the room. They uncovered a ledger, a journal page, and a dead rat marked with a strange, burned symbol. These were swiftly pocketed. As they emerged into the alley, Theo and the grey-cloaked men were still there. Kyron attempted a distraction, using Thaumaturgy to mimic the sound of falling crates. It fell flat. The men were unmoved. Still, the party slipped by, their pace measured, their captive bound. Until, further down the street, a gang of thugs blocked their path. Molerats, by the look of them. Tensions bristled.
    
5. Samsons acted quickly, casting Enlarge on Merios, who unfurled his wings, now spanning wide as a ship’s sail. Carrying Jaspin, he took to the sky. The thugs, caught between awe and disbelief, let the rest of the party pass unchallenged. At the Bastion, Captain Malma stood waiting at the doors. She gave them a long, measured look. “Well then. You found him. You know where the rooms are. Use them. Freshen up. You reek.” Samsons, weary and still irked about his mimic encounter, handed Captain Malma the dead rat with the branded symbol. “You’ll want a cleric to look at this,” he said, frowning. “It stinks of curses.”
