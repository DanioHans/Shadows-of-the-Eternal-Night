### **Introduction to Dungeons & Dragons**

Dungeons & Dragons is a **storytelling game where you take on the role of an adventurer** in a fantasy world. I act as the **Dungeon Master (DM)**, meaning I control the world, its inhabitants, and the challenges you face. Your choices shape the story, and together, we create an adventure.

You can **try to do anything**—talk to NPCs, fight enemies, explore ruins, or come up with creative solutions to problems. Whether you succeed depends on **your character’s abilities** and a roll of the dice.

At its core, D&D is about **teamwork, problem-solving, and creativity**. There’s no “winning” or “losing” in the traditional sense—it’s about the journey and how you shape the story.

### **Important Rules to Remember**

#### **1. The Dice Decide (Rolling a d20)**

- Most of the time, when you want to do something difficult, you’ll roll a **20-sided die (d20)** and add a number based on your character’s skills.
- If you roll **high**, you succeed. If you roll **low**, you might fail—or something unexpected happens.
- I’ll tell you what number you need to roll to succeed.

#### **2. Your Character’s Abilities**

- Your character has **six abilities** that define what they’re good at:
    
    - **Strength** (STR) – Physical power
    - **Dexterity** (DEX) – Agility and reflexes
    - **Constitution** (CON) – Endurance and toughness
    - **Intelligence** (INT) – Knowledge and logic
    - **Wisdom** (WIS) – Awareness and insight
    - **Charisma** (CHA) – Influence and presence
- Most actions will involve one of these stats, plus a **skill** related to it.
    

#### **3. Advantage & Disadvantage**

- Sometimes, things will be in your favor (**advantage**) or against you (**disadvantage**).
- **Advantage**: Roll **two d20s** and take the higher number.
- **Disadvantage**: Roll **two d20s** and take the lower number.

#### **4. Combat Basics**

- **Turn Order**: In battle, we take turns based on an **initiative roll** (rolling a d20 + Dexterity).
- **Actions on Your Turn**:
    - **Move** – You can move up to your speed (usually 30 feet).
    - **Attack** – Try to hit an enemy (roll a d20, add your attack bonus).
    - **Use an Ability or Spell** – Special moves or magic your character has.
    - **Interact** – Talk, use an object, or try something creative.

#### **5. Hit Points (HP) & Death**

- **HP = Health**. If it drops to **0**, you fall unconscious.
- While unconscious, you **roll death saving throws** (d20):
    - **10 or higher** = 1 success (3 successes = you stabilize).
    - **9 or lower** = 1 failure (3 failures = you die).
- A **healing spell or potion** can bring you back up.

#### **6. Roleplaying & Creativity**

- You **don’t have to speak in character**, but you can if you want!
- If you want to try something outside the rules, ask—I’ll usually let you **roll to see if it works**.
- There’s no single “right” way to play—do what **feels fun**.
