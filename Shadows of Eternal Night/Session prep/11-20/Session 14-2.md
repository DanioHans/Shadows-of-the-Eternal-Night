# Session – The Crown Speaks

## Overview

The Crown issues its first public address in over a year, responding to growing unrest across Caer Hollow. The venue is the **Moonstep Terrace** — a wide elevated platform at the edge of the High Ring, overlooking a broad civic plaza where the High Ring meets the Scholar's Descent. Hundreds of citizens attend. The speech is delivered not by the King but by **Lord Castellan Aldren Graves**, the Crown's appointed voice.

The Mawrats — a slum gang who've been growing bolder as the city destabilizes — use the gathering as an opportunity. What starts as heckling and provocation escalates into thrown objects, smoke, and violence. The crowd fractures. The City Guard struggles to hold the line. The party is pulled into the chaos — protecting civilians, confronting Mawrats, and ultimately helping the Guard restore order.

This is the session where the story moves on its own. The party witnesses institutional failure, feels the city's anger directed at them personally, and ends the day understanding that Caer Hollow is no longer safe.

**Party Level:** 5 **Estimated Duration:** 1 full session (3–4 hours) **Location:** Moonstep Terrace Plaza, High Ring border → surrounding streets **Tone:** Political tension → crowd chaos → combat → resolution **Absent:** Ivor (player absent)

---

## The Venue — Moonstep Terrace Plaza

The Moonstep Terrace is a stone overlook built into the southern edge of the High Ring, designed for royal addresses and festival proclamations. Below it, a broad civic plaza — roughly 200 feet wide, 300 feet deep — occupies the transitional space where the High Ring's white stone walls give way to the Scholar's Descent's older architecture. The plaza is paved, open, and ringed by buildings on three sides with the Terrace wall on the fourth.

Today the plaza is packed. Easily 500–800 people. The Crownwatch has set up a security perimeter around the Terrace itself — no civilians on the platform. City Guard lines the edges of the plaza. The crowd fills the middle.

**Key landmarks in the plaza (use for tactical reference during the chaos):**

- **The Speaker's Terrace** — elevated stone platform, 15 feet above the plaza floor. Graves stands here with two Crownwatch officers. A large royal banner hangs behind him.
- **The Fountain of Names** — a circular stone fountain in the plaza's centre, currently dry. During the chaos this becomes a rally point, high ground, and landmark for navigation.
- **East Market Arcade** — a covered colonnade of shops along the eastern edge. During the chaos, civilians flee into this for cover. Mawrats use the columns for concealment.
- **South Street Exits** — two wide streets leading south into the Scholar's Descent. These become bottlenecks when people try to flee.
- **West Alley Network** — narrow alleys along the western edge, connecting to residential streets. Mawrats use these to enter and exit the plaza.

## Before the Speech — The Gathering

The party arrives at the plaza with the crowd. They are not summoned — they heard about the address through town criers, posters, or word of mouth. They choose to attend.

**Describe the approach:** The crowd is thick. People of every class and district are here — merchants from Eastveil, workers from Smokestack Row, robed Arcanum students, temple clergy, families. The mood is tense but hopeful. People want answers. The fact that the Crown is speaking at all is being taken as a sign that someone is finally listening.

**The party in the crowd — directed moments:**

Use these as the party moves through the crowd toward a viewing position. Don't dump them all at once — scatter them across a few minutes of narration.

- A merchant the party has helped (from Silverward or Eastveil) spots them and nods. _"Glad you're here. Maybe they'll listen to people who've actually seen it."_
- A cluster of Smokestack Row residents eye the party with mixed expressions. One of them recognises them from the slums. _"You lot. You were down in the Row, weren't you? Near Corvin's buildings."_ Not hostile, but not warm.
- **Scrap** draws attention. A group of children stare openly. One waves. Another whispers to a friend: _"Is that a golem? Can it talk?"_ An older man steers his wife away from Scrap with a hand on her shoulder, not looking at him.
- **Kyron** — someone in the crowd mutters _"two of them"_ when they see Kyron and Merios together. A nearby woman clutches a holy symbol — not of any temple the party recognises. The hostility isn't loud. It's the kind of quiet fear that doesn't need words.
- **Samson** — if wearing Arcanum robes or identifiable as a student: a protestor with a crudely painted sign (_"THE ACADEMY BLEEDS THE CITY"_) points at him. _"Oi — you one of theirs? Come to make sure we stay quiet?"_ The people around the protestor don't intervene. Some nod.
- **Queek** — people give him a wide berth. A child screams. A guard starts toward him, then recognises him and waves the guard off with a grimace. Queek's presence is tolerated, not accepted.

> 💡 **DM Tip:** Rotate these across the party. One moment per player. Keep them brief — two sentences of narration, one NPC reaction. The point is that the party feels the crowd's mood on a personal level before the speech begins.

## The Speech — Full Text

**Read this aloud.** Paraphrase if you prefer, but these words are designed to be delivered as written. Graves speaks clearly, formally, and with conviction. He believes what he is saying. He is wrong about most of it, but he does not know that.

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** The speech should take approximately 4–5 minutes to read aloud. This is intentional. The length creates an opportunity for the crowd's mood to shift during the delivery. Watch your players — when they start looking restless or exchanging glances, the crowd is feeling it too. Use that.

---

**Graves steps to the front of the Terrace. The crowd falls mostly quiet. A few shouts from the back, quickly hushed. He waits for silence. Then he speaks.**

> _"Citizens of Caer Hollow. Subjects of the Crown of Velaris."_

> _"I am Lord Castellan Aldren Graves, speaking today with the full authority and blessing of His Majesty, King Halvar Thorne."_

**Pause. Let the crowd register: the King is not here.**

> _"His Majesty wished me to convey his deep awareness of the difficulties facing this city in recent weeks. Reports of disruption, of damage to homes and livelihoods, of unease — these reports have reached the palace, and they have been heard."_

> _"The Crown does not take lightly the concerns of its people. Every report filed by the City Guard, every petition submitted through proper channels, has been reviewed by the appropriate authorities."_

**Beat 2 — The Arcanum's Line**

> _"Following consultation with the Royal Arcanum, I can assure you that the disturbances affecting certain districts are consistent with naturally occurring fluctuations in the Weave — seasonal shifts in ambient magical energy that, while unsettling, are well within the boundaries of known patterns."_

> _"The Arcanum's assessment is clear: there is no evidence of a coordinated threat, no indication of hostile magical activity, and no reason to believe that public safety is at risk beyond the ordinary adjustments that any living city must endure."_

**The crowd murmurs. "Seasonal fluctuations" does not describe what happened to Maret's shop or Corvin's buildings.**

> _"I understand that these words may ring hollow to those who have suffered damage or loss. The Crown recognises that understanding a cause does not erase its impact. To that end, the palace has authorised the following measures."_

**Beat 3 — The Measures**

> _"First: additional Crownwatch patrols will be deployed to the Eastveil Commons, Smokestack Row, and the Silverward for the duration of the assessment period. Your safety is our priority."_

> _"Second: a compensation fund, administered through the City Guard, will be made available to residents who have suffered verifiable property damage as a direct result of magical disruption. Claims may be filed at the Crownwatch Bastion beginning tomorrow."_

> _"Third: the Royal Arcanum will conduct a comprehensive review of the city's enchanted infrastructure to ensure that all public wards, streetlights, and civic enchantments are functioning within safe parameters. This review is expected to take four to six weeks."_

**Crowd reaction: the compensation fund draws some nods. The timeline — four to six weeks — draws groans and angry muttering. People have been waiting for weeks already.**

**Beat 4 — The Warning**

> _"Finally, I must address a matter of civic responsibility."_

> _"In recent days, the Crown has become aware of individuals and groups conducting unauthorised investigations into the city's magical infrastructure. While the Crown appreciates the spirit of civic concern, I must be clear: interference with enchanted infrastructure, ley conduits, or any component of the city's arcane foundation is a criminal offence under Article Fourteen of the Arcane Conduct Code."_

> _"These systems are complex, delicate, and dangerous when handled by those without proper training. The Crown cannot guarantee the safety of those who choose to act outside the law, and will prosecute those whose actions endanger public safety — regardless of their intentions."_

**This is the line that breaks the room. The crowd goes from frustrated to hostile. Shouts from the back: _"You're threatening US?"_ — _"We're the ones getting hurt!"_ — _"Where was Article Fourteen when my shop exploded?"_**

**Beat 5 — The Close**

> _"Citizens of Caer Hollow — the Crown hears you. The Crown stands with you. And the Crown will act in your defence, as it always has, and always will."_

> _"In the name of His Majesty, King Halvar Thorne — be patient. Be calm. And trust in the institutions that have kept this city safe for generations."_

> _"That is all."_

**Graves steps back. The crowd does not applaud.**

## The Mawrats — Who They Are Today

The Mawrats are a slum gang from Smokestack Row — small-time protection rackets, cursed trinket sales, petty intimidation. Normally they're a nuisance, not a threat. But the city's destabilisation has been good for business. Fear sells. Cursed charms sell better when people believe in curses. The Mawrats have been growing — more recruits from displaced workers, more territory as the Guard pulls resources toward the High Ring.

Today they are at the speech because their leader — a broad, scarred woman named **Varra Kell** — sees an opportunity. Not to overthrow the Crown. Not to make a political statement. To **demonstrate that the Crown cannot protect this city**, which makes the Mawrats' own "protection" more valuable. If the City Guard looks weak in front of hundreds of people, Smokestack Row's residents have one less reason to trust the law and one more reason to pay the Mawrats.

Varra has brought **12–15 Mawrats** to the speech, scattered through the crowd in plain clothes. They are not soldiers. They are thugs, pickpockets, and brawlers. Their plan is:

1. Heckle during the speech (already happening by beat 4).
2. Throw objects at the stage after the speech ends.
3. Start fights with City Guard when the Guard responds.
4. Use the chaos to loot nearby shops and demonstrate that the Guard cannot maintain order.
5. Disappear into the alleys when the Crownwatch mobilises.

They are **not trying to kill anyone**. They want spectacle and profit.

## Escalation System

The disruption progresses through **four phases**. Each phase escalates naturally from the previous one. You control the pace — if the party intervenes early and effectively, the escalation slows or stops. If they hesitate or the dice go badly, it accelerates.

### Phase 1 — Provocation (Immediately After the Speech)

**What happens:** The crowd is angry but not violent. The Mawrats, scattered through the crowd, begin escalating. They throw rotten food at the Terrace. One throws a clay bottle that shatters against the royal banner. Others shove nearby citizens and shout slogans they've rehearsed: _"The Crown hides!"_ — _"Pay us in gold, not words!"_ — _"Seasonal fluctuation THIS!"_

**What the party sees:**

- Objects flying toward the stage. City Guard moving to intercept but unsure who's throwing.
- Citizens near the Mawrats getting shoved and scared. A woman falls. A man shields his daughter.
- **Scrap:** A thrown bottle shatters near him. Vixen bristles. A child nearby flinches and grabs Scrap's leg — the same kind of instinctive trust from earlier. _"Everyone is moving but you and the child. What do you do?"_
- **Kyron:** A Mawrat near him shoves a bystander into Kyron. The bystander looks at Kyron, sees a tiefling, and recoils — afraid of Kyron rather than the thug who pushed them. The Mawrat laughs.

**Party options:**

- Identify the Mawrats in the crowd: DC 13 Perception to spot coordinated movement. Scrap gets advantage (pattern recognition).
- Protect nearby civilians: no check needed — just action.
- Confront a Mawrat directly: Intimidation DC 12 to back one off. They're brave in a group, not alone.
- Alert City Guard: the Guard is already aware but stretched thin. Pointing out specific Mawrats helps — the Guard can focus.

**If the party acts effectively here:** Phase 2 still happens (the Mawrats have too many people to stop with one intervention) but the Guard has better targeting and the escalation is slower.

**If the party doesn't act:** Phase 2 happens within 2 minutes.

---

### Phase 2 — The Brawl

**What happens:** Multiple fights break out simultaneously across the plaza. The Mawrats aren't all fighting — some are instigating fights between civilians and the Guard, then stepping back. A Guard shoves a protestor who wasn't a Mawrat. The protestor's friends respond. Now it's not just Mawrats versus Guard — it's pockets of genuine civilian anger mixing with orchestrated violence.

**What the party sees:**

- Three distinct fight clusters across the plaza. The Guard is trying to contain all three and failing.
- **Sergeant Halna** is on the ground, shouting orders, visibly overwhelmed. She has maybe 15 Guards for 500+ people.
- A Mawrat with a cudgel is beating a downed Guard near the Fountain of Names. This is the first real violence — not a shove, an actual attack.
- Smoke begins rising from the East Market Arcade — someone (Mawrat) has set a small fire in a shop awning. Not a building fire yet, but it's spreading.

**Combat encounter (optional, scale to your table):**

If the party engages the Mawrats directly, use the following:

**Mawrat Thug (use Thug stat block, MM p.350):** AC 11, HP 32, Multiattack (2x mace, +4, 1d6+2 each). They fight in pairs. They flee when alone or below 10 HP. They are not brave.

**Mawrat Agitator (use Bandit stat block, MM p.343):** AC 12, HP 11. Carries smoke pots and a shortsword. Their job is to throw smoke, start fires, and run. They don't stand and fight. If cornered, they surrender immediately and claim to be "just a citizen."

**Encounter groups (use as many as needed):**

- **Fountain cluster:** 2 Thugs beating a downed Guard. If the party intervenes, combat. If they win in 2 rounds or less, nearby civilians cheer — the party is visibly helping.
- **Arcade fire:** 1 Agitator feeding the fire with lamp oil. 1 Thug keeping bystanders away. Stopping the fire requires either putting it out (DC 12 Athletics with water from the fountain, or creative magic — but see below) or pulling the Agitator away.
- **South exit bottleneck:** 1 Thug and 1 Agitator blocking a street exit while people try to flee, demanding coin to pass. Classic protection racket in real time.

> 💡 **Scrap in combat:** Scrap is AC 18 with a battleaxe. In a brawl with thugs, he is genuinely intimidating. Describe the Mawrats' reactions — they've never fought a Warforged. One freezes. Another swings at him and the weapon bounces off his armour with a clang. His stillness in chaos is terrifying. Let his player feel powerful here.

> 💡 **Kyron in combat:** If Kyron casts a spell, it works normally — this isn't the Dead Frequency's volatile zone. But the crowd sees it. A tiefling casting magic in a riot. Some civilians flinch away from him even though he's helping. Describe the double-edged nature: his spell stops a thug, but a nearby family scrambles away from _him_. This is the cost of being a visible sorcerer in a frightened city.

**If the party splits their efforts across multiple clusters:** Reward this. Each cluster they resolve reduces the chaos. Track it loosely — if they handle 2 of 3 clusters, Phase 3 is shorter and less dangerous.

**If the party focuses on one cluster:** The others escalate. The fire spreads. The downed Guard at the fountain takes more damage. The bottleneck gets uglier.

---

### Phase 3 — The Riot Edge

**What happens:** The plaza has tipped. It's no longer isolated fights — it's a crowd-wide surge of panic and anger. People are running in every direction. The Guard lines have broken in two places. Graves has been evacuated. The Crownwatch on the Terrace is holding the platform but not advancing into the crowd — their orders are to protect the stage, not the plaza.

**What the party sees:**

- **Varra Kell** — the Mawrat leader — is now visible. She's standing on the rim of the Fountain of Names, arms raised, shouting to the crowd. Not to the Mawrats — to the _civilians_. She's turning the riot into a recruitment pitch:

> _"Where are your guards NOW? Where is your KING? The Crown speaks and the city BURNS! Who protects Smokestack Row? Who protects YOUR street? NOT THEM!"_

She is charismatic in a brutal way. Some civilians are listening. Others are too scared to care. But she is making a point, and the longer she talks, the more damage the Mawrats' reputation gains from this event.

- **Halna** reaches the party (or they reach her). She is bleeding from a cut above her eye. She is furious but disciplined:

> _"I have twelve Guards still standing and three hundred people between me and that woman on the fountain. I can't reach her without trampling civilians. YOU can. Get her down. Get her quiet. The Crownwatch won't move until I request it and I am NOT requesting it — those armoured bastards will crack skulls without asking whose skull it is."_

This is the party's clear objective: **reach Varra Kell at the fountain and end her spectacle.**

**The path to Varra:** The crowd is a physical obstacle. Moving through it requires either:

- **Athletics DC 13** to push through (risking shoving civilians).
- **Stealth DC 14** to slip through gaps (Kyron or a smaller character).
- **Persuasion DC 12** to part the crowd: _"City Guard business — make way!"_ (works once, then the crowd stops believing it).
- **Scrap:** He can simply walk forward. People move for a Warforged. No check. But they move in fear, not cooperation. Narrate: _"The crowd parts for you. Not because they trust you — because you are made of iron and you are walking toward them with purpose."_

**Reaching Varra — the confrontation:**

Varra is not a boss fight. She is a gang leader standing on a fountain in a riot. She has **2 Thugs** flanking her as bodyguards.

**Varra Kell:**

- Use **Bandit Captain** stat block (MM p.344). AC 15, HP 65. Multiattack (scimitar + dagger).
- She will fight if attacked but prefers to talk. She's winning the crowd — violence makes her look like a victim.

**Party options:**

- **Fight her:** The Thugs engage. Varra fights if pressed. If dropped below 20 HP, she surrenders loudly and dramatically: _"See? THIS is what the Crown does! Beats the people who speak truth!"_ This is a win mechanically but a loss politically — she becomes a martyr.
- **Intimidation DC 16:** Someone physically imposing (Queek, Scrap, Merios) can shut her down through sheer presence. She steps off the fountain with a sneer and disappears into the alleys. Not arrested, but the spectacle ends.
- **Persuasion DC 17:** Someone talks her down. This is hard — she's riding a wave. But if the argument is good (appealing to the fact that her own people in Smokestack Row are being hurt too, or that the Crownwatch _will_ come if she doesn't stop and they won't be gentle), she calculates the odds and withdraws. She's pragmatic.
- **Kyron's moment:** If Kyron is the one who confronts Varra, she looks at him — a tiefling, an outsider, someone the city fears — and laughs. _"You? You're defending THEM? They'd burn you before they'd burn me, horn-boy. You know it."_ This is cruel and it's partly true. How Kyron responds — silence, anger, words — is his moment. There is no right answer. The table sees him choose who he is.

**If the party neutralises Varra:** The Mawrats lose coordination. The remaining thugs scatter into the alleys. Phase 4 begins.

**If the party can't reach Varra or fails:** The Crownwatch descends from the Terrace — 8 heavily armoured soldiers who move through the crowd like a blade. They don't distinguish between Mawrats and civilians. Halna swears. The party can try to intercept and moderate the Crownwatch response, or they can let it happen. Either way, order is restored — but the cost is ugly.

---

### Phase 4 — Restoration

**What happens:** The riot is over. The plaza is emptying. Guards are tending to wounded. Shop owners are assessing damage. The fire in the Arcade has been put out (either by the party or by bucket chains organised by civilians). Varra is either arrested, fled, or being quietly treated as a folk hero depending on how Phase 3 went.

**The party helps the Guard.** This is the ideal resolution you wanted. Halna is organising triage and crowd management with too few people. The party can:

- Help carry wounded to the nearest healing hall (the Silverward's Sanctuary of the Gentle Hand is closest).
- Stand guard at the south exits to prevent looting.
- Assist Guards in identifying Mawrats who haven't fled — DC 12 Perception to pick them out of the crowd.
- Scrap can physically lift debris from a collapsed market stall that has trapped someone.
- Kyron can use minor magic to stabilise a wounded civilian — and this time, the people around him see him _healing_, not casting in anger. A woman who flinched from him earlier watches him save someone's child and doesn't flinch now. This is small but it matters.

**Halna's gratitude:** She is not effusive. She is a professional. But she nods at the party when the last wounded person is moved:

> _"You didn't have to stay. Most people with your kind of strength would have walked. I won't forget it."_

If the party asks about the Mawrats, she says:

> _"They're getting bolder. They used to steal purses. Now they're setting fires and beating my Guards. Something's changed in the Row. Something's feeding them."_

She doesn't know what. The party might suspect: the lattice destabilisation is creating the conditions the Mawrats exploit.

## Aftermath Scenes — Pick 1–2

### Veyne

Captain Veyne finds the party that evening. She is controlled, but the controlled kind that means she's furious underneath.

> _"I watched the speech from the Bastion wall. I watched Graves read words he didn't write. I watched the Castellan of Caer Hollow tell eight hundred people that their suffering is seasonal."_

She pauses.

> _"The Castellan read what he was given. The question is who wrote it."_

If the party shares what they know from the Silverward — the "infrastructure assessment activity" breadcrumb — Veyne's expression tightens. She does not comment directly but she says:

> _"I'm keeping a list. Things that don't add up. The list is getting long."_

She is building a case. She is not ready to share it yet. But the party is now on her list of people she trusts.

### The Tiefling Stranger (Kyron)

**Only if Option A or B was the disruption — no magical flare.** During the chaos, Kyron spotted an older tiefling woman in the crowd who was watching him with an unreadable expression. She didn't panic. She seemed to recognise something about him.

After the event, if Kyron goes looking for her: DC 15 Investigation in the Scholar's Descent turns up nothing. But a bookseller near the Arcanum mentions an older tiefling woman who visited his shop three days ago, asking about texts on sorcerous bloodlines and clockwork magic. She paid in foreign coin and didn't give a name.

This is a seed. It doesn't need to go anywhere yet.

### The Child's Parent (Scrap)

If Scrap protected the child during the riot, the parent finds the party later — at their lodging, at the Bastion, wherever they are. The parent is a baker from Eastveil. He is awkward. He doesn't know how to talk to a Warforged.

> _"I... my daughter. During the... you were there. She was — you kept her safe."_

He puts a wrapped package on the table. Bread. Fresh. Still warm.

> _"She keeps talking about you. She says you're 'the metal man who didn't run.' She wants to know if you eat."_

This is not a quest hook. It is a human being thanking Scrap for being a person. Let it land.

---

## Session End

The city is quiet. Guards patrol. The plaza is being cleaned. Somewhere in Smokestack Row, Varra Kell is either in a cell or telling her people that today proved her right.

The party sits with the knowledge that the Crown is failing, the Arcanum is lying, and the city's anger is now a physical force. Tomorrow or next session, Ivor returns and Lin has a job. But tonight, they sit with what they saw.

End on: _"The city is quiet. But it doesn't feel like peace."_
