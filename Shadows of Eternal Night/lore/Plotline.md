# Lore Overview – Shadows of Eternal Night

## BBEG: Nagash, Supreme Lord of the Undead

[[Nagash]], once a mortal priest of [[Nehekhara]], transcended death through forbidden sorcery to become the first and most powerful necromancer in existence. Now, as a god-like undead entity, his will persists through dark cults, cursed tomes, and ancient rituals. Though his physical form rarely moves across the world, his influence is ever-present, whispering into the minds of the dead and those desperate enough to court undeath.

## BBEG Goals and Motivations

[[Nagash]]'s ultimate goal is the **total convergence of the [[Shadowfell]] and the [[Material Plane]]**, reshaping reality into a world where **death is absolute and eternal**. This convergence would not merely destroy the living world—it would **overwrite it**, transforming every city, every forest, every soul into an extension of his dominion.

### Core Motivations:
- **Control of All Souls**: Nagash believes that all souls rightfully belong to him. In his vision, death is not an end, but a transfer of ownership. Life, by contrast, is a rebellion against his natural rule.

- **Hatred of the Divine**: Once a mortal who defied the gods, Nagash seeks to render divine beings powerless. By merging the planes, he aims to **sever the gods' influence** over the Material Plane. Prayers will go unheard. Miracles will fail. Only Nagash's will shall shape fate.

- **Ascension Through Convergence**: Nagash's form is already a fusion of godhood and undeath, but the convergence serves a **higher transformation**. It is said that by fusing the planes, Nagash will anchor his essence across all existence—**not just a ruler of death, but a fixed law of reality**.

- **A World Without Rebirth**: The convergence would permanently sever the cycle of reincarnation and resurrection. To Nagash, **true eternity lies not in returning to life, but in never escaping death**.

## Effect on Players and the World

The rise of Nagash is not a distant event—it is a spreading affliction. The world, and the players themselves, will feel his influence long before they ever stand before him. Every step they take is within a world slowly unravelling, succumbing to his design.

### World Effects

- **Dimmed Light**: Even outside the Shadowfell, light no longer shines with the same clarity. Torches feel cold. Dawn is slow to arrive and quick to vanish. In areas touched by convergence, the sun may vanish altogether, replaced by a pale, static twilight.
  
- **Weakened Divine Presence**: Gods of life and light struggle to reach their faithful in regions corrupted by Nagash. Prayers often go unanswered. Clerics and paladins may suffer diminished effectiveness unless they find sanctified or resistant ground.
  
- **Rise of the Dead**: Death is no longer a guarantee of peace. The newly dead may rise moments after falling, drawn to Nagash’s will. Entire battlefields become self-replenishing armies of the undead.
  
- **Magical Instability**: The arcane weave becomes tangled. Necromantic spells are stronger, while radiant and healing magic may falter or fail without warning. Resurrection becomes near-impossible without divine intervention or powerful relics.

### Player-Facing Effects

- **[[Shadow Corruption]]**: Prolonged exposure to the Shadowfell or undead-dominated regions begins to corrupt the player characters. This may manifest through:
  - Nightmares, hallucinations, or whispers from beyond.
  - Mechanical debuffs (e.g. temporary max HP reduction, exhaustion build-up).
  - Behavioral shifts—impulses of cruelty, apathy, or detachment from life.
  - Gradual spiritual detachment from their gods.

- **[[The Mark of the Veil]]**: Those who linger too long in areas near convergence may bear a subtle mark—a sigil, scar, or aura visible only to undead or seers. This mark can attract the attention of Nagash’s minions, or prevent peaceful interaction with holy factions.

- **[[Sanctuaries of Light]]**: Rare locations within the Shadowfell resist Nagash’s corruption—flickers of resistance that hold the old ways. These sites may:
  - Allow players to commune with their gods or recover from corruption.
  - Act as the only functional portals between planes.
  - Serve as meeting points for the Shadowfell’s last bastions of resistance.

- **Inevitable Tension**: Like the mind flayer parasite in Baldur’s Gate, players may begin to suspect that something inside them is changing. Whether corruption is visible, measurable, or purely narrative is up to the DM—but it should create urgency.

- **Cycle Interference**: Souls no longer pass peacefully. NPCs important to the players may be turned into enemies. Friends may be unreachable in the afterlife. The players themselves may be denied resurrection or face consequences for returning.

## Background Before the Players Enter

Nagash's resurgence did not begin with armies or rituals—it began with silence. The world has felt his return long before it understood the cause. The veil between the Material Plane and the Shadowfell has been thinning for decades, but only recently have the signs become undeniable.

### Historical Context

- **Whispers of Return**: Scattered records, hidden in forbidden libraries or whispered among old death cults, spoke of a being who once defied the gods, conquered death, and fell into ruin. That being—Nagash—was believed destroyed or lost to myth. Yet across centuries, fragments of his works, the **Nine Books**, endured. Their rediscovery has fueled a new age of necromancy.

- **The Growing Silence**: In the last two decades, certain temples have reported a severing of divine connection in remote regions. Death rites began to fail. Souls became restless. Some priests, desperate to restore peace, turned to forbidden methods—unwittingly furthering Nagash's influence.

- **Localized Corruption**: Entire villages fell silent overnight. Animals fled certain forests. Rivers turned black and still. At first, scholars blamed wild magic, natural disasters, or isolated cult activity. But those paying closer attention noticed the pattern—a creeping circle of decay emanating from forgotten places tied to the old world.

- **Divine Division**: The gods are not united. Some send visions and agents to oppose Nagash, while others remain silent—or worse, whisper doctrines that twist mercy into submission. A few divine entities may be corrupted, deceived, or strategically aligned with Nagash, believing the convergence to be an inevitable or even necessary act of cosmic renewal.

- **Shadowfell Resistance**: Not all denizens of the Shadowfell welcome Nagash. Within its depths, **hidden sanctuaries** survive—remnants of an ancient resistance, or mortal incursions into the plane that refused to collapse. These bastions of light may serve as rare safe havens for the players, offering divine connection, knowledge, and portals.

### Recent Events

- The campaign’s opening acts (see [[Prologue]] and [[Act 1]]) reflect the world reaching a **critical threshold**. Cults have become bolder. Undead walk in organized formations. [[The veil]] now tears in visible seams.
- Most nations are still unaware or in denial. Some rulers suspect the truth but suppress it, fearing panic or political instability.
- A final shift is approaching. If left unchecked, the convergence will enter a **point of no return**, after which the Shadowfell and Material Plane will begin to merge irreversibly.

## Minor BBEGs and Factions

- **The Cult of the Pale Hand** – Zealots who worship Nagash and seek to awaken his artifacts.
- **The Vampire Lord of Sylvania** – Seeks power through the Book of Blood but plots to betray Nagash once strong enough.
- **The Banshee Queen of Mourndusk Keep** – Twisted by grief, she seeks to expand Nagash’s silence across the land.
- **The Death Knight of Hollowreach** – Once a noble paladin, now the vanguard of Nagash's campaign in the Material Plane.
- **The Servants of Null** – Not direct allies of Nagash, but opportunists drawn to the chaos, seeking to bind the converged world to their dragon god of annihilation.

## Custom Rules and Campaign Variations

- **Radiant Nullification**: Radiant damage and divine magic are suppressed in Shadowfell-aligned regions. Disadvantage may apply, or effects are halved.
- **Servants of Nagash**: Fallen characters may return as undead under Nagash’s control unless specific rites are performed. Saving throws can reverse this state.
- **Shadowfell Rules**:
  - Hope is mechanically limited; long rests only recover one level of exhaustion.
  - Light sources are unreliable or provide reduced visibility.
  - Divine intervention and sending spells may fail or be intercepted.
- **Deathbound Resurrection**: Resurrection spells require rare reagents or divine permission, which may be denied in corrupted zones.
