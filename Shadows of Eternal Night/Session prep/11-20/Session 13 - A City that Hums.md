	## Overview

The party begins the session with no active quest, rested at the **Violet Cap** inn in Eastveil Commons. Caer Hollow feels different from when they left for Duskwatch — busier, more tense, the kind of city that is pretending harder than usual that everything is fine. The magical disturbances that have been quietly accumulating are starting to surface at street level.

This session is a free opener. It gives the players room to breathe, follow up on loose threads, and encounter the two quest hooks organically — one via the bounty board at the Eastveil Commons notice post, one via an NPC who approaches them directly. Once they pick a hook the session transitions into either **The Rat King's Ransom** or **The Silverward Dissonance**.

**Present this session:** Samson, Ivor, Queek, Merios **Absent:** Kyron, Scrap **Party Level:** 5 **Starting Location:** The Violet Cap, Eastveil Commons

---

## Opening — The Violet Cap, Morning

The Violet Cap is quiet at this hour. An elf-run establishment, it keeps itself a cut above the noise of the Brass Ladle — softer lighting, tea rather than ale at breakfast, a proprietor who has never once raised his voice. The four of them are the loudest thing in the room by default.

Read or paraphrase:

> _The morning comes in grey through the shuttered windows. Somewhere outside a crowd is already moving — voices, not yet angry, but pointed. The smell of fresh bread from the kitchen is doing its best against the mood. Your gold from Duskwatch sits a little heavier than it should. Two hundred pieces each for a ghost town and three corpses and a thing beneath a fountain that probably has a name in some book Samson hasn't found yet._

Let the players settle into the morning naturally. This is a good moment for character conversation — they have been through something strange together and the city has not acknowledged it at all.

---

## Loose Thread — The Crystal

If the players ask about the void shard or Samson brings it up, this is a natural moment to let that thread breathe without resolving it.

**What they currently know:**

- It appeared in Samson's bag without explanation at the city gate on their return.
- Detect Magic gives mixed, unstable results — no clean school of magic, nothing definitive.
- It feels cold in a way that suggests it is drawing warmth rather than lacking it.
- Reflections in its surface move slightly late.

**What they can do this session:**

- Samson could attempt to research it at the Arcanum — a DC 15 Arcana check reveals only that the shard is resistant to standard divination and that its composition does not match any known mineral or magical material in the Arcanum's catalogue. A faculty member who notices him researching it might ask pointed questions about where he found it.
- They could attempt to sell or appraise it — any reputable dealer will decline to touch it after a moment's examination and offer no explanation beyond discomfort.
- They could ignore it entirely, which is also fine.

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** Do not resolve the crystal this session. It is a Shadowfell Act 2 reveal. Let it sit uncomfortably. If players press hard, the most anyone can tell them is that the shard seems to actively resist being understood — which is itself an answer of a kind.

---

## The City Outside

When the party steps out of the Violet Cap, give them a brief impression of Caer Hollow's current state. Pick two or three of the following details to describe as they move through Eastveil Commons:

- A small cluster of people arguing outside the city notice board — not a protest, just civilians venting. Someone's enchanted lock failed overnight and their shop was robbed. They want to know who is responsible for the magical disruptions.
- A Crownwatch patrol passes — two soldiers, moving briskly, not stopping to chat the way they usually do.
- A street vendor selling minor charms and trinkets has a table of items that are all faintly misfiring — a warming stone that is cooling instead, a light charm flickering between colours it was not enchanted with. He is trying to sell them anyway at a discount and looking increasingly desperate.
- A City Guard pinning fresh notices to walls across the square — standard city administration, but he is moving fast and not making eye contact with anyone.

These details establish that something is wrong with the city's ambient magic without explaining why. Do not linger on them unless players engage.

---

## Hook 1 — The Bounty Board

The **Eastveil Commons notice post** is a wooden board beside the market square fountain, maintained by the City Guard for public postings — lost property, civic announcements, minor bounties. It is usually unremarkable.

If the players approach it, or if they pass through the square and a DC 11 Perception check catches something pinned at eye level:

> _Among the usual notices — a lost cat, a debt dispute, a request for a qualified seamstress — one posting stands out for its handwriting. Urgent. Cramped. The kind of writing produced by someone who does not normally ask for help._

The notice reads:

---

_URGENT — PEST PROBLEM AND STRUCTURAL DAMAGE — SMOKESTACK ROW_ _Buildings collapsing. Rats unlike anything you've seen. Tenants displaced and furious._ _This is beyond what I can handle myself._ _Paying 60 gold upfront, 60 gold when the problem is solved._ _Ask for CORVIN RAST at the Sooty Flagon, Smokestack Row._ _No questions. Fast work. Fast coin._

---

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** If the players take this hook, transition to **[[Session – The Rat King's Ransom]]**. Corvin is at the Sooty Flagon waiting, increasingly agitated.

---

## Hook 2 — Maret Soln Approaches

Another posting stands out for its topic, not related to all others:

_ARCANE CONSULTATION SOUGHT — SILVERWARD DISTRICT_ _Herbalist and reagent supplier seeking someone with practical arcane knowledge._ _Stock dying. Neighbour injured. Problem is getting worse._ _Not looking for Arcanum bureaucracy. Looking for someone who actually solves things._ _Ask for MARET SOLN at the Verdant Cloister supply entrance, Silverward._ _Reasonable coin for reasonable work. Do not send students._

At some point during the morning — whether the party is at the notice board, eating breakfast at the Violet Cap, or wandering the market — a woman in herbalist robes finds them.

She has been asking around. Cloister contacts, market vendors, anyone who might know where the group that handled the Duskwatch business is staying. She is not frantic but she is close to it, and she hides it poorly under clipped, precise speech.

She introduces herself as **Maret Soln**, supplier to the Verdant Cloister and several Arcanum clients. She gets to the point quickly:

> _"I've heard you deal with unusual problems. I have one. My stock is dying — not rotting, not pests, dying. Enchanted reagents discharging on their own. My neighbour lost three fingers last week when a stored ward-stone exploded. Whatever is causing it is getting worse every day and I need someone to find it and stop it before I lose everything I've spent twelve years building."_

She offers 80 gold for a resolution, plus rare reagents worth approximately 40 gold if the job is done cleanly.

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** If the players take this hook, transition to **[[Session – The Silverward Dissonance]]**. Maret will walk them toward the Arcanum to collect the restoration scrolls as a first step.

---

## If Players Take Neither Hook Immediately

Some parties will want to spend time in the city first — shopping, visiting NPCs, following up on other threads. This is fine. The hooks do not expire.

**Corvin Rast** will still be at the Sooty Flagon. His patience is not infinite but he will wait another day before finding someone else.

**Maret Soln** will try to find them again the following morning if they do not respond. She is not proud enough to let her shop die because they were busy.

If the players go somewhere specific, use the city atmosphere details above to continue seeding the sense of low-level magical wrongness without forcing a hook.

---

## Optional — Veyne Contact

If players seek out Captain Veyne during this free time, she is available at the Crownwatch Bastion. She has little new information to offer but can confirm:

- Magical disturbance reports have increased across three districts since the party left for Duskwatch.
- The Arcanum has attributed this to seasonal ley fluctuation. Veyne does not believe them but has no counter-evidence yet.
- She is keeping a list. She does not say of what.

This is a brief scene — Veyne is not offering a quest here, just existing as a concerned presence who is paying attention. It reinforces that the city's problems are not imaginary and that at least one person in authority is watching.

---

## Transition

Once the players commit to a hook, move directly into the relevant session note. There is no hard pacing target for this opener — let it run as long as it needs to before the players have something to chase.

---

## Session End State Goals

By the end of Session 13 the party should have:

- Encountered the city's ambient magical instability as a lived experience rather than reported information.
- Chosen and begun one of the two available side adventures.
- Ideally completed that adventure, ending on its resolution and its information reward.

If the adventure runs long, end at a natural stopping point within it and complete it at the start of Session 14.
