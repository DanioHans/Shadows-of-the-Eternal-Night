Acting Innkeeper of _Gull’s Rest_

_Human woman, early 20s, tired hands, steady posture_

**Role:** Emotional consequence, grounded grief  
**Tone:** Polite, restrained, quietly broken

### Background

Mira has worked the inn since childhood.  
Her father ran Gull’s Rest alone after her mother died.

During the dream:

- He drowned in the cellar after flooding began
    
- No one remembers seeing him go down
    
- His body was found only after the curse lifted
    

Mira **does not rage**.  
She has cried all she can.

---

### How She Treats the Party

- Calm
    
- Distant but civil
    
- Thanks them — _genuinely_
    

If the party brings up responsibility:

> “If it wasn’t you, it would have been something else.  
> That’s what dreams do. They make accidents.”

She does not forgive.  
She does not accuse.

This makes her presence _uncomfortable_ in the best way.

---

### Long-Term Use

- She may remember the party years later
    
- She may hesitate before offering aid
    
- She becomes a reminder that victory has cost
