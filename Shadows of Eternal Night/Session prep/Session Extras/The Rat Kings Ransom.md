## Overview

A slumlord in **Smokestack Row** posts a bounty after a series of structural collapses and a rat infestation drives his tenants out. What begins as a pest control job leads the party into the sewers beneath the district, where they discover something far more troubling than vermin — a deliberate arcane construction buried deep in the city's foundations.

**Party Level:** 5  
**Estimated Duration:** 2–3 hours  
**Location:** Smokestack Row → Caer Hollow Sewers  
**Quest Type:** Bounty Board

---

## Hook

A crudely written notice is pinned to the bounty board at the **Crownwatch Bastion** or the **Eastveil Commons notice post**:

> _PEST REMOVAL & STRUCTURAL ASSESSMENT — SMOKESTACK ROW_ _Buildings collapsing. Rats like you've never seen. Tenants rioting._ _Paying well. Ask for CORVIN RAST at the Sooty Flagon, Smokestack Row._ _No questions asked. Fast coin for fast work._

---

## The Client – Corvin Rast

A heavyset, sweat-prone landlord who owns four tenement buildings clustered around a sewer junction in Smokestack Row. He is not a good man — his buildings were already barely habitable — but he is genuinely panicked. He's losing rent money, facing displaced and furious tenants, and one of his buildings partially collapsed overnight, killing nobody but destroying the ground floor entirely.

**Personality:** Loud, impatient, dismissive of anyone he thinks is beneath him. Softens quickly when scared. Responds well to competence and badly to questions about his property standards.

**What he tells the party:**

- Three weeks ago the rats started coming up from the sewers in unusual numbers.
- Two weeks ago tenants began complaining of a low hum through the floorboards at night.
- One week ago walls started cracking — not from age, from below, like something is pressing upward through the foundations.
- Four days ago the northeast corner of Building 3 collapsed entirely. No casualties.
- His tenants are now camped in the street and threatening to go to the City Guard.

**What he wants:** Someone to go into the sewers, find what's causing the problem, and stop it. He doesn't care how.

**Reward:** 60 gold up front, 60 gold on resolution. If the party asks for more, he haggles to 50/80. He has the coin, he just hates spending it.

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** Corvin knows nothing about arcane structures or Verrath. He is a pure civilian. Do not let him accidentally give away more than he should — his ignorance is part of the point.

---

## The Tenants – Street Camp Outside Building 3

Before going into the sewers, the party may speak with displaced tenants camped outside. This is optional but rewards good roleplayers.

**Useful information tenants can provide:**

- The hum started suddenly, not gradually. "One night it wasn't there. The next it was."
- Rats began appearing before the hum — first normal sized, then wrong.
- A child mentions seeing a rat "with light coming out of it" near the collapsed wall. She is not believed by adults.
- An older tenant says the smell from the sewers changed a few weeks ago. "Used to smell like rot. Now it smells like lightning."

**DC 13 Insight:** The tenants are genuinely frightened, not just angry. Whatever is happening beneath them feels wrong in a way they can't articulate.

---

## The Sewers – Overview

The party enters through a maintenance hatch in the alley behind Building 3. The sewers beneath Smokestack Row are old, narrow, and low-ceilinged — tight enough that Merios will need to fold her wings.

**General atmosphere:**

- Warmer than it should be.
- A faint electrical smell, like air before a storm.
- Occasional flickers of dim purple-white light from deeper in the tunnels, too faint to illuminate.
- The hum is audible immediately — low, constant, felt in the chest more than heard.

**Navigation:** Three tunnel branches lead from the entry hatch. The rats have left clear trails — claw marks, droppings, shed fur — all converging toward the eastern branch. A DC 11 Survival or Investigation check confirms this.

---

## Encounter 1 – The Mutated Rats

Halfway down the eastern branch the party encounters the first wave of mutated rats. These are not normal vermin.

**Appearance:** Larger than expected, patchy fur that seems to shift between solid and translucent. Their eyes emit a faint purple glow. They move in unsettling coordination, as if sharing a single awareness.

**Behaviour:** They do not flee. They hold ground and attack as a group, retreating only when more than half are dead.

**Combat:** Use [[Giant Rat]] stat blocks with the following modifications:

- HP increased to 10 each
- Bite deals 1d4+1 piercing + 1d4 force damage (arcane exposure)
- On a hit, target must make DC 11 Constitution save or suffer minor magical feedback — disadvantage on concentration checks until end of next turn

**Wave 1:** 6 mutated rats  
**Wave 2 (triggered if party pursues):** 4 mutated rats + 1 Rat Brute (use [[Giant Weasel]] stat block, reskinned, HP 25)

**Post-combat:** The rats that flee all move in the same direction — deeper east toward the heat source. A DC 12 Arcana check confirms the arcane energy in their bodies is not natural mutation. Something has been irradiating them from a fixed source over a sustained period.

> 💡 **Queek Note:** Queek may have complicated feelings about killing rats. Play this straight — these are not Skaven, they are animals. But if Queek's player leans into it, let them have the moment. It can be genuinely funny or genuinely sad depending on how they play it.

## The Node Chamber

Following the rats leads the party to a section of sewer that should not exist.

The tunnel opens into a wider chamber — clearly not part of the original sewer infrastructure. The walls have been reinforced with fitted stone and metal bracketing etched with geometric runes. At the centre of the chamber, embedded in the floor and rising to chest height, is a **crystalline node**: roughly cylindrical, dark purple-black in colour, humming at a frequency that makes teeth ache. Thin lines of carved stone radiate outward from its base like roots, disappearing into the walls in multiple directions.

**First impressions:**

- The chamber is noticeably warmer than the tunnels.
- The purple light is coming from this — not bright, but constant.
- The remaining mutated rats are clustered at the edges of the chamber, not approaching the node but unwilling to leave it. They do not attack unless provoked.
- The rune-etched bracketing on the walls is fresh. This was built recently.

**Investigation checks:**

|Check|DC|Result|
|---|---|---|
|Arcana|12|The node is drawing ambient ley energy upward through the foundations. The cracked buildings above are a side effect of how aggressively it pulls.|
|Arcana|16|The radial channels in the walls connect this node to others — this is not a standalone construction. It is part of a network.|
|Investigation|13|The construction is expert work. The runes are not decorative — they are functional, architectural. Whoever built this understood what they were doing at a high level.|
|Investigation|17|The node is oriented — the channels don't radiate randomly, they flow in a deliberate direction. Toward the Scholar's Descent district.|
|History|14|The style of the rune-work matches Arcanum architectural notation — the kind used in official city infrastructure projects, not wild or cult magic.|

**Samson specifically:** If Samson examines the node, he recognises fragments of the notation from advanced Arcanum coursework — specifically from a theoretical paper on ley amplification he once read as supplementary material. The author of that paper was a senior faculty member. He does not remember the name without a DC 15 History check, at which point he recalls it was **Verrath**.

## The Problem — Tampering With the Node

The party cannot simply destroy the node. It is heavily warded and tampering triggers immediate, painful arcane feedback.

**If a player attempts to physically strike or damage the node:**

- They must make a DC 14 Constitution saving throw.
- On a failure: 2d10 force damage and are thrown back 10 feet, knocked prone.
- On a success: 1d10 force damage and a clear message that direct force is not the answer.

**If Samson or another arcane caster attempts to dispel or counter the node:**

- The ward absorbs the spell entirely, and the caster takes 1d6 psychic damage as feedback.
- DC 16 Arcana confirms the ward is too sophisticated to brute-force dispel at their current level.

**The actual solution:** The nodes are drawing power through the foundations. They cannot be disabled from here — but the structural damage to the buildings above can be stabilised by disrupting the _flow channels_ in the walls, not the node itself. These are less heavily warded. Breaking or blocking them reduces the upward pull enough to stop further collapse.

This requires physically destroying the carved channels in the chamber walls — not magically, just with tools or weapons. It takes time and effort (describe it as hard physical work in a cramped space), but it is achievable. Each channel takes roughly two rounds of sustained effort to break.

**There are five channels.** The party needs to break at least three to meaningfully reduce the pull. Breaking all five stops the collapses entirely but causes the node to emit a sharp tone and pulse of light — it registers the interference. Someone is watching these nodes.

---

## Returning to Corvin

With the channels broken and the rats no longer irradiated by a stable source, the remaining mutated rats will disperse into the broader sewer network over the coming days. The hum will not stop entirely — the node is still active — but the structural damage will cease.

**Corvin's reaction:** Relieved, then immediately looking for a way to pay less. If the party presents any physical evidence of the constructed chamber (rubble from the channels, a sketch of the runes, description of the node) he waves it away — not his problem, not his sewers, he just wants his buildings back.

**If the party pushes Corvin for more information:**

- DC 12 Persuasion: He admits he got a visit two months ago from an official-looking man who surveyed his buildings and said there might be "infrastructure maintenance" happening beneath the district. He was given a small payment and told not to worry about any unusual sounds.
- DC 16 Persuasion or Intimidation: He produces the payment — a single stamped chit from the **Royal Arcanum Infrastructure Office**, signed with an indecipherable initial. The chit is dated six weeks ago.

|Accomplishment|Result|

|Broke 3+ flow channels|Structural collapses cease. Node still active but disrupted.|

## What the Players Learn

This should feel like a side quest that turned up something unexpected, not a plot revelation. The players have found evidence of deliberate arcane construction beneath the city tied to Arcanum notation. They cannot yet connect it to Verrath unless Samson makes his History check. Even then it is a memory, not proof.

The important seeds planted:

- Someone is building something beneath Caer Hollow deliberately.
- It is connected to the Arcanum at an institutional level.
- It is a network, not a single structure.
- It is causing real harm to real people who have no idea why.
