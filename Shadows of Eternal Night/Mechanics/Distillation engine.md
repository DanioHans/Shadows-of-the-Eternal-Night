
Excerpted from the private journal of Arcanist Pelion Varr, former researcher of the Royal Arcanum.
## Purpose of the Engine

The **Arcane Distillation Engine** was designed to concentrate faint ambient magical energy from the surrounding environment into a usable form.

Rather than relying on direct spellcasting or rare reagents, the engine draws from the **residual arcane field present in any populated magical city**.

According to Varr’s notes:

> “Magic is never truly spent. Every spell, enchantment, or ritual leaves a trace in the air and stone. The engine merely gathers what others discard.”

The device was intended to allow alchemists to produce **highly potent reagents** without requiring powerful spellcasters.

---

## Basic Function

The system operates in three stages:

1. **Collection**  
    Copper conduits and etched runes draw ambient magical resonance into the chamber.
    
2. **Condensation**  
    The **core crystal** compresses this energy, forcing it into a stable arcane state.
    
3. **Regulation**  
    A containment circle prevents the energy from feeding back into itself and creating unstable surges.
    

When functioning properly, the engine produces small droplets of **arcane condensate**, an extremely concentrated magical reagent.

---

## Limitations

Varr notes several critical constraints:

- Ambient magical energy must remain **low and steady**
    
- Sudden magical surges can destabilize the system
    
- Excess power can cause **resonance feedback**
    

One entry warns:

> “If the chamber begins drawing more energy than the containment circle can regulate, the system may enter a harmonic feedback loop.”

Such a loop would cause the engine to:

- draw increasing amounts of magical energy
    
- amplify nearby enchantments
    
- produce uncontrolled magical reactions
    

---

## Shutdown Procedure (Incomplete)

Several notes reference emergency shutdown methods:

- disabling the **power conduits** to reduce energy intake
    
- disrupting the **containment circle**
    
- damaging or stabilizing the **core crystal**
    

However, Varr remarks that:

> “In a true resonance cascade, conventional shutdown may prove impossible. Erratic actions could cause magical anomalies or even explosions, proceed with caution”

---

## Final Entry

The last readable entry reads:

> “The design works… but only barely. Even minor fluctuations in the arcane field make the apparatus unpredictable.  
> I will recommend the Arcanum suspend the project before someone attempts to scale the design.”

No further entries are recorded.
