The Ritual of Dusk Crossing is a forbidden rite contained within the [[Book of Eternity]].  
It allows mortal practitioners to open a temporary portal between the Material Plane and the Shadowfell — bypassing the need for high-level planar magic, but at terrible cost.

The ritual is delicate, exhausting, and invites corruption upon both the caster and the land itself.

---

## Purpose

- Open a **one-way portal** between the Material Plane and the Shadowfell (or vice versa).
- Portal is temporary (6 hours) and unstable beyond that timeframe.
- Allows physical passage for living and dead alike.

---

## Components Required

- **Ash of a Willing Soul**:  
  - Ash gathered from a relic or heirloom of a deceased individual who willingly gave their life to a cause.  
  - Not a fresh sacrifice; no need to kill anyone.
  
- **Reflective Anchor**:  
  - A basin of still water, a polished mirror, or a lake surface — must remain perfectly undisturbed during the ritual.

- **Circle of Shadow Sigils**:  
  - Drawn in ground using blackened chalk, ash, or blood-mixed ink.
  - Requires 30 minutes of precise preparation (DC 15 Arcana check to draw correctly).

- **Rite of Sundering (Chant)**:  
  - An invocation in the ancient tongue of shadow beings (requires deciphered Book sections).

---

## Casting the Ritual

**Time Required**: 3 uninterrupted hours.

**Skill Checks**:
- DC 17 Arcana or Religion check at the end of the casting period.
- Assistance allowed (1 helper = +2 bonus, up to +4 maximum).

**Failure Consequences**:
- Portal opens incorrectly — transports the ritualists into a random corrupted section of the Shadowfell.
- Burst of shadow energy (6d10 necrotic damage to all within 30 feet, DC 16 Constitution save halves).

---

## Effects of a Successful Ritual

- **Portal**: A shimmering obsidian mirror forms vertically at the ritual site.
- **Duration**: Remains stable for 6 hours.
- **Size**: 15-foot diameter, enough for large groups.
- **One-Time Use**: Once closed naturally or forced shut, the same portal cannot be reopened without a new ritual.

---

## World Effects

- **Shadow Scar**:  
  - The ground surrounding the ritual site (30–40 feet radius) becomes visibly tainted:
    - Plants die.
    - Light is dimmed unnaturally.
    - Animals avoid the area.
  - Scar slowly heals over months unless purified magically.

- **Tether**:  
  - For as long as the portal remains open, planar energies bleed into the nearby environment.
  - Entities from the Shadowfell may sense the opening (DM discretion for encounters).

---

## Dangers and Warnings

- **Ritual cannot be interrupted**: If interrupted, the backlash becomes violent and unpredictable.
- **Ambient Corruption**: Prolonged time near an active portal raises corruption accumulation rates.
- **Scrying Risk**: Opening a portal makes it easier for beings of the Shadowfell — including agents of Nagash — to locate the ritualists temporarily.

---

## Optional Enhancements (Advanced Study)

- **Amplified Portal**: With more components (e.g., soul-bound artifacts), casters can create a two-way stable portal for 24 hours (requires extended research and higher risks).
- **Anchor to Specific Location**: Rare rituals allow pinpointing a destination instead of random Shadowfell entry points.
