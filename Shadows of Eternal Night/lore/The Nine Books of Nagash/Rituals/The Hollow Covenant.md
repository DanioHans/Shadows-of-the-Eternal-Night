The Hollow Covenant is a forbidden rite contained within the [[Book of Eternity]].  
It allows a living creature to sever their divine connection — willingly or unwillingly — forsaking the gods and reshaping their soul into something half-forgotten.

The transformation is irreversible unless redeemed through equally powerful magic or immense penance.

---

## Purpose

- Sever a character’s bond to their deity or divine patron.
- Prevent divine magic from affecting them positively (e.g., no divine healing, no resurrection).
- Reforge the soul’s foundation into something shadow-bound but self-powered.

---

## Components Required

- **Relic of the Forsaken**:  
  - A religious symbol or artifact meaningful to the target’s faith.

- **Chains of Silver and Ash**:  
  - Two chains forged under a new moon, infused with shadow-ash.

- **Chamber of Echoes** (optional but beneficial):  
  - Performing the ritual in a desecrated temple grants advantage on ritual casting.

---

## Casting the Ritual

**Time Required**: 1 uninterrupted hour.

**Skill Checks**:
- DC 18 Religion or Arcana check.
- Assistance allowed if all participants are willing.

**Failure Consequences**:
- 4d10 necrotic damage backlash.
- Fragmented severance — partial loss of divine aid but no resistance to radiant damage.

---

## Effects of a Successful Ritual

- **Eclipsed Status**:
  - Cannot benefit from divine healing or resurrection.
  - Gains advantage on death saving throws.
  - Resistance to radiant damage.
  - Loses access to divine features (Channel Divinity, Divine Intervention) unless adapted.
  
- **Soul Resonance**:
  - Shadow entities sense the Eclipsed within 300 feet but hesitate to attack immediately.
  - Undead creatures may hesitate, observe, or allow the Eclipsed to retreat if they pose no immediate threat.

- **Alignment-Based Side Effects**:
  - **Good-aligned**:  
    - Occasional hallucinations of pleading spirits.
    - Disadvantage on Insight checks against divine beings.
  - **Neutral-aligned**:  
    - Minor shadow bleeding — faint mist trails in low light.
  - **Evil-aligned**:  
    - Hunger for dominance subtly increases; minor ego-boosting whispers.

---

## Redemption Path

- **Available during Act II**:
  - Characters who deeply regret the Hollow Covenant may seek rare, hidden rites of purification.
  - Redemption requires extreme sacrifice (e.g., permanently giving up powers, sacred quests, or unique relics).

> After crossing further into shadow (beyond Act II), redemption becomes near-impossible.

---

## Divine Repercussions

- **Deity Reaction**:
  - Good aligned deities consider this betrayal.
  - Death, Fate, or Shadow-aligned deities may attempt to forge new dark pacts.

- **World Reaction**:
  - Holy ground resists them.
  - Sanctuary spells and divine blessings fail to protect them.
  - Commoners may instinctively distrust or fear them.

---

## Defensive Measures (If Tampered)

- Animated Chains strike out (animated object stats).
- Soul Rift backlash: one creature loses spellcasting ability for 24 hours (DC 18 Wisdom save negates).
