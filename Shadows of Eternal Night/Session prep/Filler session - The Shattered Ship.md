# The Shattered Ship (Dream Filler)

A lucid dream one-shot where the party awakens aboard a surreal vessel at sea. Combines absurd callbacks to past sessions with dreamlike combat and symbolic choices. Serves as a filler episode when not all players are present.  

---

## Act 1: Awakening – The Frozen Farce
The party awakens on the deck of a massive galleon adrift on a black ocean under a starless sky. All is frozen: sea flat, crew statuesque, sails stiff.  

On deck are three frozen tableaux of absurd campaign memories, lined up like stage sets. The frozen crew point toward them. A whisper fills the air:  
*"The play must go on. Perform… or drown in silence."*  

### Scene 1: The Basilisk in the Market
- Tableau: A massive basilisk statue snarling, townsfolk mannequins wield sausages and spoons.  
- Whispers: *"You freed it… tame it now."*  
- Correct Performance: Over-the-top attempts to tame or befriend (petting, bribing with sausages, singing, riding).  
- Wrong Performance: Attacking or treating it seriously → statue cracks, psychic backlash.  

### Scene 2: Queek for Sale
- Tableau: Queek in a cage with a "FOR SALE" sign, mannequins with coin-purse heads as buyers.  
- Whispers: *"What’s he worth? Sell, or be sold."*  
- Correct Performance: Ridiculous trade (shadows, sausages, memories).  
- Wrong Performance: Taking it seriously or refusing → mannequins split, psychic backlash.  

### Scene 3: Jaspin and the Sausage
- Tableau: Jaspin mid-choke on an impossibly oversized sausage.  
- Whispers: *"Will you save him… or feed him more? Bigger, louder, funnier."*  
- Correct Performance: Exaggerated mockery/choking scene, feed him more until he bursts into glitter.  
- Wrong Performance: Sincerely trying to heal him → sausage writhes like a serpent, psychic backlash.  

**Resolution:** Each correct scene dissolves into mirror-dust that fuels the sails. Once all are completed, the ship shudders to life and begins to move. Failures cause cracks in the hull (foreshadow flooding).  

---

## Act 2: The Dream Kraken
From the depths rises a nightmare: a colossal squid-like beast, head and tentacles breaching the water. Its eyes glow with void-runes.  

- Combat encounter against a true nightmare monster.  
- Tentacles grab, crush, and drag PCs; the head surfaces and screams psychic waves.  
- Dream mechanics: PCs may manifest powers (Wisdom checks) but with twisted outcomes.  
- When defeated: the Kraken collapses into black water, but the ship begins to sink.  

See: [[Chain-Kraken]]  

---

## Act 3: Flooding and the Mutiny
The ship floods rapidly, water rushing up through the cracks. Crew panic, then accuse: *"You brought the water with you!"*  

- The vessel sinks into the black ocean.  
- The mutiny occurs underwater: mirror-crew attack as shadow-doubles while the party risks drowning.  
- PCs must use dream logic to survive: breathing through strange objects, reshaping the water, etc.  
- Combat resolves when the crew are defeated or the ship breaks apart completely.  

See: [[Mutiny Crew]]  

---

## Act 4: The Choice
The sinking ship is drawn toward a colossal **Void Rune of Light** on the horizon.  

The party has three options:  
1. **Steer the ship** – wrest control of the wheel. Ship avoids the rune, dream collapses → they awaken safely, but without visions.  
2. **Do nothing** – let the ship sink beneath them. They drown in dream and awaken safely, but shaken.  
3. **Embrace the water** – willingly jump into the black sea. Each PC receives a vision of possible future events (burning Caer Hollow, Blackening, Verrath, Nagash).  

The latter feels like the "better" ending, but all choices allow survival.  

See: [[Future Vision Table]]  

---

## Epilogue
The party awakens back at their camp. No time has passed, but one or more details linger: damp clothing, salt taste, fragments of glass in their belongings.  

---

## Notes
- **Tone:** Balance absurd comedy in Act 1 with dream-horror in later acts.  
- **Player Guidance:** Whispers provide clues; exaggeration and performance are always rewarded.  
- **Fail Forward:** Wrong choices always progress story, but with psychic backlash.  
- **Dream Powers:** Optional mechanic to encourage creativity; success works with a twist, failure causes backlash.  

---
# Dream Mechanics (Combat)

- **Dream Powers:**  
  Once per round, a PC may attempt to manifest a dream effect as a bonus action (Wisdom check DC 12–18 depending on scope).  
  - Success: Works but twisted (e.g., conjure fireball → explodes but leaves burning shadow on caster).  
  - Failure: Psychic backlash (1d6–3d6) and disturbing imagery.  

- **Mechanics Framework:**  
  Bosses telegraph dream mechanics (chants, runes, visible distortions).  
  Players have 1 round to counteract (silence, disruption, escape, etc.).  
  If not stopped, the effect resolves at the start of the next round.  

- **Breathing Underwater:**  
  In Act 3, PCs may describe how they survive (imagine gills, breathe shadows, drink water like air). Creative reasoning is rewarded with a simple Wis check (DC 12). Otherwise, standard suffocation rules apply.  

- **Fail Forward:**  
  Failure escalates tension (damage, paranoia, ship cracking) but never halts progress.
