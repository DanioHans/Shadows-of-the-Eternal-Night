The Hourglass Reversal is one of the most perilous and forbidden rites contained within the [[Book of Eternity]].  
It was designed by Nagash during his earliest ascension attempts, long before he became the Supreme Lord of Undeath.

The ritual seeks to "reverse" the natural march toward death — delaying its inevitability — by stealing vitality and timeline anchors from others.

Performing this ritual promises hollow immortality at terrible, world-shattering cost.

---

## Purpose

- Sever the caster’s fate from natural death.
- Tie their existence to stolen lifeforces.
- Achieve "immortality" — without true life, without true death.

---

## Components Required

- **The Soul Trinity**:
  - Three willing sacrifices:
    - **Innocence** (e.g., a child or equivalent pure soul).
    - **Valor** (e.g., a hero, knight, or champion).
    - **Faith** (e.g., a priest, oracle, or deeply religious figure).
  - Souls must be offered willingly or through magical compulsion (extreme corruption required if unwilling).

- **The Hourglass Vessel**:
  - A specially crafted artifact: an obsidian hourglass filled with sand.

- **The Blood Seal**:
  - Blood from a being of ancient or noble lineage (e.g., demigod, ancient king, celestial descent).

- **The Chamber of Sundering**:
  - A ritual space beyond hallowed grounds — usually desecrated temples, ancient battlefields, or deep Shadowfell nodes.

---

## Casting the Ritual

**Duration**: 3 nights under full moons.

**Procedure**:
- Night 1: Invocation of Names — inscribing the names of the soul donors around the Hourglass Vessel.
- Night 2: Binding of Wills — binding the soul threads into the caster’s shadow.
- Night 3: Shattering of Time — completing the transference, tearing the caster from the Weave of mortal fate.

Each night requires:
- 2 uninterrupted hours of ritual.
- DC 20 Arcana or Religion checks (one per night).

**Failure on any night**:
- Immediate backlash: caster suffers psychic devastation (8d12 psychic damage).
- Summons shadow-entities hungry for the broken soul.
- Ritual fails entirely, leaving the caster cursed: accelerated aging and inability to benefit from healing magic.

---

## Effects of a Successful Ritual

- **Immortality Achieved**:
  - Caster no longer ages naturally.
  - Immune to disease, poison, magical aging.
  - Immune to effects that forcibly kill through age or timeline manipulation.
  
- **The Hollowed Curse**:
  - Emotional decay sets in:
    - Slowly lose capacity for joy, love, or ambition over months.
    - Become colder, more detached, eventually forgetting personal ties.
  
- **Shadow Magnetism**:
  - Shadow creatures, undead, and entities of the Void are drawn toward the immortal.
  - Negative energy fields surround the immortal, decaying nearby life over decades.

- **Planar Resonance**:
  - Immortal becomes a living beacon tied partially to the Shadowfell.
  - Divine detection spells automatically sense them.
  - Gods of death and shadow know their existence.

---

## Long-Term Consequences

- **Corruption Clock**:
  - Over years (or accelerated through corruption mechanics), the immortal slowly transforms into a shadow-anchored being.
  - Without intervention (e.g., finding another anchor or divine reclamation), the immortal eventually:
    - Loses all remaining empathy.
    - Becomes an extension of Nagash’s influence.
    - May unknowingly act as a **Shadow Beacon**, advancing Nagash’s veil-fall agenda.

- **World Impact**:
  - Regions where the immortal resides too long begin to suffer:
    - Crops fail.
    - Wildlife withers.
    - Spirits become restless.
    - Holy ground blackens subtly.

---

## Defensive Measures (If Tampered)

- Hourglass Vessel violently explodes, dealing:
  - 10d10 force damage in a 30-foot radius (DC 20 Dexterity save for half).
- Caster or nearest creature cursed with **Aging Curse** (age 10d10 years instantly).

---

## Redemption or Reversal?

- **Before Final Collapse**:
  - Rare, impossible rituals may exist to sever the Hourglass Curse (future questline if desired).
  - Redemption demands cosmic-scale sacrifice (e.g., giving up the stolen lives permanently, soul-shattering purifications).

- **After Collapse**:
  - The immortal is lost. They become a unique undead tied directly to the Shadowfell.
