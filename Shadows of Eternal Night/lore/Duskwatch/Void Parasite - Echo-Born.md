# The Echo-Born Void Parasite
*Also known as: The Somnal Echo, the Hollow Seed, the Dream-Eater, the Black Reflection*

> “It mimics what it touches.  
> It grows in what it consumes.  
> It dreams in the shape of its prey.”

The Echo-Born Void Parasite is a **low-tier Void entity** originating from the fringes of the [[Shadowfell]].  
It is not sentient in any humanoid sense. It behaves like a **psychic parasitic organism**, feeding on memory, fear, and dream-imprint.

When left undisturbed, it remains dormant.  
When stimulated, it *reflects* its surroundings through a warped, dreamlike lens.

When corrupted by Void magic, it can become a formidable threat.

---

## 🜁 Taxonomy & Nature
### **Classification:**  
- **Voidborn Parasitic Construct**  
- *Fell Echo-Class Entity* (Arcanum categorization)  
- **Stage-3 Echo Bloom** (when actively manifesting a Nightmare Realm)

### **Origin:**  
Fragments of Void magic that **embed themselves into living consciousness**, forming a small nucleus of psychic mass.

They exist primarily in:
- dreams,  
- half-real projections,  
- reflection layers of reality.

Their physical bodies are weak and underdeveloped; the **real threat** is their *projection*.

---

## 🜁 Anatomy (Material Form)
In its true physical form, the Echo-Born parasite is:
- no larger than a rat,  
- semi-translucent,  
- pulsing with black and violet veins,  
- visibly struggling to maintain cohesion.

It cannot survive long outside a host or anchor.

**Physical Weakness:**  
AC 8–10, HP < 15, incapable of direct physical harm.

**Projection Strength:**  
Potentially catastrophic when fully anchored.

---

## 🜁 The Three Stages of an Echo-Born Parasite

### **Stage 1 — Dormant Echo**
Latent, harmless.  
Feeds on ambient emotion and dreams.

**Signs:**  
- minor feelings of déjà vu  
- recurring dreams  
- subtle reflection distortions  

Harmless unless provoked.

---

### **Stage 2 — Anchored Echo**
Occurs when the parasite:
- lodges itself in a dreaming mind, **and/or**
- binds itself to a ritual circle or arcane construct

This is what occurred in [[Duskwatch]].

**Effects:**  
- local sensory distortions  
- memory echoes  
- dream-thrall symptoms  
- villagers “looping” behaviour

It begins forming a **micro-projection layer** — the earliest form of a Nightmare Realm.

---

### **Stage 3 — Echo Bloom (“Dreamer-State”)**
When Void magic strengthens its reflection layer, the parasite enters Echo Bloom.

It manifests:
- a mirrored version of an area (Nightmare Realm)  
- shadow-doubles  
- corrupted sleepwalkers  
- dissociated reflections  
- psychic pressure on nearby minds  

This is the form known as **The Hollow Dreamer** in Duskwatch.

---

## 🜁 How an Echo-Born Parasite Consumes Magic
Void magic does not create —  
it **consumes, reflects, and replaces existing magical structure**.

Thus the parasite can “imitate” multiple schools:
- **Enchantment** → thrall effects, looping behaviour  
- **Illusion** → projection of altered surroundings  
- **Necromancy** → black ichor, memory drain  
- **Abjuration** → runic anchors and barriers  

This explains why Detect Magic yields **mixed, contradictory results**.

---

## 🜁 Why Duskwatch Collapsed
The Duskwatch parasite began as a **Stage 1 Dormant Echo**, discovered by local scouts.

A **containment ritual** was attempted, based on Crownwatch training and incomplete Arcanum notes.

### The mistake:
Containment circles *contain structured magic*.  
Void magic **consumes structure**.

The ritual fed the parasite *exactly* what it needed:
- runes  
- emotional imprints  
- reagents (moonshade, lantern oils)  
- arcane notes  
- a cluster of sleeping minds  

The parasite entered **Stage 3 Echo Bloom**.

The result:
- villagers’ minds trapped in projection loops  
- physical villagers partially responsive  
- runes spread like fungal mycelium  
- nightmare version of the town forming beneath reality  
- the projection gained enough mass to manifest a boss entity:  
  **[[The Hollow Dreamer]]**

---

## 🜁 The Nature of the Nightmare Realm (Echo Layer)
The Nightmare Realm is **NOT** a separate plane.  
It is the parasite’s **projection substrate**:
- built from consumed memories,  
- shaped by dreams,  
- maintained by Void runes,  
- overlaid on the Material Plane like a warped reflection.

It is sometimes called:
- The Echo Layer  
- The Shadow-Mirror  
- The Dreamer’s Fold  

Inside:
- villagers behave like corrupted sleepwalkers  
- reflections act independently  
- layers of memory repeat  
- physical harm can sever the link and wake the victim

---

## 🜁 Vulnerabilities
### **In the Material Realm:**
Nearly invulnerable — kills the host before dying.

### **In the Nightmare Realm:**
The projection can be destroyed, which collapses the parasite’s power.

### **In Its Core:**
The true physical parasite is extremely weak once separated from its projection.

This is why the final confrontation *must* occur in the Echo Layer or at the heart of the containment circle.

## 🜁 Relationship to Higher Void Powers
The Echo-Born is **not a lieutenant or servant** of greater Void forces.  
It is a **byproduct** — a microbial organism in the ecosystem of the Void.

However…

### **Greater entities (such as the force behind the Blackening) CAN amplify it.**

This is likely what happened to Verrath at the Cradle Tower:
- exposure to Void runework  
- repeated proximity to anomalies  
- influence of a higher conceptual entity (not a parasite)  
- eventually becoming a conduit

Thus Duskwatch was not planned —  
but the Void used it.

## 🜁 Aftermath of an Echo Collapse
When the Echo Layer collapses:
- villagers awaken, exhausted and traumatized  
- runes decay into black ash  
- reflections realign  
- the parasite core becomes inert  
- a “Void pulse” travels along the leylines (foreshadowing the Blackening arc)

This pulse is detectable by high mages or Void-sensitive PCs.
