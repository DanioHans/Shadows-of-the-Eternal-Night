_The curse is broken. The village wakes up. Life resumes — imperfectly._

## 🜂 Current State (Post-Dream)

Duskwatch has **returned fully to the Material world**, but its people are:

- **Exhausted** (1–2 weeks without true rest)
    
- **Hungry and dehydrated**
    
- Emotionally unstable: relief, confusion, anger, guilt
    
- Largely unaware of what truly happened
    

Most villagers remember:

- Being _present_ but unable to choose
    
- Vivid, shared dreams
    
- A sense of **being watched kindly, then cruelly**
    

They do **not** understand the concept of a “Dreamer” or parasite.

---

## 🜂 Establishing Duskwatch as a Real Village

### What the Party Sees Immediately

- Cooking fires being relit for the first time
    
- Villagers helping one another without prompting
    
- People sleeping _in the open_ from sheer exhaustion
    
- Livestock being checked frantically
    
- Children clinging to adults
    

The village is **alive**, but barely holding together.

## 🜂 Key NPCs (Post-Dream)

### **Mayor Elric Thorne**

_Human, late 50s, exhausted but resolute_

- Has been awake for less than an hour when the party meets him
    
- Suffers headaches and tremors
    
- Genuinely grateful, but overwhelmed
    

**What he knows**

- Someone _was_ researching strange phenomena near the well
    
- That person was **not** from Duskwatch
    
- The research was done quietly, “so as not to cause panic”
    
- The mayor allowed it — and now deeply regrets it
    

**What he admits**

> “If this came from curiosity… then I let it in.”

He will **direct the party** toward:

- His manor
    
- A locked study
    
- The researcher’s belongings
    

---

### **Mira Fenlock – Acting Innkeeper (Gull’s Rest)**

_Daughter of the deceased barkeep_

This is where you ground the accidental death.

- Her father **drowned in the cellar** during the dream-state
    
- No one saw it happen — bodies acted on instinct
    
- She does **not accuse** the party outright
    
- Her grief is quiet, sharp, contained
    

If the party brings it up honestly:

- She accepts it as “something the dream made happen”
    
- But she will **never fully forget**
    

This creates:

- Emotional consequence
    
- Long-term RP weight
    
- No immediate hostility
    

---

### **Brother Halven – Chapel Keeper**

_Gaunt, shaken, spiritually unsettled_

- Knows _something_ was wrong early
    
- Tried prayer and ritual — none worked
    
- Feels abandoned by his god **or unheard**
    

He provides:

- Emotional framing
    
- The sense that divine forces were _obscured_, not absent
    

---

## 🜂 Villager Mood & Interrogation Tone

Villagers are **not eager to answer questions**.

Use this framing:

- Short answers
    
- Frayed patience
    
- “I don’t remember clearly”
    
- “Please… later.”
    

If the party pushes too hard:

- They get frustration, not hostility
    
- People shut down emotionally
    

This reinforces:

> Duskwatch is not hiding answers — it simply doesn’t have them.

---

## 🜂 The Academy Researcher — Field Ledger

### What Exists

The party can find:

- A **weathered field ledger**
    
- Notes written over several days
    
- Academic, detached tone that becomes increasingly frantic
    

This belonged to:

> **Arcanist Calder Veyl**  
> _(Academy of Applied Thaumaturgy — Caer Hollow)_

---

### Contents of the Ledger (Player-Facing)

The notes establish:

- The phenomenon was **not summoned**
    
- It was **discovered**
    
- The well sat atop a _natural weakness_
    
- The entity fed on **repetition, fear, and unconscious thought**
    
- Early tests involved _dream observation_, not control
    

Key passage:

> “The projection reacts poorly to awareness. Once observed directly, it resists, adapts, and deepens.”

Later entry:

> “I fear it has begun to look back.”

Final entry is incomplete.

---

### What This Answers

- Duskwatch was **not cursed intentionally**
    
- The Academy bears indirect responsibility
    
- The entity beneath was **never meant to be engaged**
    
- This was **reckless research**, not malice
    

This cleanly:

- Redirects blame away from villagers
    
- Creates institutional culpability
    
- Seeds Caer Hollow investigation later
