# Session – The Dead Frequency (Continuation)

## Recap — Where We Left Off

The party descended beneath the **Sulfur Baths** in Smokestack Row on a retrieval job from **Lin**. Their target: a sealed metal case hidden in **Ossa Dren's** vault — a converted pump room locked behind mechanical security. Inside the case: Arcanum transit permits and a construction schematic that prove institutional involvement in the secret subterranean project.

They navigated the tunnels, found evidence of another team already present (boot prints, lamp oil smell), and followed the east branch toward the vault. **Dross's team** — Hael, Pol, and Grenn — had been failing at the vault door for roughly 20 minutes when the party arrived. The party was not quiet enough on approach, and **Grenn tackled Merios** as a precaution when they rounded the corner.

**Current state:**

- Merios is on the ground, grappled by Grenn (the muscle). Not injured — it was a tackle, not an attack.
- Hael has drawn his shortsword but has not struck. He is assessing.
- Pol is pressed against the vault door behind them, lockpick still in hand, visibly terrified.
- The rest of the party is in the tunnel branch, weapons likely drawn, facing the standoff.
- The vault door is still locked. One tumbler is jammed from Pol's failed attempt.
- **Scrap is absent this session.** Vixen is with the party but Scrap's player is not. Handle Scrap as background — he is present but hangs back, watchful, not acting unless directly needed. Do not give Scrap spotlight moments meant for his player.

**The volatile magic rules are still in effect.** Any spell of 1st level or higher requires a DC 13 Con save or triggers a surge (2d6 force damage to the caster, audible throughout the tunnels). Cantrips require DC 10. Detection spells auto-surge. Magic items function normally.

## The Standoff — Resolving the Cliffhanger

This scene opens the session. The energy should be tense but not locked into combat. Hael is a professional — he tackled first and is now reading the room. The party's response in the first 30 seconds determines whether this becomes a fight, a negotiation, or something in between.

### If the Party Chooses Diplomacy

Hael speaks first if given a beat of silence:

> _"We have a contract. You have weapons. Neither of us needs this to get ugly."_

If the party responds without immediate violence, Hael orders Grenn to release Merios (Grenn obeys, but stays close). Hael sheathes his shortsword — a deliberate gesture of de-escalation.

**What Hael will offer:**

- He doesn't care what's in the vault. He was paid to retrieve a sealed case and bring it back. He will propose: the party opens the door (since Pol clearly can't), they split whatever is inside, everyone walks away.
- If the party refuses to split: Hael calculates. He's outnumbered. He'll negotiate down to walking away empty-handed if the party lets him leave without trouble. He'd rather report failure to Dross than die in a tunnel.
- **Price to buy him off entirely:** 200 gold — more than Dross is paying. He'll walk and tell Dross the vault was empty when he arrived. The party probably doesn't want to spend this much, but the option exists.

**What Hael knows (if questioned):**

- Hired by Dross through a dead-drop system. Never met Dross face to face.
- Told to retrieve "a sealed case from the Dren property" and deliver it to a location in Scholar's Descent. He was given the tunnel entrance location and nothing else.
- He does not know what's in the case. He does not know who Ossa was beyond "a dead smuggler."
- He does not know anything about the lattice, Verrath, or the broader conspiracy. He is a contractor doing a job.

**Pol** will surrender information faster than Hael if pressured — he's scared and this is clearly above his pay grade. He can confirm the same details as Hael but adds that the dead-drop location changes every job, suggesting Dross is careful about being traced.

> 💡 **Ivor Note:** This is Ivor's scene. He speaks the same language as Hael — professional to professional. Encourage Ivor's player to take the lead in negotiation. If Samson or another player starts dominating the conversation, have Hael pointedly address Ivor: _"I'm talking to the one who knows how this works."_

### If the Party Chooses Combat

Grenn is grappling Merios. Initiative.

**Round 1 priorities:**

- **Grenn** (Thug, HP 32) holds Merios unless forced to release. He fights defensively — shove, grapple, maul swing if threatened. He is not trying to kill. He is buying time.
- **Hael** (Veteran, HP 45) engages the nearest threat with shortsword + hand crossbow. He fights smart — uses cover from the tunnel walls, targets whoever seems most dangerous. He will call for surrender if the party drops Grenn quickly.
- **Pol** (Spy, HP 20) runs. He bolts down the main corridor toward the south exit. If caught, he surrenders immediately and begs. He is not a fighter.

**Volatile magic in combat:** If Samson or Kyron casts a spell, remind them of the surge rules. A surge mid-combat alerts Dross's watchers above ground and starts a clock — reinforcements won't arrive during this fight, but Dross will know someone was here.

**Combat should be short.** Dross's team is outmatched. Hael surrenders when Grenn goes down or when he takes more than 25 damage. He is pragmatic: _"Alright. Alright. We're done. Take the bloody case."_

**If Hael is killed:** The party loses a potential future informant. Dross eventually learns his team disappeared, which accelerates his response but gives no immediate consequences this session.

**If Hael is captured alive:** He cooperates fully. He values his life over Dross's contract. He can become a recurring minor contact if the party treats him well — someone who knows how Dross operates even if he doesn't know why.

## The Vault Door — Ivor's Lock

With Dross's team dealt with, the party faces the door. Pol jammed one of the three tumblers during his attempt, making the lock slightly harder.

- **DC 16 Thieves' Tools** to pick (DC 17 if Pol's jammed tumbler isn't reset first).
- **Resetting the jammed tumbler:** DC 12 Thieves' Tools. Quick fix if Ivor examines it first.
- On a failure by 5 or more, a second tumbler jams. DC 12 to reset each before trying again.
- If Ivor's player narrates his approach — slowing down, feeling the mechanism, describing his technique — consider advantage or a +2 circumstantial bonus. Reward engagement.

> 💡 **Give this moment time.** Describe the mechanism: the weight of the tumblers, the precision required, the satisfying click when each one catches. This is Ivor's centrepiece. Don't rush past it.

**Alternative entry — the hinges:** DC 14 Investigation to notice the hinges are on the tunnel side. DC 13 Strength to remove pins. Noisy (metal-on-metal), but functional.

**Alternative entry — the drainage conduit:** Still exists, but with Scrap absent, Vixen can still scout through it (DM controls Vixen briefly). The conduit is passable for a Small creature or a squeezing Medium creature (DC 14 Acrobatics). If Vixen scouts successfully, all trap Perception DCs inside the vault drop by 2.

## The Vault Interior — Challenge Sequence

The vault is roughly 30 by 20 feet. Converted pump room. Stone walls, exposed pipe brackets, a desk against the far wall with the case in a locked drawer. Between the door and the desk: Ossa's mechanical defences.

**The room is dark.** No torches, no lanterns. Anyone without darkvision needs a light source — and carrying a torch means one hand occupied while navigating traps.

### Challenge 1 — The Weighted Floor

The first ten feet beyond the door are a **counterweight system**. A balanced stone slab that tilts when more than 150 lbs stands on it, creating a ramp that slides everything back toward the doorway. Not lethal — designed to push intruders out.

- **DC 13 Perception** to notice the seam lines and flex.
- **Solutions:**
    - Cross one at a time, lightest first. Characters over 150 lbs need DC 12 Acrobatics.
    - Jam the counterweight: DC 14 Investigation to find the fulcrum, then a piton or iron bar locks it.
    - Vixen (if she scouted) can scratch at the fulcrum point from the far side to show where to jam it.

> 💡 **Group challenge.** This works best when multiple players contribute — one spots the seam, another identifies the mechanism, a third provides the muscle to jam it. Don't let one player solve it alone.

### Challenge 2 — The Pipe Maze

The middle section is cluttered with old pump infrastructure — thick iron pipes floor to ceiling, some horizontal at waist or chest height. Ossa added **two spring-loaded bolt launchers** hidden in pipe brackets, triggered by tripwires at shin height.

- **DC 14 Perception** to spot tripwires.
- **DC 13 Thieves' Tools** to disarm each.
- If triggered: bolt fires, +5 to hit, 1d8+2 piercing. Loud — metal on metal, audible in the tunnels.
- **Navigation:** DC 11 Stealth for anyone in medium or heavy armour to avoid clanging against pipes. Failure isn't dangerous — just noisy.

> 💡 **With Scrap absent**, the party loses his automatic advantage on Perception here and his narrative ease with the pipes. This section becomes slightly harder and less atmospheric without him. That's fine — it makes his absence felt without punishing the party.

### Challenge 3 — The Gear Lock

The final five feet before the desk contain a **gear-driven floor mechanism**. When triggered by weight, two things happen: a heavy iron plate drops from the ceiling to block retreat, and a mechanical arm swings the desk into a recessed wall cavity — taking the case with it.

This trap doesn't hurt. It **takes the prize away.**

- **DC 15 Perception** to spot the trigger. If Vixen scouted, DC drops to 13.
- **Disarming:**
    - **Ivor — Thieves' Tools DC 15:** Jam the trigger from above.
    - **Scrap's option (unavailable this session):** With Scrap absent, his unique interaction with the gears doesn't happen. The party must use conventional disarming or accept the consequences.
    - **Brute force alternative:** If the mechanism triggers and the desk retreats, the party can disassemble the wall mechanism from the outside — 1 hour of noisy, physical work. Ivor can speed this to 30 minutes with a DC 14 Thieves' Tools check.
    - **Kyron — The Resonance Moment:** If the mechanism triggers and the desk disappears, or if conventional disarming fails, this is where Kyron's unique moment lives.

### Kyron's Moment — The Resonance

If the gear mechanism needs energy to reset — or if the party reaches a point where only magic could solve the problem but the volatile zone makes that dangerous — Kyron can try something no one else can.

Narrate directly to Kyron's player:

> _"The energy down here isn't chaos. You've felt it since you descended — beneath the instability, there's a rhythm. Geometric. Deliberate. Your clockwork glyphs have been flickering in response the whole time. Not painfully. Sympathetically. Like two instruments tuned to the same key."_

> _"You think you could push. Just a small pulse, channelled with the lattice's frequency rather than against it. Not a spell — something deeper. Something that's yours."_

**Mechanic:** Kyron makes a straight **Charisma check, DC 13**. Not a spell, not a save — this is his innate Clockwork Soul connection.

- **Success:** His glyphs align with the lattice pulse. A small wave of ordered energy flows through the mechanism. The gears reverse. The desk slides back. No surge, no sound. The party stares. Samson has no explanation for what just happened.
- **Failure:** Standard surge — 2d6 force damage to Kyron, audible boom. But even in the failure, Kyron felt the lattice _almost_ respond. The connection is real. It just wasn't enough this time. The party must disassemble manually.

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** This moment should be brief and quiet. Don't explain it. Narrate what happens. Let the table sit with it. The weight comes from realisation, not exposition.

## The Case — What It Contains

The desk drawer has a simple lock — DC 10 Thieves' Tools, trivial for Ivor after the triple-tumbler door.

Inside, a flat metal box the size of a thick book. Mechanical clasp, no magic.

**Contents:**

- **Three Arcanum transit permits** — official documents authorising transport of "arcane infrastructure materials" through city sub-channels. Stamped with the **Royal Arcanum Infrastructure Office** seal. Signed with an indecipherable initial. Dated within the last two months.
- **A single folded construction schematic** — hand-drawn, precise, showing a cross-section of a **crystalline node** embedded in a chamber beneath the city. Annotations in Arcanum shorthand describe "ley convergence amplification" and "radial channel alignment." The schematic shows one node and its immediate connections — not a complete map, but unmistakably a piece of a larger design.

**This is finite information.** Three permits, one schematic. There are no "more entries" to ask about. The permits prove institutional involvement. The schematic proves deliberate, sophisticated construction. Together with any previous evidence the party holds, the picture tightens significantly.

> 💡 **Samson's moment:** This is his analytical payoff. After the heist, not during it. He reads the schematic and understands what it means. If they have the Rat King evidence or the Silverward "infrastructure assessment" notice, he connects the dots. His value was not in the vault — it is in what the vault's contents mean. Let him have this.

## Extraction

With the case in hand, the party needs to leave the tunnels. What they face depends on what happened earlier:

**If Dross's team was dealt with diplomatically (Hael walked away):**

- Clean exit. Dross's watchers above ground note the party leaving the Baths but don't intervene — their orders were to watch, not engage.
- Dross learns within a day that someone entered Ossa's vault. He doesn't immediately know it was the party unless Hael describes them.

**If Dross's team was fought:**

- Check: did any spell trigger a surge during the fight? If yes, Dross's watchers above are alert and have sent word. The party exits the Baths to find the watchers gone — they've reported in. Dross will know specifics within hours.
- If no surge: the watchers are still in position but just watching. The party can slip out via the side entrance to avoid being identified.

**If combat was loud or the gear lock shrieked:**

- Same as surge — the watchers are aware something happened below. Accelerated Dross response.

## Returning to Lin

Lin meets Ivor at a pre-arranged location — not the Baths. She examines the case, takes **one transit permit** for herself (payment for rebuilding her information network), and lets the party keep the rest including the schematic.

She pays the agreed gold. Then:

> _"The experiment. You asked."_

A pause.

> _"Ossa moved goods for a lot of people. One of her clients — years ago, before the city work started — transported research materials for a wizard working out of the Arcanum annex in the Third Kingdom. Experimental magic. Memory displacement. Ossa said the wizard wasn't trying to erase anyone's mind. He was trying to copy it. The erasure was a side effect."_

> _"That's all I have. Ossa never gave me the name. She might not have known it."_

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** This is a rumour, not a resolution. Ivor's memory loss may have been a side effect of memory _duplication_, not destruction. New questions: who wanted to copy his memories? Why? Is the copy still out there? These are threads for later. Do not resolve them here.
