### Issued by the Office of Magister-Auditor Cael Reno

### Crownwatch Bastion, Caer Hollow

---

_The following is provided as a courtesy to those unfamiliar with formal court proceedings. The Bastion expects all attending parties to conduct themselves accordingly. Ignorance of procedure is not accepted as grounds for leniency._

---

## Before You Enter

All weapons, wands, staves, and rods are to be surrendered at the entrance and will be returned upon the conclusion of proceedings. Personal effects and spell components are not subject to confiscation, but their use inside the chamber is strictly prohibited.

The chamber operates under **arcane assurance**. Those who take the witness stand do so under conditions that the court considers binding. You are advised to bear this in mind before you speak.

---

## Conduct in the Chamber

The Magistrate holds the floor at all times. You may speak when addressed, or when formally recognised. To request the floor, **stand and wait**. Do not speak over a witness, over opposing counsel, or over the Magistrate.

Address the Magistrate as _Your Honour_ or _Magistrate_. Address witnesses directly and by name.

The proceedings have a structure. There will be a time for accusation, and a time for defence. If something is said during accusation that you wish to challenge, note it — your opportunity will come. Interrupting a witness mid-testimony will not be tolerated and will reflect poorly on your case.

---

## What You Are Permitted to Do

**Object.** If a witness makes a claim you believe to be an assumption rather than fact, a statement they could not have directly observed, or something you consider irrelevant to the charge, you may stand and state your objection. The Magistrate will hear it and rule. An objection is a request, not a right — but it will be considered.

**Question witnesses.** After each witness for the accusation has given their account, you will be given the opportunity to question them directly. Use this time carefully. Ask questions. Do not make speeches — that is what the defence phase is for.

**Call witnesses of your own.** During the defence phase, you may call witnesses the court has not yet heard from, provided they are present or can be summoned without unreasonable delay. You may speak on the accused's behalf yourself. You may present evidence relevant to the charge.

**Request a recess.** If you require a moment to confer, you may ask the Magistrate. He may grant it at his discretion.

---

## What You Are Advised Against

Attempting to speak for the accused before they have been given the opportunity to speak for themselves.

Treating the witness stand as a platform for argument. Witnesses answer questions. Arguments are made to the Magistrate, during the defence phase, by counsel.

Introducing evidence or raising claims during cross-examination that bear no relation to what the witness actually said. Stay within the bounds of their testimony.

Demonstrating contempt — for the court, for the Magistrate, for witnesses, or for the process itself. The Magistrate is not your adversary. He is attempting to establish what is true. Treat him accordingly.

Any use of magic inside this chamber will be treated as a separate offence and will result in immediate removal. This applies to all members of the attending party without exception.

---

## A Final Note

The court is aware that some of those present are not accustomed to formal proceedings. This is not unusual. The Magistrate will allow reasonable latitude for those navigating this process for the first time.

That latitude has limits.

Come prepared to speak plainly, to listen carefully, and to make your case on the strength of what you actually know. The court responds to honesty and to reason. It responds less well to performance.

_The session will begin when the Magistrate is seated._
