## 🜁 DM TRUTH – WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENED
*(Not directly available to players)*

The Echo-Born Void Parasite originated in the **Shadowfell**, existing in an unanchored, dormant state.  
Recent Shadowfell instability weakened planar boundaries, allowing the parasite to breach into the Material Plane.

Duskwatch became vulnerable due to:
- proximity to leylines
- reflective geography (fountain, well, chapel)
- heightened emotional stress among villagers

The parasite crossed over **recently**, escalating rapidly:
- Stage 1 → Stage 2 within weeks
- Stage 3 Echo Bloom within days of intervention

### The Villager (The Anchor)
A former **Arcanum initiate** (washed out or reassigned) identified the anomaly:
- recurring dream loops
- reflection lag
- emotional resonance spikes
- contradictory magical signatures

Knowing the anomaly was beyond their capability, the villager **contacted Verrath directly**, bypassing local Crownwatch and Arcanum channels.

The villager:
- assisted in ritual setup
- served as the resonance focus
- became the living anchor

They did not act out of ambition, but urgency.

### Verrath’s Involvement
Verrath arrived with:
- theoretical Void knowledge
- experimental containment sigils
- incomplete understanding of Echo-type behavior

He supervised or modified a **containment ritual** intended to stabilize and observe the anomaly.

Fatal error:
- Void magic does not obey structure
- containment glyphs were consumed
- the ritual accelerated Echo Bloom

Once Stage 3 manifested:
- destroying the host risked uncontrolled Void backlash
- projection collapse could spread the anomaly

Verrath chose **observation and withdrawal** over direct intervention.

Duskwatch was left contained-at-distance.

This philosophy becomes foundational to the Blackening.
