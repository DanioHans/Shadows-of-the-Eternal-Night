

**Level**: 4  
**Tone**: Interrogation, intrigue, subtle foreshadowing  
**Length**: ~2.5–3 hours  

---

## Purpose
- Conclude the Jespin Wrent smuggling arc.  
- Give players layered but contradictory information — nothing fully clear.  
- Provide their first real hook toward Duskwatch.  
- Show cracks in Caer Hollow’s order.  

---

## Structure

### 1. Return & Setup (~20 min)
- PCs return to **Crownwatch Bastion** with Wrent in custody.  
- Present: **Captain Malma**, **Cael Rynel** (scribe/investigator), **Sergeant Halna**, **Verrath** (brief appearance).  
- Tone: professional inquiry, not an arrest — but players may feel cornered.  
- Verrath’s cameo:  
  - Coolly praises Samson for “obedience.”  
  - Warns against meddling “beyond one’s measure.”  
  - Leaves before interrogation begins.

---

### 2. Interrogation of Jespin (~40 min)

Wrent is evasive, fearful, and tries to downplay. Reveals more with persuasion/intimidation or by being pressed.

**Tier 1 – Easy (DC 10 Persuasion / Intimidation or baseline questioning):**
- He smuggled creatures for coin.  
- Basilisk was “destined for richer hands” but broke loose too early.  
- *Quote:* “I’m no tamer! I just move things where they’re asked.”  

**Tier 2 – Moderate (DC 13–14):**
- Paid unusually well in advance.  
- A pouch stamped with a **Silver Sigil** delivered payment.  
- *Quote:* “Never saw his face. Gloves, voice like sand. But that pouch… nobles carry weight different.”  

**Tier 3 – Hard (DC 16+ or very good RP):**
- Mentions **Duskwatch**: “Stopped there once. A whole village like a stage play with no actors. Silent, wrong. I didn’t stay.”  
- Delivered smaller shipments near there before.  

**Tier 4 – Automatic if his possessions are searched:**  
- **Ledger fragments** (see below).  
- A **burned rat carcass** marked with strange symbol (if not already taken).  
- Journal page with paranoid rambling:  
  - *“Leather rots fastest near the tannery drains. Mustn’t linger there.”*  

---

### Ledger Fragments (Handout Ideas)

Use short, cryptic notes the players can puzzle over:  

- “2 cages. 1 basilisk. Paid in full — Silver Sigil. Destination: H.R.”  
  - (H.R. = High Ring; players may guess royalty/nobility.)  

- “Small crates, Duskwatch route. No taverns. No questions.”  

- “Leather rots. Do not trust the boy with the scar.”  
  - (False lead / paranoia.)  

- “Coin runs backward. Ledger kept separate.”  
  - (Hints at hidden book or second set of accounts.)  

---

### 3. Secondary Threads (30–40 min)

If interrogation runs short:  
- **Ledger puzzle**: Allow Arcana/History/Insight checks to piece out meaning.  
- **Silver Sigil pouch**: If shown, a nervous Arcanum clerk later admits:  
  - *“I’ve seen that crest on papers from Verrath’s office.”*  
- **Branded rat**: If taken to Silverward → cleric mutters about a “mark of unmooring,” which they can’t explain further.

---

### 4. Free Roam in the City (~40–60 min)

After interrogation, PCs are released. They have freedom to wander — insert these if you need pacing filler:

- **Pickpocket attempt**  
  - A child/thief bumps into a PC (Perception DC 12 to notice).  
  - If caught, thief whines that “the glove-man” (Wrent) owed money all over the slums.  
  - Chase option if they run (Athletics/Acrobatics DC 13).  

- **Rumor at the Market**  
  - NPCs gossip: “They say the creature was meant for a noble’s menagerie. Guess nobles like stone statues.”  

- **Small scuffle**  
  - Greyspite cultist and slum merchant arguing.  
  - Players can intervene (combat or RP).  
  - Merchant mutters after: “Wrent’s lot sniffed around here last week.”  

- **Arcanum visit (Samson/Kyron tie-in)**  
  - A clerk is willing to share minor Arcanum gossip.  
  - *“Something’s off in the restricted wings. Spells aren’t holding like they should.”*  

## Rumors of **Duskwatch**

1. _“That place ain’t right. Went through Duskwatch a fortnight past—villagers just… stared. Didn’t say a word.”_
    
2. _“Shadows move funny there. Like they don’t match what’s casting them.”_
    
3. _“They say the church bell still rings, even though no one’s been up in that tower for years.”_
    
4. _“A hunter swears he saw two versions of the same man walking the fields. One vanished when the other spoke.”_
    
5. _“The fog don’t roll out of Duskwatch. It rolls _into_ it.”_
    

---

## Rumors of **Ironhold**

1. _“Ironhold’s miners are striking again—claim the shafts whisper at night.”_
    
2. _“Whole veins of ore turned brittle, worthless. Like the rock itself is rotting.”_
    
3. _“Saw a man come back from Ironhold with eyes black as coal. Said he hadn’t slept in three nights.”_
    
4. _“Merchants used to stop there on the road south. Not anymore. Too many wagons gone missing.”_
    
5. _“The old watchtower on the cliffs? They say its beacon burns without fire. Just a dull red glow.”_

---

### 5. Wrap-Up (~20 min)
- PCs rewarded:  
  - 50 gp each.  
  - Merios’ sigil cloak.  
  - Samson: restricted archive access.  
### New Leads — Quest Options

Captain Malma (or Cael Rynel) briefs the party:  

> “Two matters have crossed my desk. You’ve proven capable, and frankly, my resources are thin. I won’t order you, but I’ll offer you these choices:”

### Option 1 — Duskwatch (Main Arc)
- **Hook**: Caravans stopped arriving. Reports of silence and unresponsive villagers.  
- **Tone**: Eerie ghost-town, Shadowfell bleed but not yet full.  
- **Reward**: Recognition, gold, and most importantly, **critical clues to the Shadowfell arc**.  
- **Level Up**: Completing Duskwatch should bring party to level 5.

### Option 2 — Ironhold (Side Arc)
- **Hook**: Mining town east of Caer Hollow. Reports of ore veins turning into brittle black crystal. Miners missing without a trace.  
- **Tone**: Tangible danger, investigation of corrupted environment. Less eerie than Duskwatch, more physical.  
- **Reward**: Gold, possibly an elemental shard (future crafting). Foreshadowing of Forgotten Magic contamination.  
- **Level Up**: Completing Greyforge can also justify level 5 if Duskwatch is delayed.  

**NPC Messengers / Sources**:  
- A nervous miner brought to Caer Hollow, begging the court for aid (Greyforge).  
- A caravan guard survivor reporting blank-faced villagers (Duskwatch).  

---

## Outro

- Players decide their direction.  
- End on their preparations: buying supplies, debating routes.  
- If time runs short, you can let them finish with Malma’s briefing and “fade out” on their decision.


---

## NPC Roles
- **Jespin Wrent** – Skittish, cowardly smuggler. Gives info in pieces.  
- **Captain Malma** – Stern, fair, acknowledges PCs’ competence.  
- **Cael Rynel** – Judicial investigator, formal and exacting.  
- **Sergeant Halna** – Keeps guard sharp, suspicious of outsiders.  
- **Verrath** – Short appearance; distant but intimidating presence.

---

## Outcomes
- Wrent arc is closed.  
- Players earn coin and reputation.  
- First hints of Duskwatch established.  
- PCs begin to feel **watched** and enmeshed in Caer Hollow’s politics.
