**Primary Tone:** Aftermath, exhaustion, relief  
**Secondary Tone:** Quiet unease, returning normality  
**Pacing Goal:** Flexible RP → reflective travel → light resolution  
**Hard Goal:** Close Duskwatch cleanly and reposition the party for Caer Hollow intrigue

## I. Duskwatch — Waking World

### Purpose

Let the players **feel the cost and consequence** of what they’ve done.  
This section can take **as long as the players want**.

---

### State of the Village (Post-Dream)

Duskwatch is awake — fully, physically, undeniably real.

- Villagers are **exhausted, hungry, dehydrated**
    
- Fires are being relit
    
- People sit or lie down wherever they can
    
- Livestock is alive but neglected
    
- No one understands _what_ happened — only that it’s over
    

There is **relief**, but no celebration.

---

### Player Focus (Open RP)

Likely actions:

- Distributing food and water
    
- Checking on specific villagers
    
- Asking for explanations
    
- Seeking someone “in charge”
    

Villagers are:

- Polite but tired
    
- Short in their answers
    
- Emotionally raw
    

They **cannot explain the dream**, only describe fragments:

- Repetition
    
- Pressure
    
- Being watched
    
- Acting without choice
    

---

### Key NPC Touchpoints

(Reference your existing notes as needed)

- **Mayor Elric Thorne** — overwhelmed, ashamed, cooperative
    
- **Mira Fenlock** — acting innkeeper of Gull’s Rest, grieving
    
- **Brother Halven** — shaken faith, unsettled silence
    
- **Tamsin Roe** — disturbed by shipment ledgers written in his own hand
    

The party can learn:

- Someone _was_ researching anomalies beneath Duskwatch
    
- The research was sanctioned quietly
    
- Notes and belongings remain behind
    

---

### The Field Notes (Explicit Lead, Soft Push)

If the party looks for answers, they are directed toward:

- The Mayor’s study
    
- Or a sealed room containing the researcher’s effects
    

They can recover:

- **A field ledger belonging to Arcanist Calder Veyl**
    
- Notes describing:
    
    - The anomaly as _discovered_, not summoned
        
    - The well as a natural weakness
        
    - Warnings that the phenomenon reacted badly to observation
        

This is the **first clear thread tying Duskwatch to Caer Hollow’s institutions**.

> You do **not** need to read the ledger in full this session — it can be skimmed, summarized, or taken along.

---

### Closing Duskwatch

Before the party leaves, offer a quiet moment:

- A shared meal
    
- A simple thank-you
    
- A question like: _“Is it really gone?”_
    

No definitive answers.

Duskwatch returns to routine — scarred, but alive.

## II. Travel to Caer Hollow (4 Days)

### Purpose

Decompression, character RP, reflection.

No urgency. No ticking clock.

---

### Travel Tone

- Calm roads
    
- Familiar terrain
    
- The world feels _solid again_
    

Encourage:

- Private conversations
    
- Processing doubles, dreams, fear
    
- Reading or discussing the field notes
    

---

### Optional Encounter: Bandits (If Needed)

**Use only if you want to:**

- Break up long RP
    
- Reintroduce grounded danger
    
- Contrast dream horror with mundane violence
    

**Bandit Concept**

- Desperate, not organized
    
- Poorly equipped
    
- Target travelers because supply lines have been unreliable
    

This reinforces:

- Duskwatch wasn’t isolated
    
- Something is quietly destabilizing the region
    

Combat should be:

- Short
    
- Unremarkable
    
- Almost disappointing
    

Reality, not spectacle.

---

## III. Arrival at Caer Hollow

### Purpose

Close loops, reset status quo, prepare for the next arc.

---

### On Arrival

- City is busy, noisy, alive
    
- No visible signs of Duskwatch’s suffering
    
- The contrast should feel _wrong_
    

The party can:

- Report in
    
- Claim promised rewards
    
- Restock
    
- Secure lodging
    

Authorities respond with:

- Relief
    
- Bureaucratic distance
    
- Polite deflection
    

Nothing feels hostile — just **slow**.

---

### If Time Allows: Light Resolution

Possible scenes:

- Turning in reports
    
- Being thanked, but not questioned deeply
    
- Rewards granted without ceremony
    

If the party presses for escalation:

- They are told matters will be “reviewed”
    
- Or referred to later meetings
    

This subtly reinforces:

> Information disappears upward.

---

## IV. Rumours & Whispers (Only If Players Push Fast)

Use **one-liners**, overheard or casually mentioned — no exposition.

Pick 1–2 at most.

- “Strange shipments keep vanishing _inside_ the city now.”
    
- “The Academy’s been asking questions nobody remembers answering.”
    
- “Someone high up’s been delaying reports. Always the same signature.”
    
- “They say the King hasn’t personally reviewed a border report in months.”
    
- “Some mage vanished from the Academy archives. No one agrees on when.”
    

These are **seeds only** — do not elaborate unless the players insist.

---

## Session End State

By the end of Session 11:

- Duskwatch is resolved emotionally
    
- The party has tangible evidence (field notes)
    
- Caer Hollow feels _stable but untrustworthy_
    
- A larger pattern is implied, not revealed
    

Session 12 can begin:

- With investigation
    
- With political tension
    
- Or with the first real push against institutional silence
