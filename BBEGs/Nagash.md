**Nagash Lord of the Undead  
  
(CR 18)

  
Large Creature  
Speed 0 feet,  Fly 20 feet (hover)

HP 200

AC 50

Saving throws: Con +13, Wis +13, Dex +10, Str +11

Damage Resistances: Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing from magical weapons - Silvered?

Damage Immunities: Necrotic, Poison, Psychic; Bludgeoning, Piercing and slashing from non-magical weapons.

Condition Immunities: [[charmed]], deafened, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, prone, stunned

Senses: truesight 120 ft., passive Perception 17

  

He is a Wizard with access to Lores of: Blood, Spirit, Nehekhara, Vampires and Undeath

  

STR 20 (+5)

DEX 18 (+4)

CON 24 (+7)

INT 17 (+3)

WIS 25 (+7)

CHA 15 (+2)

PROF (+6)

  

Spell Attack Mod: +13 to hit, +7 to damage

Melee Attack Mod: +16 to hit, +5 to damage

  

Passives:

  

The 5 Books of Nagash

In order to slay Nagash all of his books must be destroyed, until then he is immune to all damage. For every destroyed Book, Nagash loses 5 AC (cannot be below 25 AC)

Death Magic Incarnate

All friendly creatures within 20 feet of Nagash suffer two less damage from attacks, this passive can stack with other modifiers. (eg, damage is 10, they take 8)

Arch-Necromancer

Once per turn the Arch-Necromancer ability allows Nagash to reroll the attack dice if the result would miss , although he must accept the second result and can only be used for spells.

Morikhane, The Black Armour 

A Magic Armor which gives Nagash advantage on Constitution saves.

  
Soul-Capsule

If Nagash hits a creature and makes them fall unconscious, they are transformed into a Servant of Nagash. (See Vampire Spellcaster or Vampire Warrior depending on the creature affected.) They have no control over their actions, but still make death saving throws. This curse is lifted if the Servant's health goes to 0 or below (they revive immediately with 1 hp) or they make their saving throws. Any creature afflicted by this curse is not able to die. One of the two conditions listed must be met. 

  

Attacks, Melee:

  

Zefet-Nebtar, The Mortis Blade 

  

A Magic weapon which gives him +5 to hit (3d10 magical slashing damage)

Reach of 10 feet  
  

- Multi-Wound - 1 Action

Up to three targets can be hit with a single attack roll if they are within 10 feet of each other.

Damage is divided to all targets rounding down. (e.g 16 damage / 3 targets = 5 damage to each)

  

- Necro Slash - Bonus Action, 1 round casting time

After casting this ability, Nagash wind ups his sword and imbues it with the spirits around him. On his next melee attack, if it is a hit, roll an extra 2d6 Necrotic damage

  

Soul Crush

This ability can be used as a bonus action, recharge of 5 rounds

Nagash stomps down onto the ground creating a crater 20x20 feet that becomes difficult terrain, all creatures within 15 feet of Nagash must succeed on a DC 20  Dexterity saving throw. On a failed save they take 2d6 Necrotic damage. Soul Crush pushes all creatures affected 20 feet from him.

  

Attacks, Magic:

  

The 5 Books of Nagash

- Nagash has mastered the art of 5 Magical Lores; : Blood, Spirit, Nehekhara, Vampires and Undeath

- He must use his move action to switch between the books.

- He gains certain passives specific to the book he is holding.

- He must switch books every 2 turns

- Nagash cycles through all 5 books in the order below of; Undeath, Vampire, Blood, Spirit, Nehekhara.

- If Nagash were to cycle to a book that is already destroyed, he does not cycle to the next book in the set. He must use his turn to either switch books and not have a move action or move. Nagash still can attack using his melee attacks.

- All spells from the 5 books of Nagash add the spell casting modifier to hit and on damage. (+13 to hit, +7 to damage)

  

- Lore of Undeath

  

Passives: 

Supreme Lord of the Undead

Any minions summoned from the Rising Death or Spirit Call spells can not be affected by spells or effects that target undead creatures.   

  

Rising Death

Whenever a spell is cast toward Nagash (whether it hits him or misses him) He absorbs a portion of power from the spell, keep a counter of how many spells have been casted (maximum of 20). When Nagash switches to the Lore of Undeath Nagash summons undead minions equal to the amount of spells on the counter, the counter then resets.

  

Spell:

Soul Stealer - 1 action

Soul Stealer is a direct damage spell with a 120 foot range against up to 3 targets. On a hit the target takes 2d12 Necrotic damage and must make a DC 18 Constitution save. On a fail they are weakened and have disadvantage on strength saving throws. They must roll the save on each of their turns. Nagash Lord of the Undead gains 1 Rising Death counter for each creature weakened by this effect.

  

Destruction:

In order to destroy the Lore of Undeath, the players must defeat 25 Shadows summoned by Rising Death and have no undead present when Nagash switches to this book.

  

- Tome of Vampire's

  

Passives:

The Curse of Undeath

Whenever the Curse of Years spell inflicts damage to a creature, Nagash regains the damage as Temporary HP

  

Invocation of Nehek

When Nagash Switches to this book any undead minions in his control regain 1d6+7 HP

  

Spell:

Curse of Years - 1 action

A hex spell with a 120 foot range, which is casted on a point with a circle AoE of 50 feet from the point of origin. All other Creatures in the target location when casted roll a DC 26 Strength save. On a fail they are slowed,  have disadvantage on Dexterity saves and take 1d8 force damage. Any other creature that starts or ends their turn inside the spell’s area makes a DC 20 Strength save. On a fail they take 1d12 force damage and are pushed 10 feet toward the spell’s point of origin.

  

Destruction:

In order to destroy the Tome of Vampire's, 4 or more creatures must escape from the Curse of Years spell.

  

- Oath of Blood

Passives:

  Life Leeching

When Soulblight inflicts damage to a creature, generate 1d4 into the Blood Pool for each creature damaged.

  

Blood Pool

When Nagash switches to Oath of Blood the Blood Pool overflows onto the battlefield. All non-friendly creatures must make a DC 18 Dexterity save. if one creature fails, all the dice in the Blood Pool are rolled, each creature takes Necrotic damage equal to the damage roll divided by the amount of creatures hit rounded down. (e,g damage is 48, 4 creatures affected, each take 12 damage) the Blood Pool starts with 5 dice in it.

  

Spell:

Soulblight - 1 action

A very powerful hex spell. Any non-friendly creature in Nagash's line of sight has disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws and -1 to AC. All non-friendly creatures that try to take a movement action take 1d8 Necrotic damage per 10 feet traveled.  
  

Destruction:

In order to destroy the Oath of Blood there must be 0 dice in the Blood Pool 4 rounds after Nagash has switched to Oath of Blood for the first time. 

  
- Scroll of Spirit

  

Passives:

Wreaking Watcher

A 40-foot radius of magical darkness extends out from Nagash, moves with him, and spreads around corners. The darkness lasts as long as Nagash is using the Scroll of Spirit. Darkvision can't penetrate this darkness, and no natural light can illuminate it. If any of the darkness overlaps with an area of light created by a spell of 2nd level or lower, the spell creating the light is dispelled.

  

Cloaked Spectre

Any friendly creatures that are in the range of Wreaking Watcher are stealthed and have double movement speed.

  

Spell:

Spirit Call - 1 action

Nagash summons 3 Spirits

  

~Undead Ethereal, only piercing damage by magical weapons and spells can harm a Spirit.

Spirit

HP 20, AC 16.

+11 to hit

speed 0 feet, fly 30 feet (hover)

Spirits have a chance to dodge attacks, roll a percentile, if the result is from 90-100 the spirit dodges.

- Exorcism-

The spirit attacks by flowing through its target, sucking life force from it. on a hit 1d6 +4 necrotic damage and the spirit regains half the damage as HP.

- Spirit Bomb - 

Nagash can cause a Spirit to explode as a Bonus action, Burst 1. 2d8 necrotic damage

Target(s) in the blast area must succeed on a DC 18 Dexterity Saving throw, on a fail they roll a DC 14 Constitution save, on a Dex save they take half damage. On a failed Con save the Spirit goes inside the target and explodes from within dealing 2d20 necrotic damage.

  

Any creature harmed while inside Wreaking Watcher gains spirit sense, they can detect the general direction of a spirit and gain advantage against spirits. (Bringing them back to normal rolling to hit.)

  

Destruction:

In order to destroy the Scroll of Spirit the players must be able to kill 6 Spirits from within the area of Wreaking Watcher, not counting Spirit Bomb deaths. And have no more spirits left when he switches back to the Scroll of Spirit.

  

- Lexicon of Nehekhara

  

Passives:

  

            No Easy Task

            When Nagash switches to the Lexicon of Nehekhara all friendly creatures’ AC become 0

Restless Undead

Whenever an Undead creature is attacked within 100 feet from Nagash, Nagash may use his reaction to cast either Khsar's Incantation or Ptra's Incantation as a reaction spell only on the attacked creature, ignoring range. The attacked creature regains 2d6 HP Immediately.

  

Calming Desert Winds

While Nagash is holding this book, none of his allies can attack, only move (unless Ptra's Incantation is used). They MUST make opportunity attacks, however.

  

Spells:

Khsar's Incantation -1 action

All friendly creatures within 50 feet of Nagash may make a free immediate move action (ignoring initiative, this still can garner opportunity attacks). You can't use this spell to move a creature more than once per turn.

  

Ptra's Incantation -1 action

All corpses (undead creatures that have died this past turn) rise from the dead with 1 HP and take a free, immediate melee action if an enemy creature is within 5 feet (ignoring initiative) 

  

Destruction:

In order to destroy the Lexicon of Nehekhara the players must not attack any enemies or get hit by a Spirit or Shadow for 1 round.

  

  
Nagash Wins if all enemies are turned into his Servants, as no one can die in this encounter.**
