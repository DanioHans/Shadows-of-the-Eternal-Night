### **Alternative Interrogation Scene: The Joint Debrief**

**Location:** An upper chamber in the [[Crownwatch Bastion]], usually used for coordinating with the Arcanum in magical matters. It’s secure, official, but not a prison cell.

**Setup:**

- After the basilisk is brought down and order is restored, all players are escorted together.
    
- **Captain Teron Veyne** oversees the initial debrief.
    
- Shortly into the questioning, the door opens and **High Arcanist Verrath** himself enters — not out of concern for the attack, but to verify rumors that an _Academy student_ and other magically gifted individuals were involved.
    

**Tone & Dynamics:**

- Verrath remains aloof and enigmatic, asking **high-level magical or philosophical questions** (e.g. “Did the creature show signs of unnatural decay?”).
    
- Captain Veyne grounds the conversation with **practical questions** (“Where were you standing when it escaped?” “Did anyone see who tampered with the cage?”).
    
- Everyone speaks — either voluntarily or when questioned directly.
    

**Optional Tension:**

- Queek might get some side-eyes but is allowed to speak for himself.
    
- Scrap is clearly not trusted but handled gently — possibly even _ignored_, which could be insulting.
    

**Outcome:**

- The party leaves with a **reputation**, some **respect**, and perhaps **Verrath’s subtle interest** in watching their progress.
    
- Verrath may request that **Samson provide a written report** or that **Kyron visit the Arcanum for “assessment.”**
    
- They’re not conscripted, but Captain Veyne says:  
    _“You handled yourselves well. If you find trouble like this again — or if it finds you — you come to us first.”_

## 📖 Session 1 Narration Blocks

---

### Opening – Market Day in Caer Hollow

*The streets of Caer Hollow hum with color and movement. It is Market Day — the once-a-month celebration where trade, chatter, and coin spill freely beneath banners strung across alleyways.*

The Eastveil Commons are more alive than usual: hawkers shout over one another, children dart between stalls with sweetfruit skewers, and the scent of spiced grains and roasting meats fills the air. A central stone fountain trickles quietly beneath the noise, surrounded by crates of produce and handwoven blankets. Beyond the main square, narrow alleys wind between tightly packed vendor stands, some barely wide enough to pass through.

Even the city guard seem relaxed, more focused on watching for pickpockets than keeping order. The sun sits high — and for once, Caer Hollow feels entirely at peace.

---

### Inciting Incident – The Basilisk Breaks Loose

*A sharp crack splits the market noise — a sound like splintered glass and grinding stone.*

From the beast smuggler’s stall, a cage erupts with arcane sparks. The crowd surges away as a long, low hiss echoes from within. A scaled creature — strange, twitching, flecked with unnatural arcane veining — claws its way forward. Its eyes shimmer with pale light, and where they fall, stone follows.

Panic blooms like wildfire. A woman screams as a nearby weasel bolts into the crowd. One of the guards drops his spear and flees. The basilisk prowls forward, unbothered, tail carving a deep line into the packed dirt.

---

### Mid-Fight – Market Chaos

*The square is chaos — stalls overturned, goods scattered, and civilians scrambling for cover.*

A crate of smaller beasts bursts open: birds whirl upward in screeching flocks, while a massive centipede scuttles across a fruit vendor’s cart. Cries of alarm rise and fall, occasionally silenced by the grim *crunch* of stone creeping across flesh.

Guards hesitate at the edge of the square. Some are shouting orders, others simply watching. The defense of Caer Hollow, it seems, has fallen to strangers.

---

### Aftermath – Crownwatch Arrival

*Moments after the last blow falls and the danger abates, the sound of heavy boots breaks the aftermath silence.*

A squad of Crownwatch guards, armor gleaming beneath deep blue cloaks, pushes through the wreckage. Their captain — a tall man with storm-gray hair and a scar bisecting his brow — surveys the square.

“You five — with us,” he says curtly. “We’ll need a full report. Now.”

Other guards begin cordoning off the area. One kneels beside a half-petrified merchant. Another mutters about “another damn Wastes beast” under his breath. The people of Caer Hollow begin to peer back into the square, warily, from alleys and doorways.

---

### Crownwatch Bastion – Temporary Detainment

*The Crownwatch Bastion sits in the High Ring — not far from the palace, but colder in its grandeur.*

Stone walls ring a modest keep adorned with the sigil of Velaris: a rising flame beneath a silver crown. Inside, the halls are silent save for the occasional echo of bootsteps or the ruffle of parchment. The players are led into a broad chamber with long benches and a magically lit ceiling, where they are asked to wait.

Eventually, they are questioned — not accused, but clearly watched.  
The figure leading the inquiry introduces himself with calm finality: **Captain Teron Veyne**.

He listens more than he speaks. Occasionally, a scribe at his side scribbles a name or detail.

Eventually, Veyne gives a subtle nod.  
“You handled yourselves well. That much is clear. If there are more of these incidents — and I believe there will be — I’ll want you watching for them.”

He does not ask. He simply expects.

---

### Closing – A Thread Pulled

*Outside, Caer Hollow resumes its rhythm, though the pulse is off.*

People whisper about statues in the square and monsters with glassy eyes. Vendors repack their goods with shaking hands. Somewhere in the Eastveil Commons, a slum alley door creaks open — but no one steps out.

The players leave the Bastion with a quiet burden: trust, unspoken expectations, and the first signs that something in Velaris is no longer right.

*The Watch begins.*
