
## Recap
- The party explored the **Gull’s Rest Inn** and **Ruined Watchtower**, finding disturbing evidence of the missing scouts and the spreading rune network.  
- Ivor decoded fragments of the **Crownwatch cipher**, linking the missing patrols to this area and hinting at something buried near the tower’s well.  
- At dusk, the mirrored doubles were seen entering the **Fountain Square**.  
- As the party returned, the reflections stepped into the fountain’s blackened water and vanished — leaving only silence, ripples, and the toll of the unseen bell.  

Night is now falling faster than usual. Lanterns dim instead of brighten. The village of Duskwatch stands perfectly still.

---

## Session Goals
1. **Close the Investigation Thread**
   - Optionally let the group inspect the **Hunter’s Hut** if they choose to cover every lead.  
     - Reward: final scout note confirming the rune network stretches beneath Duskwatch.  
     - Reinforce that the “well east of the tower” is where the anomaly converges.  
   - Otherwise, summarize that all material clues point toward the same source — *something below*.

2. **Advance the Mirrored Countdown (Phase IV – Overtaking)**
   - The reflections have now crossed fully into the physical world, entering *through* mirrors, water, and glass.  
   - The fountain is now the focal point — every reflective surface bleeds shadowlight.  
   - PCs occasionally see their reflections *act independently* for a split second before snapping back.  
   - Long rests, delays, or indecision cause “reality skips” — they lose brief moments of time.

3. **Initiate the Nightmare Transition**
   - The barrier between realms is breaking; the players can:
     - Try to flee or camp → reflections overtake them during the night (automatic pull into Nightmare).  
     - Investigate the fountain or well → crossing becomes voluntary or semi-voluntary.  
   - The reflection mechanic becomes more aggressive but *not yet hostile* — they are being herded.

4. **Foreshadow the Dungeon Entry**
   - Establish that the **well near the tower** resonates with the same hum as the chapel’s fragment.  
   - Whispering grows louder: “He waits below the dream.”  
   - When the players are ready (or when night fully falls), the reflections leap into the well’s mirrored surface, beckoning them to follow.  
   - Whether they choose or are pulled depends on pacing — you can end here or open with the mini-dungeon intro.

## Session Structure Outline

### 1. Opening Scene – “The Still Night”
> The air feels thicker now. The usual marsh winds have died.  
> Not a single villager moves.  
> In the fountain, the black water swirls counter to its own reflection, forming a perfect circle.  

- If the players interact: the reflection of the moon breaks into four fragments (matching the number of remaining runic anchors).  
- Allow a few roleplay beats — paranoia, theories, emotional moments.

---

### 2. Optional Scene – The Hunter’s Hut
- The hut’s interior smells of mildew and faint smoke.  
- A half-written report lies under the bed, confirming the scout followed “the light of the well” before losing contact.  
- Under a trapdoor: claw marks and damp earth lead outward toward the tower.  
- Adds closure to the material investigation and transitions focus to the *source*.

---

### 3. Escalation – Reflections Overtake
- First mechanical sign: when one PC blinks, another’s reflection moves instead.  
- NPCs’ voices overlap with those of the players.  
- The world begins *looping* slightly — footsteps repeating, dialogue echoing.  
- Whispers: *“It’s already tomorrow.”*  

If they attempt a rest, they dream of themselves walking underwater toward a light — partial entry into the Nightmare.  

---

### 4. Transition Setup – The Well Awakens
- As they approach the Ruined Tower, the well is now full of **black mirror-water**, humming faintly.  
- Arcana or Religion DC 14 → recognize the same signature as the chapel and fountain.  
- A faint reflection of each PC stares up from the water, perfectly still.  
- Touching the water causes ripples to move *upward* rather than out.  

**Possible outcomes:**
- They choose to jump: reflections reach up, hands merging with theirs.  
- They hesitate: night fully falls, their doubles re-emerge and pull them down.  
- Either way → next session begins the **mini-dungeon in the Nightmare Realm**.

---

## Themes & Tone
- Reality erosion: repetition, reflection, and déjà vu.  
- Heightened quiet — use silence between scenes as tension.  
- Offer illusion of control — players can “decide when” to cross, but the *how* is inevitable.

---

## Key Reminders
- This session should *feel like the descent before the plunge.*  
- Keep encounters minimal; the tension is psychological and atmospheric.  
- Emphasize that every clue now converges: chapel runes, storehouse marks, cipher, and fountain all point *below*.  
- End on imagery that mirrors the Dreamer’s motif: **reflections, silence, descent**.
