# Void Magic

Void Magic is the cosmic counterpart to Radiant Magic —  
**entropy against order, unmaking against creation, silence against harmony.**

It is one of the primordial forces that shaped existence before the planes settled.  
The Void does not corrupt because it is “evil”;  
it corrupts because **its nature is antithetical to meaning, identity, and stability**.

---

## The Void vs. Radiance

### **Radiant / Light**  
- Brings structure  
- Defines form  
- Imposes order  
- Grants clarity  
- Creates truth  

### **Void**  
- Unravels structure  
- Dissolves form  
- Undermines order  
- Obscures clarity  
- Rewrites truth  

Radiant magic creates **what is**.  
Void magic creates **what could be, by consuming what already is.**

The two cannot coexist in pure form.

This duality has been known to planar scholars for centuries —  
but only as **cosmological theory**, like studying black holes or divine power from afar.

---

# Void as a Cosmic Force (Known to Scholars)

The **concept** of the Void is well-known in planar philosophy:

- It is associated with the **Far Planes**, the **Outer Darkness**, the **deepest Shadowfell**, and regions “outside” mortal cosmology.
- It is the origin of entities that do not obey physical or magical laws.
- It is older than gods and predates mortal language.

**But Void Magic is not studied. Not understood. Not practiced.**  
It is believed to be impossible for mortals to wield.

The Royal Arcanum’s stance:
> “Void is theory. Radiance is practice. One shapes the world; the other consumes it.”

That stance is outdated.

---

# What the Arcanum *Does Not Know*

### 1. Void Magic **can** be wielded  
—but only by:
- aberrant beings  
- certain Shadowfell entities  
- planar horrors  
- extremely gifted (or unlucky) mortals  
- or conduits created through unknowable means

### 2. The Shadowfell is a “thin place”  
Void influence bleeds through more easily there, warping:
- memory  
- emotion  
- identity  
- perception  
- magic stability  
- even the boundary between life and death

### 3. A mortal studying Void too closely risks:  
- losing sense of self  
- hearing echoes not their own  
- perceiving contradictory truths  
- merging with something “beyond the veil”  
- attracting entities that whisper back

### 4. Radiant magic reacts to Void  
Like two chemicals that explode on contact:
- Radiant wards flicker  
- Healing misfires  
- Light sources distort  
- Holy symbols fail  
- Divinations return static  

Void is not inherently “evil,”  
but mortals interpret anything uncontrollable or incomprehensible as malevolent.

---

# Controlled vs. Uncontrolled Void Magic

### **Controlled (Extremely Rare)**  
Requires:
- immense intellect  
- emotional isolation  
- iron discipline  
- a mind able to hold multiple contradictory truths simultaneously

Controlled Void Magic can:
- unravel illusions instantly  
- silence magical effects  
- sever the link between body/soul  
- warp perceptions  
- create zones of null-magic  
- rewrite local reality  
- manipulate fate-like threads  
- consume other spells as fuel

Few beings in history have managed this.

Verrath is… inching toward it. Dangerous.

### **Uncontrolled (Common in Shadowfell leaks)**  
What Duskwatch experienced.

Symptoms:
- reflected doubles  
- memory loss  
- corrupted dreams  
- parasitic illusions  
- warped identities  
- sound and time distortions  
- anchor runes  
- color drain  
- psychic echoes

These effects are **not** the Void itself —  
but *what happens when Void influence leaks into a mortal domain.*

The Hollow Dreamer did not *cast* Void Magic.  
It was a **fissure**, not a wielder.

---

# Why No One Practices Void Magic

- The Arcanum suppresses all research.  
- Religious institutions consider it a threat to creation itself.  
- Even necromancers avoid it — it does not bend life; it erases it.  
- Mortals who study it too closely either vanish, go mad, or awaken something watching them back.

Most simply believe:

> “Void spells are impossibly dangerous and categorically uncastable.”

They are wrong.

# Who Currently Understands It?

### **1. Verrath (first hints)**
He has:
- early field notes  
- fragments of theory  
- one or two experimental runes  
- a conceptual grasp of its existence  
- absolutely no mastery  

His research is dangerous because it is:
- brilliant  
- quiet  
- and *almost correct*

### **2. Nagash (later discovery)**
Not a wielder of Void,  
but someone who recognizes power when he senses it.

### **3. Certain Shadowfell entities**
They do not “cast” Void Magic —  
they **are shaped by it.**

### **4. The players (eventually)**
If you choose for one or more to become Void-touched through their arcs.

# What PCs Might Learn in Act I–II

Slow trickle of knowledge:
- Void is the opposite of Radiant magic.  
- The Shadowfell leaks Void influence.  
- Something (or someone) in Velaris is researching it.  
- Duskwatch was a *small leak*, not the true force.  
- The Crown knows nothing.  
- The Arcanum suppresses mention of it.  
- Some runes match Void theories.  
- Someone in the Arcanum broke protocol and studied it anyway.  
- Verrath’s notes hint at a pattern.  

When the Blackening occurs,  
the PCs will realize Void Magic is no longer theory.

It is **here**.
