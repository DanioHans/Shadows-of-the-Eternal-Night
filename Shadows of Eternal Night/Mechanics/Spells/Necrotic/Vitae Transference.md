# Spell: Vitae Transference
*3rd-level necromancy*  
**Casting Time:** 1 action  
**Range:** 30 ft  
**Components:** V, S  
**Duration:** Concentration, up to 1 minute  

### Description
You channel the life of one creature to mend another.  
Choose two creatures within range (including yourself). One becomes the **donor**, the other the **recipient**.  
The donor takes **3d8 necrotic damage** that ignores resistance, and the recipient regains hit points equal to the damage dealt.  

If the donor is unwilling, it makes a Constitution saving throw; on a success, the spell fails.  

### At Higher Levels
When cast using a slot of 4th level or higher, increase both the damage and healing by 1d8 for each slot level above 3rd.

### Side Effect
Each time this spell is cast, roll a d20:
- **1–2:** You see flickering black veins crawl under your skin for 1 minute.  
- **3–20:** No visible effect, though your reflection lags half a second behind for an hour.

### Lore
Originally a **forbidden Caer Hollow transmutation-necromancy hybrid**, Vitae Transference was banned after several apprentices reversed its flow during testing.  
**Duskwatch Tie-In:** Samson could uncover the formula hidden in the **Ruined Watchtower** journal of *Salen Kett*—its margins filled with Arcanum shorthand implying Academy oversight of the experiments.
