
**Royal Arcanum of Velaris — Scholarly Circulation**

---

## 🜁 Description

A slim but dense academic volume, bound in dark blue leather and stamped with the sigil of the **Royal Arcanum of Velaris**.

The writing is formal, precise, and notably restrained.  
No speculation, no prophecy — only classification and observed theory.

---

## 🜁 Core Thesis

Most arcane practitioners draw from **a single, dominant source** of magic.

Whether learned, inherited, or bestowed, the magic expresses itself through:

- One planar influence
    
- One conceptual framework
    
- One internal logic
    

This singularity provides **stability**, predictability, and coherence.

---

## 🜁 On Composite Sources

A central chapter discusses **rare exceptions**: individuals whose magic cannot be traced to a single origin.

These _composite manifestations_ are described as:

- Uncommon, but not unheard of
    
- Often misclassified as instability or corruption
    
- More accurately understood as **overlapping inheritances or influences**
    

The text is careful here:

> “Multiple sources do not imply conflict.  
> They imply history.”

---

## 🜁 Common Misinterpretations

The book explicitly rejects several popular assumptions:

- That multiple sources indicate divine punishment
    
- That such casters are inherently dangerous
    
- That one source must eventually suppress the others
    

Instead, it suggests:

> “Arcane systems are not exclusive unless forced to be.”

---

## 🜁 Long-Term Implications

The final section notes that composite magic tends to:

- Express differently under stress
    
- Adapt rather than fracture
    
- Reveal hidden structure over time
    

It concludes with a restrained warning:

> “Where multiple frameworks coexist, new forms may emerge —  
> not through addition, but interaction.”

No examples are provided.

---

## 🜁 Marginal Note (Optional, Unattributed)

In a different hand, beside the final passage:

> Complexity is not weakness.”

No name.

---

## 🜁 Narrative Function

This book exists to:

- Legitimize dual or evolving magic **academically**
    
- Support Kyron’s background without resolving it
    
- Establish precedent for _non-singular magic_ before Void influence appears
    
- Make future transformations feel **understood**, not sudden
    

It is **theoretical groundwork**, not revelation.
