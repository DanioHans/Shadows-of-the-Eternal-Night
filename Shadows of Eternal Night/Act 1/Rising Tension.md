# Act I – Phase 1: Rising Tension

**Level Range**: 4  
**Tone**: Unease, investigation, growing doubt, slow unraveling.

---

## Summary

After uniting in [[Caer Hollow]], the party is sent to investigate increasing disturbances in the borderlands — notably in [[Greylake]] and the [[Weeping Fields]]. These locations present isolated supernatural events that gradually hint at something deeper and more coordinated. 

This phase is designed to establish tone, provide small moral and investigative victories, and spark questions the players will carry into later acts.

---

## Player Goals

- Resolve supernatural incidents affecting Velaris’ frontier.
- Identify inconsistencies in the kingdom’s official narrative.
- Observe magical and divine instability first-hand.
- Return to [[Caer Hollow]] with a deeper sense of unease.

---

## Narrative Function

- Set emotional contrast before the Blackening.
- Offer meaningful but local-scale agency.
- Tease the existence of **unknown magic** without giving it a name.
- Establish the kingdom’s institutions as fragile or corrupted.

---

## Key Locations & Regional Goals

### [[Greylake]]
- **Problem**: Burials are being disrupted. The ground resists the dead — bodies are unearthed by unseen forces.
- **Player Goal**: Assist with a ritual of re-consecration, discover a corrupted ley marker beneath the town’s chapel.
- **Tease**: One local priest suffers seizures and speaks in mirrored sentences — signs of planar bleed or memory inversion.

### [[The Weeping Fields]]
- **Problem**: Visitors and caretakers report vanishing grave markers, and a cold fog clings to the grass day and night.
- **Player Goal**: Investigate recent pilgrim disappearances, uncover a memory tomb being disturbed by scavengers or rogue mages.
- **Tease**: Signs of an **unfamiliar magic discipline** at play — runes and symbols that don’t match known schools of arcana.

### [[Temple of Dawn]]
- **Problem**: The high priest is missing, and the temple's sanctified wing has been magically sealed by its own relics.
- **Player Goal**: Help decipher the seals, locate the missing priest (or their remnants), and restore access.
- **Tease**: Restoration magic subtly warps, causing minor changes in visual memory (e.g., players recall events differently).

---

## Major NPCs

- **Brother Halven** – Elder monk at the Temple of Dawn. Nervous but insightful.  
  - Can sense shadow corruption but cannot name it.
  - Offers spiritual insight and emotional framing.

- **Archivist Renna Virel** – Scholar from Caer Hollow investigating pre-Velarian tomb structures.  
  - Documents unfamiliar runes; suspects a **forgotten magical theory** was buried after past wars.

- **Captain Dervan Thorne** – A garrison leader growing increasingly unstable.  
  - Swings between rage and lethargy.  
  - Carries orders from the capital that contradict his memories of them.

---

## Important Themes Introduced

- **Magical Decay & Instability** – Early signs of disruption, unexplained failures, or reactivity in spells.
- **Loss of Divine Contact** – Not total, but uneven; prayers feel more personal, less empowered.
- **Hints of a Forbidden Discipline** – A strange school of magic emerges subtly in symbols, effects, and corrupted relics.

---

## Optional Threads

- A funerary relic may “sing” at night — audible only to one party member.
- Shadowed wildlife may watch but not attack, seeming unnaturally restrained.
- Players may find a partially-burned **expulsion decree** from a long-closed magical academy in the Weeping Fields.

---

## Transition

Upon completing this phase, players:
- Return to [[Caer Hollow]] with conflicting reports and growing doubt.
- Are granted an audience with the king and [[Verrath]], unaware of the trap being prepared.
- Prepare for Phase 2 — investigation, foreshadowing, and the Blackening.

(Level up to 5 after resolving both major field regions.)
