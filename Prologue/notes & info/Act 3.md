### Dungeon entrance puzzle

**Puzzle Description:**

- **Entrance:** The entrance to the ruins is a large stone door with an intricate, circular pattern and several symbols arranged in a ring. The door also features four rotating rings, each with a different set of symbols. The objective is to align the rings to form the correct pattern.

**Symbols and Clues:**

- The symbols on the door represent four ancient creatures: Owl, Serpent, Stag, and Raven. The correct sequence for the symbols can be referenced subtly throughout the adventure.

**Phrase on the Door:**

- "The key to entry lies in the past; align the symbols and gain access fast."

**References Throughout the Story:**

1. **Elara's Storytelling:**
    
    - While in the village, [[Elara]] tells a story about an ancient ritual that involved these creatures: "When the moon was high and the stars aligned, the owl stood vigilant, the serpent coiled, the stag stood proud, and the raven watched."
2. **Tavern Rumors:**
    
    - In the tavern, a villager mentions an old tale: "They say the ancients revered the owl for wisdom, the serpent for cunning, the stag for strength, and the raven for foresight."
3. **Elder's House Clues:**
    
    - In the elder’s house, players find a tapestry depicting the four creatures in the correct order: Owl, Serpent, Stag, Raven.
4. **Graveyard Symbols:**
    
    - In the graveyard, some tombstones are marked with the symbols of the owl, serpent, stag, and raven in that order, indicating the significance of these creatures.
5. **Missing Villager's Home:**
    
    - In the missing villager's home, among the strange symbols, players can find a rough drawing of the four creatures in the correct sequence.

**Puzzle Solution:**

- **Steps to Solve:**
    1. Align the rings to form the sequence: Owl, Serpent, Stag, Raven.
    2. Players need to figure out the correct order based on the subtle hints provided throughout their investigation.

**Clue from Ambush:**

- If the party successfully detected and defeated the ambush, they find a parchment with the portion of the ancient tale, providing a clear hint:
    - "When the moon was high and the stars aligned, the door to the ancients' secrets was revealed. The owl stood vigilant, the serpent coiled, the stag stood proud, and the raven watched."

#### 2. Exploring the Dungeon

**Dungeon Layout:**

- The dungeon is a series of interconnected rooms and corridors, partially collapsed and overgrown with roots and vines. Dim light from bioluminescent fungi provides a faint glow.

**Key Areas:**

1. **Entrance Hall:**
    
    - **Description:** A large hall with several statues of ancient warriors, some of which are missing limbs or heads. Cobwebs hang from the ceiling, and a thick layer of dust covers the floor.
    - **Encounter:** Several [[Skeleton|Skeleton Swordsman]] and [[Skeleton|Skeleton Bowman]] sit motionless along the walls. Some appear to be villagers, recognizable by their clothing.
2. **Hall of the Dead:**
    
    - **Description:** A long corridor lined with alcoves, each containing a skeleton or zombie lying in repose.
    - **Decision Point:** Players must decide whether to kill the undead or leave them be. Some undead may be disguised as missing villagers, adding to the moral dilemma.
    - **Mechanics:** If disturbed, roll a d6:
        - 1-3: The undead remain inert.
        - 4-6: The undead rise and attack (2 [[Skeleton|Skeleton Swordsman]] or 1 [[Zombie Child]]).
3. **Secret Passage:**
    
    - **Description:** A hidden passage behind a loose stone in one of the corridors. Requires a DC 15 Perception check to find.
    - **Reward:** Inside, the players find a small cache of ancient gold coins and a magical item (e.g., a +1 weapon or a healing potion).
4. **The Library:**
    
    - **Description:** A room filled with old, decaying books and scrolls. The air is thick with the smell of mildew and rot.
    - **Clues and Lore:** Players can find clues about the necromancer's plans and the history of the ruins. An Intelligence (Investigation) check (DC 12) reveals:
        - The necromancer's journal detailing experiments with necromancy.
        - A map of the dungeon with marked areas of interest.
5. **The Shrine:**
    
    - **Description:** An ancient shrine with a stone altar and offerings from a bygone era. The air feels heavy with residual magic.
    - **Reward:** If the players perform a respectful ritual (e.g., offering a prayer or a token), they receive a blessing, granting temporary hit points or an inspiration die.
6. **The Crypt:**
    
    - **Description:** A small chamber with sealed sarcophagi. One sarcophagus has been recently disturbed.
    - **Decision Point:** Investigating the disturbed sarcophagus reveals a missing villager turned undead. Players can choose to lay them to rest or fight.

#### 3. The Final Chamber

**Description:**

- The final chamber is a large, circular room with a raised dais in the center. The air is thick with the stench of decay, and the walls are lined with ancient runes pulsing with dark energy.

**Boss Encounter: The Necromancer**

- **Necromancer:** The main antagonist, a powerful necromancer surrounded by dark energy and commanding several undead minions.
- **Minions:** 2 [[Skeleton Guard]] and 2 [[Zombie Brute]].

**Combat Encounter:**

- **Objective:** Defeat the necromancer and his minions.
- **Tactics:** The necromancer uses spells to control the battlefield and raise fallen minions. The minions protect the necromancer, focusing on the adventurers.

**Resolution:**

- After defeating the necromancer, the players find a hidden journal or artifact revealing more about the larger threat looming over the land (hinting at [[Nagash]]).

**Rewards:**

- **Loot:** Magical items, gold, and a key artifact related to the overarching plot.
- **Experience:** Significant XP for defeating the necromancer and successfully navigating the dungeon.
