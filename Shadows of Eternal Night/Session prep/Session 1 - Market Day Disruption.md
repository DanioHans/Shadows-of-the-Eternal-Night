### Purpose
Unify the PCs naturally in a first combat encounter.  
Introduce subtle signs of corruption from the **Verdant Wastes**.  
Begin building Crownwatch interest in the players.  
Lead into the start of the Act I investigation arc.

---

## 🏙️ Scene Setup — Getting Players Together

### Setting: Market Day in [[Caer Hollow]]

- A major **seasonal market** fills the streets.
- Merchants, farmers, nobles' servants, and wandering scholars fill the square and nearby streets.
- Guards are present but relaxed — it's a celebrated day.

### How to Guide Players In:

| Character                | Hook                                                                                                                             |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **[[Samson]]**           | Sent to the market by an Academy mentor for a rare reagent or scroll.                                                            |
| **[[Merios]]**           | Accompanies the farmers delivering produce to the market — directly involved in stalls.                                          |
| **[[Warforged Ranger]]** | Drawn by curiosity — **market day is exciting** for a newly conscious being.                                                     |
| **[[Ivor]]**             | Meeting a contact in the crowd (set up or improv; a smuggler or informant).                                                      |
| **[[Kyron]]**            | After a failed Academy meeting, advised to "learn about the city" — wanders into the market, possibly alongside or after Samson. |
| **6th player (TBD)**     | Easy to place — anyone can have reason to be at market day.                                                                      |

You do not need to force them to meet first — let **proximity + combat** do the work.

## 🐍 Trigger — Releasing the Basilisk

### Background

- A **beast smuggler** has been trying to profit from **Verdant Wastes creatures** — selling "rare exotics" at the market.
- He does not realize the **arcane instability** in these creatures.
- His cage of **juvenile basilisk** (already tainted and nervous) is poorly warded.

### Trigger Event

- Market day heats up; the beast smuggler's stall draws attention.
- Minor **magical surge** (you can flavor this as a leyline pulse or Forgotten Magic ripple — a nice teaser).
- Cage snaps open.  
- Basilisk escapes — **panic ensues**.

Optional: Have 1–2 **minor exotic creatures** (CR 1/4–1/2) also escape — useful for:
- Giving non-frontline PCs something to do early in the fight.
- Making the scene feel more chaotic.

Example filler creatures:
- **Giant Centipede (Verdant variant)** — fast and scary.
- **Giant Weasel** — more of a distraction.
- **Swarm of Ravens** — covering the square in chaos.

## 🗺️ Combat Flow Suggestions

1️⃣ **Start of Fight**
- Market square full of panicking civilians.
- Players either near the square or pulled in by crowd screams.

2️⃣ **Round 1–2**
- Minor creatures harass civilians / block sight lines.
- Basilisk stalks forward — **you can make it visibly "off"** (arcane veins, twitching, unnatural eyes).

3️⃣ **Mid-Fight**
- Some **NPC guards flee or freeze** — the players are the ones taking action.
- Allow players to **protect civilians, engage tactically**.

4️⃣ **End of Fight**
- Crownwatch finally regroups and arrives.
- PCs find themselves the most competent people present.
- Crownwatch captain or sergeant notices their effectiveness.

---

## 🎖️ Aftermath — Tying to Crownwatch Bastion

- Once the fight ends, the **players are clearly noticed**.
- **Crownwatch officers** approach:
  - "You lot — with us. You’re going to explain yourselves."
  - Not hostile — they need witnesses.
  - Players who resist are asked more firmly (don’t force them; play it socially).

- Crownwatch leads the group to the **Bastion**:
  - Players are asked about the incident.
  - PCs may learn:
    - This isn’t the first strange market disruption.
    - Some guards suspect **Academy involvement** (not open knowledge yet).
    - The escaped creatures came from **the Verdant Wastes trade**.

- At the Bastion, **Captain Teron Veyne** (create him as an open-minded officer) suggests:
  - "I don’t know what you lot stumbled into, but you handled yourselves well."
  - "There are whispers of worse things. If you hear or see anything... bring it to me."

This gives you your **party hook**:
- They now know something strange is happening.
- They have **Crownwatch recognition** and permission to act unofficially.

---

## 🎭 RP / Tone Tips

- Let the players choose how much they engage. Some may want to chase minor creatures; others may want to protect civilians; some will charge the basilisk.
- Make the **crowd dynamic**:
  - NPCs shout and scatter.
  - Some market stalls catch fire or collapse.
  - Panicked guards / merchants add noise and confusion.

- The **Bastion invitation** should feel like a **reward**:
  - "You’re capable. We may need more eyes on the streets."
  - Not a threat.
