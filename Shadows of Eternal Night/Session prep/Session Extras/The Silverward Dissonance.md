## Overview

A herbalist and reagent supplier affiliated with the **Verdant Cloister** in the Silverward approaches the party after her stock begins dying overnight. Her magical plants are wilting, her enchanted reagents are discharging spontaneously, and one of her neighbours lost three fingers when a stored alchemical component exploded. She needs the problem solved before her entire livelihood is destroyed.

The cause is not targeted or malicious — it is collateral. Verrath's arcane construction beneath the city has elevated the ambient magical frequency across several districts, and the Silverward's dense concentration of old religious wards, temple enchantments, and decades of layered shrine magic has begun resonating badly. The herbalist's shop sits at the unlucky convergence point where multiple ward frequencies overlap and amplify each other. Left unchecked, the resonance will discharge catastrophically within days.

The solution requires the party to identify which enchanted locations in the surrounding block are contributing to the resonance, understand the direction the energy chain flows, and use emergency scrolls from the Arcanum to rebalance the wards in the correct sequence — starting from the source end of the chain, not the middle.

**Party Level:** 5 **Estimated Duration:** 2–3 hours **Location:** The Silverward district, Caer Hollow **Quest Type:** NPC Approaches **Tone:** Social investigation with a logical puzzle finale. Low combat, high roleplay.

---

## Hook

The party is approached by a tired-looking woman in herbalist's robes — practical, stained with soil and reagent dye, a small silver leaf-pin marking her as affiliated with the **Verdant Cloister**. She asks around until she finds the group, having heard through Cloister contacts that they handled the Duskwatch strangeness.

Her name is **Maret Soln**. She is not panicked but she is close to it, which she hides behind precise, clipped speech.

> _"I don't have coin for a mage and I don't trust the Arcanum to care about a single herbalist's shop. But I have a real problem and I've heard you solve real problems. My stock is dying. My neighbour nearly lost his hand. And whatever is causing it is getting worse every day. I need someone to find the source and stop it before I lose everything I've spent twelve years building."_

She offers **80 gold** for a resolution. She cannot go higher but will throw in a selection of rare reagents worth approximately 40 gold in addition if the party resolves it fully.

---

## Maret Soln – The Herbalist

A pragmatic, quietly proud woman in her late thirties. She has worked with the Verdant Cloister for over a decade, supplying the temple healers, the Arcanum's reagent stores, and several private clients with high-quality magical herbs and alchemical components.

**Personality:** Direct, unsentimental, deeply competent in her field. Not warm on first meeting but not unkind — she simply doesn't have patience for vagueness. Responds very well to people who take her problem seriously and ask smart questions.

**What she tells the party freely:**

- Three weeks ago her more magically sensitive plants began wilting despite perfect care.
- Two weeks ago her enchanted reagents began slowly discharging — crystalline components losing their charge overnight, infused oils separating.
- One week ago her neighbour Eddo, a retired enchanter two doors down, had a stored ward-stone explode while he was examining it. He survived but lost the ring finger on his left hand.
- The problem is getting worse at a consistent rate. If the pattern holds she expects a significant discharge event within three to four days.

**When asked about the ward chain — the critical early conversation:**

If the party asks about the surrounding enchantments or how to approach fixing it, Maret provides the key framing the players need before they have scrolls in hand:

> _"My ward-priest explained this once. These old consecrations talk to each other — they've been neighbours for decades and they've settled into a rhythm. If something disrupts one of them, the energy doesn't vanish, it pushes into the next one in the chain. Pull one out of sequence and the ones behind it surge forward with nowhere to go. That's probably what happened to Eddo — his ward was in the middle of something and it couldn't hold."_

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** This is the puzzle's foundational rule delivered naturally in conversation. Let the players absorb it before they have scrolls. The chain must be unwound from the source end — they need to find where the chain starts before they start using scrolls. If they miss this conversation, Sister Calowe at the Chapel will echo it more bluntly when they arrive there.

## The Arcanum Scrolls

Before the party can fix anything, they need the tools to fix it. Maret knows the Arcanum maintains a small stock of emergency **Ward Restoration Scrolls** for exactly this kind of infrastructure maintenance — old wards degrading, misaligned consecrations, post-incident magical cleanup.

Getting them requires a visit to the **Royal Arcanum** and either:

- Samson leveraging his student status — automatic success, but takes one hour and involves mild bureaucratic indignity. A junior clerk makes him sign three separate forms, questions why a student needs infrastructure scrolls, and eventually refers him to a supervisor who approves it with obvious reluctance.
- A DC 14 Persuasion check from any other party member presenting Maret's situation clearly.
- Paying a refundable deposit of 30 gold.

The party receives **five scrolls** — one for each contributing ward, with no buffer. If they use one on a red herring ward they will need to return for a replacement, which costs time and 15 gold per scroll.

## The Investigation — Finding and Reading the Chain

The party needs to visit the locations on the block, identify which wards are part of the resonance chain, and determine the direction the chain flows. Examining a ward at any location requires a **DC 12 Arcana check**. On a success, the character can feel whether the ward is straining inward (receiving energy from elsewhere) or pushing outward (feeding into something downstream). This is how the players map the chain's direction.

**The chain flows as follows:** Something beneath the Silverward — deep, subterranean, and not visible — is feeding excess energy upward into the district. The **Moonmaiden's Chapel** sits directly above this source and is drawing from it most intensely. From there the energy bleeds outward:

```
[Subterranean Source]
        ↓
[Moonmaiden's Chapel]  →  [Vayne Ancestral Home]  →  [Eddo's Ward-Lattice]  →  [Verdant Cloister Annex]  →  [Maret's Shop ✗]
                                                                                          ↑
                                                              [Woss's Stall ✗]  [Bell Path Chimes ✗]
                                                              (bystanders — react to chain but not part of it)
```

The three red herring locations sit adjacent to the chain and vibrate sympathetically but do not feed into it. A successful Arcana check at these locations reveals the ward feels reactive rather than directional — it is resonating in response to something nearby, not pushing energy anywhere.

The correct sequence to use the scrolls is therefore:

1. **Moonmaiden's Chapel** — the source end, drawing directly from below
2. **Vayne Ancestral Home** — releases stored overflow once Chapel is rebalanced
3. **Eddo's Ward-Lattice** — stabilises once the pressure from above is cleared
4. **Verdant Cloister Annex** — last, as it is projecting toward Maret and must be rebalanced after everything feeding it has been addressed

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** If the players start at the Annex or Eddo and try to rebalance there first, do not let them succeed silently. Describe the scroll activating and then the ward surging as energy floods in from upstream — the scroll burns out, the ward spikes, and windows rattle across the block. They lose the scroll and understand immediately why Maret's warning mattered. One failed attempt is a good lesson. Two is probably enough.

## The Seven Locations

### 1. Maret's Shop _(starting point — not part of chain)_

Maret's shop is the epicentre of the effect but has no significant enchantment of its own. She is the victim, not a source. Examining her shop's walls confirms that the resonance is entering from the east — toward the Cloister Annex — and concentrating here.

---

### 2. The Verdant Cloister Annex _(chain position 4 — last)_

A small storage and preparation building belonging to the Cloister temple. An old **growth blessing** worked into the foundation stone has been pushing excess energy westward toward Maret's shop for weeks.

**Resident: Brother Oswen** — a junior priest, young, cooperative in principle but not authorised to give permission for anyone to modify a Cloister consecration. He must fetch **Senior Warden Aldric**, who is in the middle of afternoon prayers and will not be interrupted. The party either waits 20 minutes, or a DC 14 Persuasion check convinces Oswen to go interrupt prayers himself. Aldric, once retrieved, is businesslike and gives consent after a brief examination of the scroll — he recognises Arcanum infrastructure scrolls and trusts them.

**Arcana check result:** The ward is pushing outward strongly toward the west. It is clearly downstream of something — strained but not the origin point.

---

### 3. Eddo's Ward-Lattice _(chain position 3)_

Eddo is the neighbour who lost his finger. His home has a **personal ward-lattice** he installed himself over twenty years — layered personal protection that is technically impressive but not professionally maintained. It has been absorbing and amplifying the resonance coming from the Vayne house before pushing it further toward the Annex.

**Resident: Eddo** — mid-sixties, formerly skilled enchanter, now retired and very angry. He is bandaged, short-tempered, and will spend the first few minutes blaming the party for being slow to arrive, blaming the Arcanum for sending students, and blaming the city generally. He gives permission eventually but insists on supervising Samson's scroll use personally and will interrupt with corrections at the worst moment.

**DC 13 Persuasion or DC 12 Intimidation** to get him to step back far enough that the scroll can work cleanly. If he keeps interfering, the rebalancing is functional but imperfect — Eddo's ward stabilises at partial effectiveness, which is enough to break the chain but means his personal protection is weakened until a proper enchanter services it. He will be grumpy about this.

**Arcana check result:** The ward is receiving heavy pressure from the southeast (the Vayne house) and pushing it northwest (toward the Annex). Clearly a midpoint in the chain. Using a scroll here first would cause the upstream energy to surge with nowhere to go.

---

### 4. The Moonmaiden's Rise — Side Chapel _(chain position 1 — start here)_

A small side chapel of the Selûne temple used for private prayer. The chapel has a **moonlight consecration** worked into the arched window — a living enchantment over a century old that has been drawing intensely from the subterranean source below.

> 💡 **Merios Note:** This is Merios' deity's temple. Gawain Brightlance trained here. Sister Calowe may recognise Gawain's name, creating a brief personal moment for Merios. If Merios presents herself as a Selûnite paladin, the entire conversation softens noticeably.

**Resident: Sister Calowe** — a senior priestess, gentle but perceptive and deeply protective of the chapel. She will not allow anyone near the moonlight consecration without a thorough understanding of what they intend. She requires a full explanation of the chain and the scroll's function. DC 13 Persuasion or Religion check — reduced to DC 9 if Merios presents her paladin identity.

If the party has not yet heard the chain explanation from Maret, Sister Calowe will provide it herself, more bluntly:

> _"You cannot simply reach into the middle of a chain and pull. Whatever is feeding this ward must be addressed — but this ward must be rebalanced first, or everything it is feeding into will surge when you cut the flow from below."_

She also provides the most important campaign-relevant observation, almost as an aside:

> _"The consecration has been singing for nearly a month. Not wrongly — just louder. As if something below has given it far more to work with than it has ever had before."_

**Arcana check result:** The ward is drawing intensely from directly below and pushing that energy outward in a clear direction toward the Vayne house. This is unmistakably the source end of the chain. The subterranean pull is strong enough that a DC 14 Arcana check can confirm it is not natural ley activity — it is concentrated, directed, and recent.

---

### 5. The Ancestral Home — The Vayne Family Residence _(chain position 2)_

A well-maintained townhouse belonging to a minor noble family who have lived in the Silverward for four generations. The house carries an **ancestral ward** — a family protection blessing renewed each generation — that has been storing and redirecting excess resonance from the Chapel toward Eddo's lattice.

**Resident: Lady Seris Vayne** — fifties, immaculate, proud of her family's heritage and immediately suspicious of strangers at her door asking about her ancestral ward. She does not believe there is a problem and does not appreciate the implication that her family's blessing is harming anyone. She will call for her manservant if the conversation goes badly.

This is the most socially difficult interaction on the block:

- **DC 16 Persuasion** appealing to civic responsibility and the Silverward's safety
- **DC 13 Deception** framing it as an Arcanum routine inspection
- **Merios presenting as a Selûnite paladin** — Lady Vayne is a quiet Selûne devotee, reducing the DC to 11
- **Queek saying anything** — Lady Vayne calls for her manservant immediately and the party must try a different approach without him present

If she warms to the party she mentions, with some pride:

> _"My ward-priest noted last year that the family blessing had grown unusually potent. He seemed quite impressed. I suppose now I understand that was not the compliment I thought it was."_

**Arcana check result:** The ward is visibly swollen with stored energy — it has been passively accumulating overflow from the Chapel for weeks. It is clearly downstream of the Chapel but upstream of Eddo. Rebalancing this before the Chapel would cause the stored energy to discharge explosively.

---

### 6. Woss's Spice Stall _(red herring — not part of chain)_

A covered stall run by a travelling merchant who rents the space year-round. The stall's awning has a minor **preservation enchantment** keeping spices dry and fresh. Woss is present and immediately cooperative — almost too eager to let them examine anything.

**Arcana check result:** The ward feels reactive rather than directional. It is vibrating in response to the nearby resonance like a bell sympathetically ringing from another bell's note — but it is not pushing energy anywhere. A DC 13 Arcana check confirms it is not part of the chain. Using a scroll here wastes it.

Woss's clue, offered cheerfully:

> _"My charm has been overworking for weeks. Triggers constantly even when nothing needs preserving. Like it's responding to something that isn't there."_

---

### 7. The Bell Path Entrance — Ward Chimes _(red herring — not part of chain)_

The entrance to the Silverward's Bell Path has civic **ward-chimes** above the archway providing a gentle calming aura. They have been ringing at odd hours with no wind for several weeks.

**Resident: Temple Guard Bran** — patrols the path entrance. No arcane knowledge but observant. He is not obstructive but does not want civilians tampering with civic property without a clear reason. DC 11 Persuasion to let them examine the chimes closely. He won't allow scroll use here under any circumstances without Arcanum authorisation, which the party technically has — DC 13 Persuasion to convince him their infrastructure mandate covers civic ward chimes.

**Arcana check result:** Same as Woss's stall — sympathetic resonance, not directional. The chimes are reacting to the chain, not part of it.

Bran's clue, matter-of-factly:

> _"Same three notes, every night, about two hours past midnight. Started a month ago. I've stopped mentioning it in my reports because nobody does anything about it."_

## The Puzzle — Summary for DM

The party should arrive at the puzzle having gathered enough information to know:

- The chain must be unwound from the source end (Maret or Sister Calowe's warning)
- The Chapel is the source end (Arcana check + Sister Calowe's observation about something below)
- The Vayne house is next (Arcana check shows it is downstream of Chapel, upstream of Eddo)
- Eddo is the midpoint (Arcana check confirms two-directional pressure)
- The Annex is last (Arcana check shows it pushing toward Maret with nothing upstream left)
- Woss and the Chimes are bystanders (Arcana checks reveal no directionality)

If the players visit all seven locations they have full confirmation of the sequence. If they visit only four or five they can still piece it together but with less certainty. Let them discuss it — this is the moment where Samson earns his keep.

**Correct sequence:** Chapel → Vayne → Eddo → Annex

## Resolution

When the Annex ward is the last to be rebalanced, the effect is immediate. The persistent low hum nobody had consciously registered until it stopped — goes quiet. Maret's shop stops vibrating. Two surviving plants visibly perk within minutes.

Maret pays the agreed reward with quiet sincerity. Then, almost as an afterthought:

> _"The Arcanum sent a circular to Cloister suppliers last month. Said disruptions were possible due to 'infrastructure assessment activity.' They didn't say where. They didn't say how long. They said not to worry."_

She pauses.

> _"My stock is dead. My neighbour lost a finger. I am a little bit worried."_

## What the Players Learn

Combined with what they found beneath Smokestack Row, the picture is sharpening:

- Something subterranean is feeding excess energy into the city's existing magical infrastructure.
- It is widespread enough to affect both the working-class Smokestack Row and the sacred Silverward.
- The Arcanum is aware of elevated frequencies and has named it _infrastructure assessment activity_ — without explaining what that means or who authorised it.
- Nobody is being told the truth.
