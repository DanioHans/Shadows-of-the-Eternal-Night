1. Session 1 summary: Day 1. The sun rose over Caer Hollow, casting long, golden shadows through the narrow cobblestone streets as the city’s monthly market came alive. Merchants unfurled their awnings, children weaved between stalls, and the smell of fruit, spice, and dust filled the air. Amid the bustle, a new arrival strode with purpose: Samson, a robed scholar of the prestigious Royal Arcanum. Tasked with a simple mission—to retrieve an outdated Planar Dampening Scroll and return it to the school’s archives—Samson had been paid handsomely in advance: 95 gold pieces for what should be an errand of no consequence. Elsewhere, in the dim stillness of a library corner, a wingless tiefling named Kyron pored over musty tomes. The scroll in his hand, outdated like the one Samson sought, drew a stern librarian’s disapproval. “Time to seek your answers in the world,” he urged. And as fate would have it, Samson wandered by at that very moment. The librarian, perhaps guided by more than mere practicality, entrusted Kyron to the reluctant scholar. Meanwhile, at the herbalist's stall, a cloaked figure lingered in the crowd: Ivor, a rogue. His contact passed him a note: “Follow the man. Note what he buys. Do not be seen.” Cryptic, but not unfamiliar. Ivor melted back into the crowd, his eyes sharp. A few stalls over, a mechanical figure stood in innocent awe. Scrap, a curious Warforged, was exploring the concept of fruit with the guidance of a fascinated child. He learned, with great disappointment, that he could not taste. Undeterred, he purchased a spiked fruit for two copper pieces—more for curiosity’s sake than flavor. From a slowly rolling cart at the market’s edge, a tiefling youth fluttered out into the crowd. Merios, wings and all, had been told by his caretaker Silas to explore but return by five. With a sweet tooth and a gleam in his eye, Merios made a beeline to the fruit stalls and treated himself to an armful of exotic delights.
    
2. Back on his mission, Samson questioned a guard for directions to the scroll vendor, tipping a full gold piece for the trouble. When the old, dusty vendor eventually found what might be the correct scroll, Samson—dutiful to a fault—refused to look at it, since he was to retrieve, not read. He paid 30 gold and took the scroll. Kyron was briefly offered a book on tieflings but returned it, longing to go back to the library he had just been ejected from. Ivor continued his pursuit, tailing a carriage into the main square. There, a hunched merchant began setting up his stall—and cages were unloaded, filled with dangerous creatures. One of them hissed. Ivor made a mental note. Suddenly, a sharp crack of magic echoed as Queek, a Skaven fighter, was torn from a battlefield and deposited—confused and frightened—into the market streets. Skittering into an alley, he pressed against the wall, peeking out. He watched, puzzled, as townsfolk fled while the guards ambled about, uninterested in the chaos. Back at the animal stall, Scrap attempted to befriend a caged centipede, debating philosophy, existence, and market pricing. But in doing so, he missed the heavy clunk of a falling lock. A cage opened. A basilisk, scales glinting, burst free.
    
3. What followed was chaos. Scrap pleaded with the centipede to help, but the creature, offended and enraged, refused. With sorrow, Scrap struck it down. As the basilisk rampaged, one by one, the scattered adventurers converged: Samson, Kyron, Ivor, Scrap, Merios, Queek—each drawn by sound, fate, or instinct. In a flurry of blades, magic, and desperate cooperation, the creature fell in less than half a minute. And just as the dust settled, the Crown Watch arrived. Late, of course. Despite their lateness, the guards insisted on an investigation and escorted the group to the Keep. Samson was allowed to complete his mission, lying about the scroll’s cost and pocketing the spare gold without guilt. At the Keep, suspicion faded slightly. The party was allowed dinner and free time in a grand common room until ten, when each was sent to their spartan cells—a bed, a chair, a toilet. The mood was mixed: tension, relief, and a strange camaraderie hung in the air. And so, as the bells tolled the hour and torches dimmed in their sconces, our newfound party settled into uncertain rest—brought together not by design, but by a twist of fate and the wild chaos of Caer Hollow's market day.
