## What Crownwatch Needs (100 gp reward)
To claim the information reward, the party must gather:
1. **Proof of Missing Shipments** — location and cause of stoppage.  
2. **Proof of Missing Scouts** — remains, journals, or other confirmation of fate.  
3. **Proof Village Is Not Functioning Normally** — villagers trapped in trance, not rebellion or desertion.  

Resolving the Nightmare entity earns an additional 200 gp.

## Gull’s Rest Inn

**Location Context:**  
The Gull’s Rest sits just off the main square, once a warm tavern for ferrymen and travelers. The wooden sign—an engraved gull in flight—hangs limp, creaking faintly in the still air. The smell of stale ale and damp wood seeps through the shuttered windows.

---

### Arrival Description
> The door gives under your hand with a reluctant groan. The air inside is heavy—stale beer, wet ash, and something faintly sweet, like spilled cider turned to syrup.  
> Tables are still set for supper. A roast on the counter has gone gray and shriveled, but the plates around it are untouched.  
> A lone barkeep stands behind the bar, polishing a glass in slow, deliberate motions. Every few moments, he croaks out, *“Last call… last call…”* His voice doesn’t quite match the movement of his lips.

The glass he polishes never gets clean—no matter how long he wipes, a faint ring of grime remains. The inn’s hearth burns with *cold flame*, casting light but no warmth.

---

### Atmosphere & Behavior
- The **barkeep** (named *Garin*) is trapped mid-routine. He doesn’t notice new patrons unless directly spoken to.  
- When addressed, he turns slowly, answers questions in **fragmented loops** of dialogue:  
  - “A room? Of course, a room. Upstairs—if the stairs would just… stand still…”  
  - “Last call soon. The ferryman leaves at dawn.” (There’s no ferryman nearby.)  
  - “The gulls don’t land anymore.”  
- If pressed, his eyes glaze white for a moment and his voice deepens to a whisper not his own:  
  > *“Dreams are for the hungry, not the lost.”*  
  Then he resets, starting the glass-polishing again.

---

### Exploration Details
- **Kitchen:** Half-prepared meals, knives rusted mid-cut, herbs petrified into dust. Touching any food item makes it briefly warm, then cold again.  
- **Common Room:**  
  - A tipped-over mug leaks a stream of ale that stops mid-pour halfway down the counter.  
  - The smell of saltwater comes and goes with the ticking of an unseen clock.  
- **Guest Rooms:** Beds look recently slept in but cold to the touch.  
  - One pillow bears a faint indentation of a head still breathing (illusion).  
  - A moth-eaten ledger lists travelers who never existed.  
- **Basement Cellar:** Locked. Arcane or brute entry reveals dozens of sealed ale barrels filled not with ale but *black brackish water*. Each barrel hums softly when approached.

---

### Clues
- **Dream Routine Evidence:** Villagers act without awareness—confirms collective trance.  
- **Rune Trace:** Arcana DC 13 near the hearth reveals faint runic markings etched into the soot along the stone base.  
- **Hidden Item:** Perception DC 15 finds a silvered coin engraved with both a **Crownwatch insignia** and a **small spiral rune**—Academy experiment marker.  
- **Social Insight:** Garin mutters “Ferryman leaves at dawn” repeatedly; investigating docks later reveals no ferryman was ever scheduled.

---

### Checks
| Skill | DC | Result |
|-------|----|--------|
| Arcana | 13 | Detects weak planar signature (illusory projection field). |
| Insight | 14 | Realizes Garin’s speech loops reset at 47-second intervals. |
| Investigation | 15 | Finds secret ledger entry: *“Delivery for Arcanum—test batch moss.”* |
| Religion | 12 | Sense divine silence—no trace of Pelor’s blessing remains on the building. |

---

### Paranoia Cues
- The sound of faint laughter filters through the floorboards every few minutes; no one is below.  
- When a PC approaches the bar mirror, their reflection remains *just a moment too long* after they step away.  
- Occasionally, all the mugs in the room *rattle once* in unison when no one speaks.  
- The phrase “Last call” begins echoing from a distant street after they leave.

---

### Narrative Purpose
- Early confirmation that Duskwatch’s people exist in **dreamlike loops**.  
- Opportunity to reveal the **Crownwatch-Arcanum connection** subtly through the silvered coin or ledger.  
- Provides Samson with clues linking Academy experimentation to planar contamination.  
- Establishes baseline horror tone before the chapel and fountain escalate it.

---

### Hooks & Follow-Ups
- If the players take the ledger, it may later **burn itself to ash** upon entering the Nightmare Realm, leaving behind only one legible phrase: *“He watches from beneath the dream.”*  
- If they break the trance (Dispel Magic, etc.), Garin collapses dead, desiccated instantly, as though drained years ago.  
- Searching the cold hearth after his fall reveals a **cracked focus lens**—the component that teaches **Murmuring Veil** to Samson.

### The Bell Investigation
If the players follow the **bell toll**, it leads them through narrow, overgrown streets toward the east side of town. The sound grows duller with each step—more like an echo remembered than a real chime. Eventually, they see the outline of a small stone chapel, its bell tower cracked and tilting slightly.

**Read-Aloud:**  
> The faint toll draws you down a lane lined with shuttered houses.  
> The bell rings again—soft, distant, and wrong, as if striking underwater.  
> When you finally glimpse the chapel, its tower lists to one side, the bronze bell inside swinging soundlessly. The rope at its base moves on its own, swaying gently back and forth though no wind stirs it.

**Details:**  
- The bell itself is **still swinging**, but produces no physical vibration when touched.  
- Closer inspection reveals **thin black residue** along the inner rim of the bell—identical to the dark silt found in the **Fountain Square**.  
- The clapper is missing, yet the sound continues to echo faintly every few minutes, matching the rhythm of the priest’s prayer loop.  
- Casting *Detect Magic* reveals faint **necrotic resonance**; the sound originates not from the bell but from **below the altar**.  
- If the PCs remain long enough, they hear their own voices layered beneath the toll, repeating something they said earlier in the day.

**Purpose:**  
- Directs investigation to the **Chapel of the Dawnfather** naturally.  
- Foreshadows the **runic bone fragment** and the **Dreamer’s planar resonance** beneath the church.  
- Symbolizes that even the divine has fallen silent—another sign of Duskwatch’s disconnection from reality.

## Nightmare Realm Extensions (200 gp reward path)
These can only be found once the party crosses into the Nightmare version of Duskwatch.

- **Innkeeper (Dream):** “The runes bind us.”  
- **Hunter (Dream):** “The circle in the marsh — that’s where it came from.”  
- **Scouts (Dream):** “We were called by our own voices.”  
- **Market (Dream):** Shadow-doubles whisper secrets of PCs; Hard Wis save (DC 15) reveals: “The Crown shall fall to shadow.”  
- **Stone Circle (Dream):** Hard Arcana/Religion (DC 18) → glimpse of *Nagash’s sigil* entwined with runes. Failing save = 2d6 psychic damage.

## DM Notes
- **Daytime:** Subtle weirdness, surface-level clues only.  
- **Nighttime:** Active dream-bleed, visual illusions, doubles, whispers.  
- **Nightmare Realm:** Truth and confrontation — clues fully connect.
