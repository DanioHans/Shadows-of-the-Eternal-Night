
**Location**: [[Caer Hollow]]  
**District Type**: Mixed residential and commercial quarter  
**Known For**: Bustling markets, diverse crowds, and tightly packed alleys

---

## Overview

Eastveil Commons is one of the liveliest and most traversed districts of [[Caer Hollow]], acting as a **meeting ground** for commoners, traders, traveling merchants, and the occasional oddity. Though not wealthy, the district has a **welcoming and practical charm**, where deals are made, gossip spreads, and livelihoods are earned.

It is home to the sprawling [[Market Square]], numerous street vendors, family-owned shops, and tightly spaced homes with rooftop drying lines and shuttered balconies.

---

## Key Features

### 🏛️ Market Square  
- A **large, irregular plaza** with a central **stone fountain**.
- Hosts the **monthly grand market**, drawing in people from across the region.
- Regular market days still occur, focusing on food, tools, and household goods.
- [[City Guard]] patrol actively, especially during market days.

### 🪜 Alleys and Lanes  
- Dense networks of **back alleys and side streets** link shops and stalls.
- Easy to get turned around without a guide.
- Common site of **petty theft, smuggling, and secret dealings** — though not as dangerous as the slums.

### 🏘️ Residences  
- Two-to-three-story row homes made of old brick or timber.
- Many residents live above their shops or stalls.
- Tight-knit but noisy communities.

---

## Inns & Taverns

- **The Brass Ladle** – Affordable tavern near the square, favored by laborers and guards. Known for loud dice games and decent stew.
- **The Violet Cap** – A more refined, elf-run establishment tucked away in a quieter lane. Offers teas, wine, and soft music.
- **Nora’s Rest** – A cozy, family-run inn often used by traveling merchants. Clean rooms, guarded stables.
- **The Split Coin** – A shady backstreet dive where deals are struck and drinks are cheap. Bouncer always present.

---

## Notable Shops

- **Three Threads & Needle** – Textile shop with quality regional dyes and magical-thread embroidery.
- **Grelt’s Candled Goods** – Wax, oils, incense, and the occasional alchemical candle. Scented wares popular with students and nobles.
- **The Arcane Latch** – Sells minor magical components, cheap scroll cases, ink, and pocket spellbooks.
- **Weylen’s Blades** – Reliable smithy run by a dwarven veteran. Deals in tools and shortblades more than war gear.
- **Salter & Sons** – Dry goods and spice seller. Sometimes trades in rare imports when the markets open.

---

## Vibe and Culture

- **Bustling, warm, and occasionally chaotic**.
- Locals are **used to odd types** — unusual visitors or adventurers don’t draw much suspicion here.
- Nights are quieter but not entirely safe — **pickpocketing and break-ins** occur.
- **Common gathering point** for festivals, minor protests, or performances.

---

## Trade and Goods

- Local produce: turnips, root vegetables, dried apples, bread wheels, goat cheese.
- Velaris-crafted goods: minor enchanted trinkets, cloth dyes, copper tools, scented oils.
- Occasional exotic wares from distant traders (at suspicious prices).
- Magical components (low-tier): chalk, crystal dust, spell ink, sometimes sold discreetly.

---

## Security

- Patrolled regularly by the [[City Guard]] — especially on market days.
- Generally **safe during the day**, more **tense at night**.
- Recent incidents (e.g., the [[Basilisk Incident]]) have **shaken public trust** in local safety.

---

## Secrets and Intrigue

- Certain alleys act as **informal smuggling routes** between Eastveil and the Slopes.
- Some vendors operate **“under the table” arcane services** or deals.
- A number of **abandoned cellars** and hidden storerooms remain beneath the district — legacy of the old city.
