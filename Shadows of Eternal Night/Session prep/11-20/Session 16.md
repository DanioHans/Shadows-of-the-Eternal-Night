# The Weight of a Verdict

**Location**: Crownwatch Bastion, Caer Hollow  
**Time of Day**: Morning into early afternoon  
**Tone**: Institutional, tense, quietly consequential  
**Primary Challenge**: Social and dramatic — no planned combat  
**Anomaly Intensity**: 1 — Background only, not foregrounded

---

## DM Overview

This session is a court proceeding. The primary challenge is social and dramatic. Queek stands trial for attempted murder and aggravated assault on a civilian in a public establishment. The party must navigate an unfamiliar institutional process to argue the attempted murder charge down to aggravated assault — if they succeed, Queek walks out with conditions. If they don't, he stays in a cell indefinitely.

The session has four movements:

1. **Setup** — weapons confiscated, Zone of Truth explained, charge read
2. **The Accusation** — Halna, Bryndis, and the escalating charge
3. **The Defence** — party testimonies, Samsons as a complication, Queek speaks
4. **Verdict and Aftermath** — one of three outcomes, then Felwin's approach

The trial should feel slow and procedural. The party is not in control here. Let that discomfort sit.

---

## Key NPCs This Session

**Magister-Auditor Cael Reno** — Presiding magistrate. The party has met him before (Session 2 interrogation). Methodical, fair, not easily charmed. He respects clear reasoning and honest accounting. He has no stake in any outcome beyond the law being correctly applied. Insight +4. Deception checks made in his presence are at disadvantage even outside the Zone of Truth.

**Sergeant Halna** — Present as lead of the city guard and witness for the City Guard. She will be precise and without editorialising. Neither vindictive nor sympathetic. 

**Oswin Sorn** --- Guard advocate, does most of the speaking for the guards

**Pell Gorvan and Corwin Brade** --- off-duty city guards who resolved the situation and took queek in

**Bryndis Kalt** — A dwarven stonemason, visiting Caer Hollow for contract work. Survived the attack, jaw bruised, controlled and direct. She is not hysterical and she is not asking for Queek's death. She is asking the court to confirm that what happened to her was wrong and will not happen again. Do not make her unsympathetic — she is right.

**Archivist Elira Felwin** — Present in the gallery as a silent observer. Do not introduce or acknowledge her until after the verdict. She approaches the party in the corridor on the way out.

**Court Cleric (unnamed)** — A mid-ranking Arcanum-affiliated cleric, present at all formal proceedings. Casts Zone of Truth at the opening of testimony. Does not speak otherwise.

## Before the Session Starts — Weapons and Preparation

When the party arrives at the Bastion for the proceedings, guards at the entrance require all weapons to be surrendered. This is standard and non-negotiable.

- Melee weapons, ranged weapons, wands, rods, and staves are taken and tagged
- Spell components are _not_ confiscated — but casting anything inside the court is a separate offence (see Escalation)
- Queek's restraints are reduced to wrist bindings for the proceedings — mask removed

Read or paraphrase court etiquette to the players before the session begins:

> _Speak only when addressed by the Magistrate, or stand and wait to be recognised before speaking. Address him as Magistrate or Your Honour. No spells are to be cast inside this chamber. The accused speaks only when invited to do so. The court is operating under arcane assurance — those who give testimony at the witness stand are subject to its conditions._

> **DM Tip — tell Samsons's player privately:** Reno is aware of the Enlarge/Reduce incident from the arresting guards' report. He will call Samsons to testify. Samsons should know this is coming and be quietly uncomfortable about it.

## Zone of Truth — The Witness Stand

A low circular dais, roughly six feet across, sits between the parties and the magistrate's desk. This is the witness stand. Before testimony begins, the court cleric casts **Zone of Truth (DC 16)** centred on the dais. The spell is cast without announcement — Reno simply says _"The court will now proceed under arcane assurance."_

**Mechanics:**

- Any creature that enters the dais to give testimony must make a **Charisma saving throw DC 16**. On a failure they cannot speak deliberate falsehoods while standing on the dais.
- On a success, the caster knows it resisted, which would require proper reasoning to not be suspicious to the court.
- Creatures that fail are aware they cannot lie directly. They can still be evasive, misleading, selectively truthful, or silent. Creative truthfulness is entirely valid.
- The Zone does not compel speaking. Silence is always an option.

**Players who ask about it:** An Arcana check DC 12 identifies the spell. On a failure they may notice the air feels slightly different, or that certain words feel harder to form — they can figure it out through play.

**Queek under Zone of Truth:** His testimony becomes more powerful, not less. He cannot dress it up. What he says, he means. Let that land.

## Movement One — The Charge

Reno reads the formal charge. His voice is the voice of a man reading a document.

> _"On the evening of yesterday , in the establishment known as the Brass Ladle Inn, the accused — a creature of unknown legal standing identified as a Skaven, held under the name Queek — did assault one Bryndis Kalt, civilian and lawful resident of Caer Hollow, causing injury sufficient to require apothecary attendance. The accused continued the assault after the victim was downed. The accused then resisted lawful restraint by City Guard officers, the resistance being prolonged and complicated by the actions of at least one member of the accused's associated party."_

A pause.

> _"The City Guard has submitted a formal request that the court consider a charge of attempted murder in addition to aggravated assault and disturbing the peace. This court will hear testimony before determining whether that charge is sustained."_

This is the first moment the players understand the full weight of what they are here for.

## Movement Two — The Accusation

### Oswin Sorn

Sorn takes the witness stand. He gives his account cleanly: He was not present but received the report from the off-duty guards. He describes the attack, the escalation, the Reduce and Enlarge spells, the eventual restraint.

>_The City Guard presents the following account to this court. yesterday evening, the accused — held under the name Queek — did enter the Brass Ladle Inn in the company of associates and, without provocation or prior exchange, assault one Bryndis Kalt with a spear, continuing the attack after the victim had fallen. The assault was witnessed by two off-duty City Guard officers who intervened at personal risk. The accused resisted restraint, the restraint being further complicated by magical interference from a member of the accused's associated party. The victim required apothecary attendance. The Guard submits that the nature of the attack — the continuation after the victim was downed, the force applied, the resistance to lawful intervention — warrants the court's consideration of attempted murder alongside the assault charge. The Guard calls Corwin Brade._

If players request to question Sorn, Reno allows it. Things that can be drawn out through questions:

- Queek did not kill Bryndis despite being in a position to do so
- He stopped responding to the frenzy when addressed by Merios — before he was fully restrained
- The Enlarge spell made restraint significantly more dangerous for the guards involved

> **DM Tip**: Sorn is honest. She will confirm things that help the party if asked directly, but she will not volunteer them. The players need to ask the right questions. Reward good thinking.


### City Guards

**Corwin Brade — testimony**

Corwin is young and idealistic per your notes. He's also still processing what he saw. He speaks carefully, like someone who has been thinking about how to say this correctly.

> _"We were off duty. Pell and I were at the bar. I heard the commotion before I saw it — chairs, someone shouting. When I turned, the creature was already on the floor over the woman. It had a spear. I couldn't tell if she was breathing."_

> _"I called for it to stop. It didn't respond. I don't think it heard me — it wasn't — it wasn't looking at anything in the room, if that makes sense. Like it was somewhere else."_

> _"We got hold of it eventually. That was when the magic started. Someone in the group made it small, which helped, and then the same person made it large again, which didn't. I don't know why they did that. I assumed it was an accident."_

**The incorrect assumption:** Corwin says he couldn't tell if Bryndis was breathing when he first saw Queek over her — implying Queek may have continued attacking a potentially unconscious or dying woman. This is an interpretive leap. Bryndis was conscious and trying to get up; Corwin was across a crowded room and misread the scene. The party can challenge this — _"You said you couldn't tell if she was breathing. Did you establish that before or after you intervened?"_ Under Zone of Truth Corwin will have to admit it was an impression formed from distance, not a confirmed observation.

**The second assumption:** He says he _assumed_ the Enlarge was an accident. This is useful — it means the party doesn't have to argue that Samsons intended harm, but it also means Reno will probe it when Samsons takes the stand.

---

**Pell Gorvan — testimony**

Pell is older and less reflective than Corwin. He was closer to the action during the restraint and has strong feelings about it.

> _"I was two stools down. Didn't see the first strike — heard it. By the time I got there Corwin was already moving."_

> _"The creature fought the hold. Strong for the size of it. We had it down twice and it got back up both times. I've restrained men twice its weight with less trouble."_

> _"The party didn't help. One of them — the tall one, robes — kept casting things. I don't know what the intention was. What I know is I had my hands full of something that kept changing size and I'm lucky I didn't lose a finger."_

> _"It stopped when the woman with the wings spoke to it. Just — stopped. Looked at her. That's when we got the irons on."_

**The incorrect assumption:** Pell says the party _didn't help_ — framing them as passive or obstructive. This is partially true but misses Merios's intervention, which was the thing that actually ended it. The party can challenge this: _"You said the party didn't help. Is it not true that it was a party member who stopped the creature?"_ Pell will have to concede this — under Zone of Truth he cannot maintain the framing — but he'll do it grudgingly. _"The winged one talked it down, yes. After the robed one made it the size of a house."_

**The useful detail:** Pell independently confirms that Queek stopped responding to the frenzy when Merios spoke to him. He doesn't understand the significance of this. The party does. It's the strongest piece of evidence that Queek was not in an uncontrollable killing state — he could be reached. That's the argument against attempted murder, and it comes from a hostile witness.



### Bryndis Kalt

Bryndis takes the stand. She is compact, bruised, controlled. She describes being attacked by something she had never encountered and had no reason to expect. She did not provoke it. She did not know it existed. She has since been told what a Skaven is and does not find this reassuring.

**Bryndis Kalt — spoken text (Movement Two, factual account)**

> _"I was sitting at a table near the window. I had a meal in front of me. I did not know the creature that attacked me. I had not spoken to it, I had not looked at it. The first thing I knew of it was the blow. I went down. It continued. The guards pulled it off me."_

A pause. Reno may ask if she has anything to add.

> _"I have been told it is not from this world. I have been told its kind fight dwarves where it comes from. I do not know what to do with that. What I know is that I was sitting at a table and something tried to kill me, and I would like the court to mean something today."_

She steps down. She does not look at Queek.

If asked whether she believes Queek intended to kill her: _"I believe it intended to finish what it started. That it did not finish is not a comfort to me. That is not the same as intention not existing."_

She is not asking for execution. She is asking for the court to mean something.

> **DM Tip**: Do not let the party bully or dismiss her. Reno will note it and it will cost them. If they are respectful and ask good questions, she will acknowledge — under Zone of Truth, and therefore meaningfully — that she has been told Queek is not from this world. She does not know what to do with that. She says so honestly.

### The Attempted Murder Question

After Bryndis steps down, Reno addresses the party directly.

> _"The charge of attempted murder requires the court to establish intent to kill. The accused struck a downed civilian and continued. Counsel for the defence: make your case that this charge should not be sustained, or it stands alongside the assault charge and is weighed accordingly."_

This is the pivot point of the session. The party must now argue — not just speak in Queek's favour, but make a specific legal argument that the attempted murder charge should be dropped. The tools available to them:

- Queek stopped. Not because he was restrained, but because Merios spoke to him. This suggests the frenzy had a ceiling — he was not in a killing state, he was in a reactionary state.
- He is not from this world. The legal concept of intent assumes a creature that understands the law it is violating. Queek did not know what attempted murder was, let alone that he was committing it.
- No precedent exists for a Skaven in this court. The charge assumes a framework that was not written for this situation.

A strong argument here — good roleplay, Persuasion DC 15, supported by specific points — will result in Reno setting the attempted murder charge aside pending the full verdict. A weak argument means it stays on the table and worsens the outcome.

## Movement Three — The Defence

### Queek Speaks

Queek takes the witness stand. His restraints are not removed but he stands on the dais.

He says what he says. Use the player's testimony note directly — read it verbatim if comfortable, or deliver it in his voice. The key beats must land:

- The instinct was real and came from real loss
- He knew this world was different. It did not reach the part of him that acts
- He is not from here and the rules here are not the rules he was built by
- He did not finish. Merios stopped him.

Let the room sit in silence after. Do not fill it. Reno writes something. Bryndis, in the gallery, is still. Do not describe her expression — let the players wonder.

### Party Testimonies

Party members may request to speak as character witnesses. Reno allows each one, in order. Suggested beats:

**Merios** — The most natural advocate. She was there, she stopped him, she can speak to his capacity to hear her in the middle of the frenzy. She can speak to his conduct over the weeks they have worked together. Under Zone of Truth, anything she says about Queek's trustworthiness or growth carries institutional weight. Persuasion DC 13 on a strong testimony shifts Reno's disposition noticeably.

**Scrap** — His testimony is not eloquent and does not need to be. He has watched Queek navigate a world that did not make him. If prompted, he can speak to what Queek has learned since arriving here — small things, specific things. Reno, unexpectedly, may find this more credible than polished advocacy precisely because it is not trying to be persuasive. Binary prompt if the player seems uncertain: _"Scrap. You have been beside Queek for weeks. One thing you have seen him learn. What is it?"_

**Kyron** — His moment is quieter. After Queek's testimony, before the party formally begins their defence, turn to Kyron directly.

> _"Kyron. When he described knowing something in his head that did not reach the part of him that acts — you recognised that."_

This is not a prompt for a speech to the court. It is a moment of recognition between the DM and the player. If Kyron's player wants to speak in Queek's defence, let them — his nature as a tiefling who has faced institutional suspicion is relevant and Reno is sharp enough to understand the parallel. If they sit with it quietly, that is also correct.

**Ivor** — Competent, brief, factual. He can speak to Queek's conduct on the vault job — under Zone of Truth, his account of Queek functioning as a reliable and controlled member of the team carries weight precisely because Ivor is not given to sentiment.

### Samsons — The Complication

Reno calls Samsons to the witness stand. This is not optional.

> _"The court notes that during the restraint of the accused, a member of the associated party — identified as a student of the Royal Arcanum — cast at least two spells in a public establishment: one reducing the accused and one enlarging the accused while City Guard officers were attempting to restrain him. The court wishes to understand the reasoning."_

Samsons is now on the Zone of Truth dais. He cannot lie cleanly. His options:

- **Honest accounting**: He made decisions in the moment. The Reduce was an attempt to de-escalate. The Enlarge was a mistake in judgement or something stranger — let his player work out what is true. An honest answer that acknowledges poor judgement actually helps Queek's case by humanising the chaos of the situation.
- **Evasion**: Technically true framings. _"I acted to prevent further harm"_ — this is true of the Reduce. It is not true of the Enlarge. Reno's Insight will probe the gap.
- **Silence**: He can decline to answer. This does not help Queek.

Reno will then add, regardless of what Samsons says:

> _"The court will be informing the Royal Arcanum that one of its registered students used magic in circumstances that directly complicated a lawful arrest. What the Arcanum does with that information is not within this court's jurisdiction."_

> **DM Tip**: This line is important. It is not a punishment from you — it is a natural institutional consequence that plants a seed. Samsons's player should feel the weight of it. The Arcanum will have questions for him. Verrath may be among those who hears about it.

## Movement Four — The Verdict

Reno deliberates. He writes. He sets down his quill and looks at Queek for a moment with the expression of a man arriving at a conclusion he did not entirely expect.

The verdict depends on how the party performed. See **Outcome Table** below.

### After the Verdict — Felwin's Approach

Whatever the outcome, as the party moves toward the Bastion's exit, a woman separates from the last remaining gallery observer.

She is middle-aged, plainly dressed for someone of obvious institutional standing. Dark practical clothes, ink-stained fingers, a leather satchel slightly too full. She carries herself with the quiet focus of someone who is always doing two things at once.

> _"I hoped to catch you before you left. Archivist Elira Felwin — Royal Arcanum, Deep Index. I have been watching your group for some time. Not in the way that should concern you."_

She does not explain herself immediately. She assesses them first.

> _"You have been in several places recently that I find interesting. The Sulfur Baths district. The sewer access points in Smokestack Row. I am not asking you to explain your business there. I am asking whether you found anything that seemed out of place. Structurally. Arcane infrastructure that should not be where it was."_

**If they are guarded:** She opens her satchel and produces a small leather codex — neat notation, locations, dates, anomaly descriptions. Two entries correspond to places the party has been. She shows them without comment.

> _"I am trying to understand something that is becoming difficult to ignore. I would benefit from eyes I can trust."_

**What Felwin knows:**

- Magical anomalies are occurring across Caer Hollow in a pattern she cannot yet fully map
- At least two anomaly points correlate with sewer infrastructure locations
- Something is drawing ambient magical energy — she has measured the deficit, cannot explain the cause
- She has internal concerns about the direction of Arcanum research, but does not name Verrath yet

**What she wants:** She asks for one thing. If the party encounters anything in the next week that feels structurally wrong — arcane machinery, unusual stonework, notation they don't recognise — bring it to her or send word. Deep Index reading room, Arcanum lower wing, ask for her by name.

> _"I am not offering a contract. I am asking for eyes. The Arcanum has many eyes. Most of them are looking in the wrong direction."_

She closes the codex. Stands.

> _"Congratulations on your creature's freedom. The testimony was unexpectedly eloquent."_

She leaves. She does not linger.

> **DM Tip**: Felwin is not warm. She is precise and she has decided the party is useful. The satchel is slightly too full because she is carrying more than she showed them. Do not over-explain her concerns. Let the players wonder what she knows.

## Escalation — If Combat Breaks Out

This should not happen. If it does, it is because someone made a catastrophic decision and the consequences are proportionate.

The Bastion is not a dungeon. It is a functioning military and judicial facility. Initiating violence here is not a fight — it is the beginning of a pursuit that ends badly.

**Forces in the building:**

- 4–6 City Guard soldiers (lightly armoured, short swords and crossbows, non-lethal priority but will escalate)
- Sergeant Halna (see her statblock — she is a serious threat at this party's level)
- The court cleric (healing and support spells, Zone of Truth still active in the chamber)
- 1–2 Crownwatch officers (significantly better equipped than City Guard — treat as Veterans, CR 3)

If combat begins inside the chamber, Reno leaves. He does not fight. He will file charges.

The Crownwatch officers will attempt to seal exits first. The party is effectively enclosed in a stone building with no weapons and multiple armed personnel between them and any exit.

**Do not plan this as an encounter. Use it as a threat.** If a player reaches for a spell or a weapon, describe the nearest guard's hand moving to their crossbow. Let the moment breathe. Most players will stand down.

If they don't stand down: this arc ends here and a new arc begins — fugitives from Caer Hollow's legal authority, Queek still in custody, Felwin unable to help openly. Not the worst outcome for a campaign, but a significant pivot.
