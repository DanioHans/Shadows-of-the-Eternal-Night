## Player-Facing Item (Act 1)

### **Unknown Shard**

**Description (read aloud):**  
A jagged shard of near-black glass.  
It absorbs light rather than reflecting it, the surface dull and depthless.  
When held, it feels unnaturally cold — not icy, but as if it is _drawing warmth out of your hand_.

If examined closely, the shard reflects faces a moment late, like an afterimage that hesitates before settling.

---

**Effect (Known):**  
Once per long rest, when you would make a saving throw against being frightened, charmed, or taking psychic damage, you may gain **advantage** on that saving throw.

---

**Quirk (Felt, not explained):**  
While carrying the shard, your dreams become unusually vivid and detailed.  
They are not nightmares — just _clear_, intense, and difficult to forget.

---

**Behavior (Implied):**

- Detect Magic yields mixed or unstable results
    
- The shard feels heavier in places where the world feels “thin”
    
- Letting go of it feels faintly uncomfortable, like forgetting something important
    

Do **not** describe this as a curse yet.

---

## Hidden Truth (DM Only – Act 2 Reveal)

When the party enters the Shadowfell, the shard’s nature becomes clear.
    
---

## Act 2 Escalation (When in the Shadowfell)

The shard’s “curse” activates **contextually**, not immediately.

Examples (pick 1–2 later):

- The shard **warms** in the Shadowfell instead of cooling
    
- Dreams begin to include _familiar places altered_
    
- The bearer occasionally feels watched during rest
    
- Shadow-aligned entities recognize it instinctively
    
- Once per arc, the shard reacts without being invoked
    

Crucially:

- It does **not** harm the party directly
    
- It **does** attract attention
    

This makes it a narrative accelerant, not a punishment.
