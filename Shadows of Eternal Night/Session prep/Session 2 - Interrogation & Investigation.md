
**Setting**: Caer Hollow, post-market chaos  
**Time**: Same day, late morning to evening  
**Focus**: Debrief and inquiry, then search for [[Jespin Wrent]]

---

## ❖ Overview

Following the basilisk incident in Eastveil Commons, the party has been summoned to Crownwatch Bastion for a formal inquiry. The city wants answers — not just about what happened, but about *how* a creature of such danger ended up in a market stall.

The players are not under arrest, but the tone is serious. Several officials are present. Once the inquiry concludes, the party is offered a task: track down Jespin Wrent, the missing smuggler. In exchange, they will be rewarded and quietly praised by the crown.

---

## ❖ Part 1: Interrogation at Crownwatch Bastion

### ❖ Location

A chamber in the lower floor of [[Crownwatch Bastion]] — sparsely furnished, arcane lanterns dimmed. Tension hangs in the air.

### ❖ Present NPCs

- **[[Captain Teron Veyne]]** – Stands silently at the edge, overseeing the proceedings.
- **[[Sergeant Halna]]** – Offers witness testimony on behalf of the guard. Occasionally clarifies things with the party.
- **[[Magister-Auditor Cael Rynel]]** – Appointed judiciary agent. Conducts the formal questioning.
- **[[High Arcanist Verrath]]** – Arrives partway through. Focused primarily on Samson's conduct.

---

### ❖ Tone and Goals

- Formal but not hostile.
- The party should feel like the city is trying to *understand and assess* the event rather than accuse.
- Verrath lectures Samson, not out of anger, but out of cold academic warning.

> *“If the crownwatch would want to detain you, you wouldn't be sitting here, you would be in a cell instead. Be grateful.”*

---

### ❖ Interrogation Prompts

Magister Cael Rynel may ask:

- Who retrieved the scroll?
- What was your intention with it?
- When did you first notice the creature?
- Did the stallkeeper (Wrent) behave suspiciously?
- Did anyone interfere with the situation?
- Was the creature summoned, smuggled, or otherwise imported?
- Who else did you interact with at the stall?

### ❖ Clues Given to the Party

- Wrenth **fled the scene** as the chaos began.
- His cart and stall were abandoned — some documents and crates remain.
- The guard pursued leads into the **Slums** but lost the trail in **Smokestack Row**, a densely packed and rough area.
- Wrent has a **record**: minor smuggling, tax evasion, and unlicensed magical items.

---

## ❖ Part 2: Assignment & Motivation

### ❖ Offer

- Track down Jespin Wrent. He is believed to be hiding in the **southern Slums**, likely Smokestack Row.
- Return him alive and provide any relevant documentation or information he possesses.

### ❖ Initial Reward

- 50 gold per person.

### ❖ Additional Incentives (if pressed)

- **Public recognition** and cleared record of involvement.
- **Access to restricted Arcanum materials** (esp. for Samson/Kyron).
- **Palace favor token** from Veyne (used once to gain minor privilege).
- **Potential magical oddities** from confiscated smuggler goods.
- **Academic or religious endorsement** (Verrath, Temple of the Dawnmother).

### ❖ Justification for Not Using the Guard

- Wrent is **not powerful**, just evasive. He blends in easily in the Slums.
- The **guard lacks the subtlety** for this kind of search — Wrent may be tipped off quickly.
- The party **faced him firsthand** and may recognize traces others wouldn’t.
- Prior to the basilisk, Wrent was **low-priority** — mostly fraud. Now he’s tied to a public magical disaster.
- Halna admits **limited local cooperation** in the Slums, making tracking difficult.
- Sending a full patrol may **escalate or alarm** the wrong people.

## ❖ Part 3: Investigation Phase

### ❖ Locations of Interest

- **Abandoned Stall** in Eastveil Commons
- **Nearby Merchants** or witnesses
- **Local pickpockets or urchins**
- **Guard contact** who mentions Smokestack Row

### ❖ Search Clues

- Stall contains:
  - A **burnt manifest** listing strange creatures, partially legible.
  - A crate marked "property of Graye & Graye Imports" — potentially a false front.
  - Empty vial labeled “for sleeping agent – 1 drop per beast”.

- Merchant next door says:
  - Wrent argued with someone two nights ago — might’ve owed money.
  - He paid for extra security last month, then canceled it suddenly.

- Young pickpocket:
  - “Saw him head toward the smoke towers — after the boom.”
  - Can be bribed for more info (1–2 silver or sweets).

- One could be a **former dockhand**, tough but dim
- Alternatively: **pickpocketing** or light resistance from gang-aligned kids
