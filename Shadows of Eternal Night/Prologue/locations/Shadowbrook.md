[[Shadowbrook(short)]]

**General Atmosphere:**

- **Overall Feel:** Shadowbrook is a small, isolated village shrouded in a perpetual fog, which seems to seep through every crevice and hang over the rooftops like a ghostly veil. The air is heavy with a sense of foreboding, and even the bravest souls find it difficult to shake off the feeling of being watched.
- **Lighting:** The village is dimly lit, with flickering lanterns casting long, eerie shadows. At night, the darkness is almost oppressive, pierced only by the weak glow of the lanterns and the occasional torch.

**Village Layout:**

- **Central Square:** At the heart of Shadowbrook lies a modest central square with a weathered stone fountain, its water now stagnant and covered with a thin layer of moss. Surrounding the square are several key buildings, including the tavern, the general store, and the village elder's house.
- **Houses:** The houses of Shadowbrook are small, wooden structures with thatched roofs. They appear old and worn, many showing signs of neglect and disrepair. The windows are often shuttered, and doors are reinforced with heavy wooden beams.
- **Paths and Roads:** Narrow cobblestone paths connect the various buildings, winding their way through the village. These paths are uneven and overgrown with weeds, giving them a neglected appearance. The main road leading into and out of the village is a dirt track, muddy and rutted from infrequent use.

**Key Locations:**

1. **The Tavern: "The Weary Traveler":**
    
    - **Exterior:** A sturdy building with a faded wooden sign depicting a tired-looking traveler resting under a tree.
    - **Interior:** The tavern is dimly lit, with a low ceiling and a cozy fireplace crackling in the corner. The air is thick with the smell of roasted meat, ale, and pipe smoke. Wooden tables and benches are scattered around the room, most occupied by wary villagers.
    - **Owner:** A burly, bearded man named Garret, who is friendly but cautious of newcomers. He offers food, drink, and rumors about the recent events in the village.
2. **The Elder's House:**
    
    - **Exterior:** A slightly larger and better-maintained house compared to the others, with a small garden of herbs and flowers out front.
    - **Interior:** The interior is cluttered with books, maps, and various trinkets. A large wooden table in the center of the room is covered with documents and scrolls.
    - **Elder:** Elara, the village elder, is a wise and kind-hearted woman in her late 60s. She is knowledgeable about the village's history and the dark events currently unfolding.
3. **The Graveyard:**
    
    - **Location:** Situated on the outskirts of the village, surrounded by a low stone wall.
    - **Atmosphere:** The graveyard is eerie and overgrown, with tombstones leaning at odd angles and graves showing signs of recent disturbances. The ground is uneven and covered with a thin mist.
    - **Notable Feature:** A large, ancient oak tree stands in the center, its gnarled branches casting ominous shadows over the graves.
4. **The Missing Villager's Home:**
    
    - **Exterior:** A small, dilapidated house at the edge of the village. The door hangs slightly ajar, and the windows are dark.
    - **Interior:** Inside, the house is in disarray, with furniture overturned and personal belongings scattered about. Strange symbols are scrawled on the walls in a dark, dried substance.
**Villagers:**

- **General Population:** The villagers are mostly common folk – farmers, laborers, and tradespeople. They are fearful and suspicious, especially of strangers, but are also desperate for help.
- **Common Attire:** They wear simple, practical clothing suited to a rural lifestyle. Many of their garments are patched and worn, reflecting their modest means.
- **Behavior:** The villagers are reluctant to speak openly about the disappearances and undead sightings. However, with some persuasion or acts of kindness, they might share what they know.

**Local Wildlife:**

- **Daytime:** During the day, the surrounding forest is eerily quiet, with only the occasional bird call breaking the silence. The animals seem to sense the unnatural presence and keep their distance.
- **Nighttime:** At night, the forest comes alive with strange sounds – rustling leaves, distant howls, and the occasional unearthly moan. These noises add to the villagers' fear and unease.
