# Archivist Elira Felwin

## Overview
Elira Felwin is a senior archivist within the **Royal Arcanum of Velaris**, responsible for the **Deep Index** — a secure section of the academy’s library housing ancient, dangerous, or politically sensitive magical texts. Known for her strict adherence to protocol and an encyclopedic knowledge of arcane history, she is both respected and quietly feared by students.

## Appearance
- Thin, silver-haired elf with piercing blue eyes
- Wears deep violet robes marked with the sigil of the Arcanum
- Always carries a floating lantern orb and a slate-bound ledger of permissions

## Personality
- Unyielding when it comes to rules and access restrictions
- Deeply curious beneath a cold, professional surface
- Has a sharp tongue but respects those who show genuine academic discipline
- Holds grudges against those who defy proper archival procedures

## Role in the Campaign
- Samson likely knows her from prior requests for obscure sources
- May deny or permit access to forbidden texts depending on favor, clearance, or bribes (not in coin — in information or favors)
- Possible witness or participant in the cover-up of certain “Forgotten Magic” being integrated into the curriculum

## Secrets & Hooks
- **Secretly tracking changes** to the curriculum under Headmaster Verrath, though she won’t admit it openly
- Keeps a **private codex** of anomalies she’s noticed — available only to those she fully trusts
- Has been approached by unknown parties seeking to access sealed texts related to **pre-Concordant Era magic**

## Possible Future Use
- Gatekeeper to deeper magical knowledge
- Potential whistleblower if convinced to act against Verrath
- Could be targeted (or protected) by players depending on their actions and alliances

## Connections
- Samson (frequent requester of rare material)
- Headmaster Verrath (technically her superior, but she keeps her own counsel)
- Council of Arcanists (respected, but not particularly political)

## Voice & Demeanor
- Speaks slowly, enunciating precisely
- Always tests others’ knowledge or intentions before giving answers
- Known to say:  
  > “Access is not a right. It is a responsibility, and one most squander.”
