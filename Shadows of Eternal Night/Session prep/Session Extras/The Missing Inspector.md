## Overview

Captain Veyne approaches the party off the record. A city infrastructure inspector named **Aldric Morse** has been missing for four days. Officially he is on extended assignment. Unofficially, Veyne knows Morse was compiling a formal report flagging structural anomalies across three city districts — unusual magical readings, unexplained foundation shifts — and was about to submit it to the Crown. Then he vanished.

Veyne cannot investigate officially without it reaching the wrong ears. She wants the party to find Morse discreetly, recover whatever he was working on, and bring him back alive.

The trail leads across three districts — Morse's office, a tavern where he met someone the night he disappeared, and eventually to a locked cellar beneath a merchant property in the Scholar's Descent where he is being held by two hired men under quiet orders from an Arcanum-adjacent contact. Morse is alive, shaken, and desperate to leave the city entirely. His partial survey map is the session's main information reward — an incomplete but unmistakable picture of something large being constructed beneath Caer Hollow.

**Party Level:** 5 **Estimated Duration:** 2–3 hours **Location:** Caer Hollow — Crownwatch Bastion, Infrastructure Office, Eastveil Commons, Scholar's Descent **Quest Type:** NPC Approaches — Captain Veyne **Tone:** Urban investigation. Primarily social and exploratory. Optional combat if players provoke the guards holding Morse.

---

## Hook — Veyne's Approach

Veyne finds the party at the Crownwatch Bastion, not in her office but in a quieter corner of the main hall. She does not sit. She keeps her voice low and does not open with pleasantries.

> _"I need something done quietly. Not Crownwatch quietly — actually quietly. No record of this conversation, no official tasking, nothing that goes through the Bastion's messenger tower. If you're willing to hear it, I'll tell you what I know. If not, we never spoke."_

If the party agrees she tells them the following, without embellishment:

- **Aldric Morse** is a city infrastructure inspector, mid-career, unremarkable record, not the kind of man who creates problems. Four days ago he stopped reporting in.
- His supervisor filed a standard absence notice — extended field assignment. Veyne checked. No such assignment was logged.
- Before he disappeared, Morse had been flagging anomalies in his foundation surveys for six weeks — elevated magical readings beneath multiple districts, unexplained structural shifts in buildings with no history of subsidence. He told his supervisor he was preparing a formal report for the Crown.
- The supervisor, a man named **Perro**, told Morse to hold the report until it could be reviewed internally. Two days later Morse was gone. Perro has since stopped discussing the matter.
- Veyne does not know if Perro is involved or simply afraid. She does not trust either answer right now.

**What Veyne wants:**

- Morse found and returned safely.
- Whatever he was working on — notes, maps, survey records — recovered.
- This done without Crownwatch involvement, without the Arcanum being alerted, and without the party making it obvious they are looking.

**Reward:** 120 gold on Morse's safe return. Additionally, Veyne will owe the party a genuine favour — access to a restricted Crownwatch resource, a charge dropped, a blind eye turned. She does not specify. She does not need to.

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** Veyne is not soft in this scene. She is asking for help precisely because she trusts the party, but she is also aware she is operating outside her authority. She will not thank them for pointing that out. If players ask why she can't act officially, her answer is simple: _"Because I don't know how far up this goes."_

## Lead 1 — The Infrastructure Office

The city's infrastructure office is a utilitarian building in the Eastveil Commons administrative quarter — not impressive, not secured, staffed by clerks who would rather be elsewhere.

Morse's direct supervisor **Perro** is present. He is a heavyset man in his fifties with the look of someone who has been sleeping badly and is trying not to show it.

**Approaching Perro:**

He is not hostile but immediately cautious. He will claim Morse is on assignment without hesitation and without eye contact. He does not want to discuss it further.

- **DC 12 Insight:** Perro is frightened, not complicit. He knows something is wrong but has convinced himself that staying quiet keeps him safe.
- **DC 13 Persuasion** (appealing to his conscience or Morse's wellbeing): He cracks enough to say that someone came to speak with Morse the week before he vanished — an official from the Arcanum, he thought, based on the robes. He does not know the name. The meeting was brief and Morse was quieter afterward.
- **DC 15 Intimidation:** Perro breaks entirely and hands over a copy of Morse's last submitted survey notes — the version before the final report, which Morse kept in the office filing system. These are useful but incomplete (see: Survey Notes below).
- **DC 17 Persuasion or a creative approach:** Perro admits he received a written message three days ago telling him to file the extended assignment notice and ask no further questions. He destroyed the message. He does not know who sent it. He describes the seal on the message as one he didn't recognise — not Crownwatch, not city administration, something with geometric patterning.

**Morse's desk:** If the party examines Morse's workspace (Perro will object but not physically stop them — DC 11 Persuasion to keep him cooperative while they look):

- His personal survey instruments are still here. A man on extended assignment would have taken them.
- A small personal journal is in the bottom drawer — not a work document. Most entries are mundane. The last entry, dated four days ago, reads: _"Meeting tonight at the Scroll's End. Don't know who arranged it. Feels wrong. Taking the draft map just in case."_

This gives the party their next lead — **the Scroll's End tavern** in the Scholar's Descent.

## Lead 2 — The Scroll's End Tavern

The Scroll's End sits just outside the Arcanum gates in the Scholar's Descent — a tavern popular with students and junior researchers, slightly rebellious in character compared to the Arcanum's formality. It is busy in the evenings, quieter during the day.

**The barkeep — Fen** — is a pragmatic woman who runs a tight establishment. She doesn't volunteer information but isn't obstructive if approached respectfully.

- **DC 11 Persuasion or a round of drinks purchased:** Fen remembers Morse. He was here four nights ago, sat alone, waited for about an hour. A man joined him — not a regular, well-dressed, calm. They spoke quietly for about twenty minutes. Morse left first, looking unsettled. The other man stayed for one more drink and then left.
- **DC 14 Persuasion or 5 gold:** Fen describes the second man more precisely — middle-aged, silver-streaked hair worn short, a small geometric pin on his collar she didn't recognise. Calm in a way that felt practised rather than natural. She noticed because he didn't touch his drink after Morse left.
- **DC 16 Persuasion or 15 gold:** Fen mentions that the same man has been in twice before in the past month, both times meeting with different people — she doesn't know who, she doesn't make it her business, but she noticed because he always orders the same thing and never finishes it.

**Other patrons:** A student named **Wex**, nursing a bad exam result in the corner, saw Morse leave and noticed he dropped something outside — a folded piece of paper that he picked up and then seemed to reconsider before tucking it into his coat. Wex didn't think anything of it at the time. He's happy to chat if someone buys him a drink and will mention this detail if the conversation turns to the man Morse was meeting.

> 💡 **Ivor Note:** The geometric pin description will mean nothing to most of the party yet, but Ivor has enough underworld familiarity that a DC 13 History or Streetwise check lets him recognise it as a marker used by certain administrative fixers — people hired to make problems disappear quietly, with connections into multiple institutions. Not assassins. Problem managers.

**Outside the tavern:** If the party examines the area outside the Scroll's End, a DC 14 Investigation check finds a faint drag mark in the alley beside the tavern entrance — not blood, just disturbed grime, consistent with someone being walked firmly rather than carried. It leads south toward the interior of the Scholar's Descent before losing coherence on the cobblestones.

## Lead 3 — Tracking the Fixer

Between Perro's description of the Arcanum-robed visitor, the geometric seal on the message, and the fixer's pin, the party has enough to start asking questions in the Scholar's Descent about who arranges quiet institutional business.

This is a free-form investigation section. The party can approach it however they choose — street inquiries, Samson using his student connections, Ivor working his criminal instincts. Reward creative approaches.

**Key information available through this section:**

- **DC 13 Investigation or street inquiries:** The geometric pin belongs to a man locally known as **Caelan Dross** — not a name anyone says loudly, but known in the Scholar's Descent as someone who handles sensitive matters for Arcanum faculty. Not officially employed. Not officially anything.
- **DC 14 Persuasion with a Scholar's Descent vendor or student:** Dross is known to use a property in the Scholar's Descent as an informal office — a narrow merchant building on **Glyph Lane** that officially belongs to a retired reagent supplier named Harven. Harven relocated to Briarford two years ago. The building has been used by rotating occupants since then, none of them official tenants.
- **Samson specifically:** His student connections give him automatic knowledge that Dross's name surfaces occasionally in faculty gossip — usually in the context of things that needed to quietly not happen. A complaint that was dropped, a student whose questionable research was overlooked. Nobody talks about it directly.

Once the party locates the building on Glyph Lane they have found Morse.

## The Glyph Lane Property

A narrow three-storey building squeezed between a closed bookbinder's and a reagent storage unit. Ground floor windows shuttered. No signage. A light visible through the shutters on the first floor.

**Exterior assessment:**

- DC 12 Investigation: The front door lock is newer than the door — recently replaced, quality hardware.
- DC 13 Perception: Faint sounds from below ground level — the building has a cellar, and there is someone in it.
- DC 14 Stealth to approach the first floor window without being noticed from inside.

**Inside the building — ground floor:** If the party enters (front door requires DC 14 Thieves' Tools from Ivor, or they find an unlocked rear window with DC 12 Investigation), they find a sparse working space. A desk with papers, a cold hearth, two chairs. The papers are mundane administrative correspondence — nothing incriminating on its face, but Samson or anyone with Arcana can note that several letters reference "assessment coordination" using terminology that matches what Morse's survey notes describe.

**The guards:** Two hired men are present — one on the ground floor, one by the cellar door. These are not soldiers or trained fighters. They are working men paid to sit somewhere and make sure nobody leaves. They are bored, slightly nervous, and not especially loyal to whoever hired them.

- If the party enters quietly and gets the drop on them: They can be intimidated into standing aside with DC 13 Intimidation without any violence. They will claim they were just paid to watch the place and don't know anything about the man downstairs.
- If the party enters loudly or confrontationally: The ground floor guard calls for his partner and they attempt to block access to the cellar. They will not fight to the death — if one is dropped or badly hurt, the other surrenders immediately.
- If the party taunts, demeans, or provokes them beyond the initial confrontation: The ground floor guard in particular — a thick-necked man named **Bort** who has a short temper and a longer memory for insults — will fight back with genuine intent. He is not skilled but he is large and angry.

**Bort stat block — optional combat:** Use **Thug** stat block (MM p.350) with HP increased to 40. His partner uses the standard Thug stat block. Neither has magic items. Both will flee if reduced below 10 HP.

> ⚠️ **DM Note:** Do not force this fight. The guards are obstacles, not antagonists. If the party is clever, diplomatic, or sufficiently intimidating, they walk past them without incident. The fight only happens if the players earn it.

## Aldric Morse — The Cellar

The cellar is dry, lit by a single lantern, and contains Morse and nothing else of comfort. He has been given water and basic food — this was not meant to be a death sentence, just a delay. He is uninjured but has not slept properly and is running on fear and cold.

**Morse's appearance:** Mid-forties, slight build, ink-stained fingers, the look of a man who has spent his career doing careful precise work and has absolutely no idea how to handle the situation he is in.

**His reaction to the party:** Immediate relief, followed immediately by suspicion — how did they find him, who sent them, are they Arcanum? Once reassured (he will recognise Veyne's name and visibly relax), he becomes cooperative and frightened in roughly equal measure.

**What Morse tells them:**

- He was approached six weeks ago by a man he now suspects was Dross, who told him that certain survey anomalies he had been flagging were part of a classified Arcanum infrastructure project and that he should quietly remove them from his reports and submit nothing to the Crown.
- Morse didn't agree. He kept surveying. The anomalies kept getting worse and more widespread.
- The night at the Scroll's End: Dross met him again and told him directly that his report would not reach the Crown and that it would be better for everyone if he took some time away from the city. When Morse refused, he was followed back to his lodgings and brought here the following morning. Nobody hurt him. Nobody explained further.
- He has been here for four days. He does not know what Dross intended to do with him eventually.

**The draft map:** Morse has it on him — folded inside his coat, which he apparently refused to remove. It is a partial survey of the city's foundation grid with Morse's annotations marking every anomalous reading he recorded over six weeks.

The markings form an unmistakable shape when viewed as a whole — a rough geometric pattern spanning several districts, all readings elevated, all converging toward a central point in the Scholar's Descent. Morse has not fully interpreted what the pattern means — he is an infrastructure inspector, not an arcane scholar — but he knows it is deliberate and he knows it is large.

> 💡 **Samson specifically:** With a DC 14 Arcana check Samson can look at the map and identify that the geometric pattern is consistent with a large-scale arcane amplification structure — the kind of thing that would require months of careful construction and significant institutional resources. He cannot identify its purpose from the map alone, but he can say with confidence that whatever is being built beneath Caer Hollow is not small.

**What Morse wants:** To leave Caer Hollow. Immediately. He has family in Briarford. He will give the party the map without hesitation in exchange for safe passage out of the city. He is not a brave man and he knows it. He asks only that Veyne knows what happened to him.

---

## Resolution

Returning Morse to Veyne — even a handoff at a neutral location rather than the Bastion itself — completes the session's primary objective. Veyne pays the agreed reward without ceremony and takes the information about Dross with a controlled expression that does not quite conceal how seriously she is taking it.

She says one thing before they part:

> _"Dross doesn't work for free. Someone is paying him. Someone who knew Morse was getting close and wanted him moved, not silenced. That tells me they're not finished yet and they don't want the attention that a dead inspector would bring."_

She does not ask to keep the map. She lets the party hold it.

## What the Players Learn

This session's contribution to the larger picture is the most explicit of the three adventures — the map is a near-complete picture of Verrath's construction network beneath the city, even if the players cannot yet name Verrath as its architect.

Combined with the previous sessions:

- Rat King's Ransom: A node in the sewers drawing ley energy upward, connected to other structures by channels flowing toward the Scholar's Descent.
- Silverward Dissonance: A subterranean source feeding the Silverward's wards from below, the Arcanum aware and calling it _infrastructure assessment activity_.
- Missing Inspector: A city-wide geometric pattern of arcane anomalies converging on the Scholar's Descent, actively suppressed by someone with Arcanum connections.

A player like Samson who has been paying attention across all three sessions should now have enough to form a very uncomfortable theory — even if they cannot yet prove it, and even if they do not yet know what the structure is for.
