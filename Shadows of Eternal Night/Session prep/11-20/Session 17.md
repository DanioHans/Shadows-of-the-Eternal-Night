# Session 17 – The Deep Below

**Location**: Caer Hollow Sewers — beneath Smokestack Row and beyond  
**Time of Day**: Afternoon entry, potentially running into evening  
**Tone**: Claustrophobic, investigative, increasingly wrong  
**Primary Challenge**: Dungeon crawl with embedded spotlight moments  
**Anomaly Intensity**: 3 — Active and Physical

---

## DM Overview

This is the campaign's first proper dungeon crawl in an urban setting. The sewers beneath Caer Hollow are large, old, and not entirely what they were built to be. Felwin has asked the party to investigate a convergence point on her anomaly map — a location in the sewer system beneath Smokestack Row where multiple readings have spiked. She wants physical evidence of what is down there.

What they find is a sewer network that has been quietly modified over months: tunnels reinforced, chambers expanded, arcane infrastructure embedded in the walls. The deeper they go, the more deliberate it looks. At the end of the crawl is a Lattice anchor node — the first one the party will see up close.

The session has four zones of increasing depth and danger, a handful of embedded spotlight moments for Scrap, Kyron, and Ivor, and ends with the party surfacing with physical evidence that connects the construction to the Arcanum.

> **Note on The Rat King's Ransom:** That document covers the upper sewer section and the first node encounter. Use it as the foundation for Zones 1 and 2. This document extends significantly beyond it into Zones 3 and 4, which are new.

---

## The Hook

Felwin contacts the party the morning after the trial — a note delivered to the Bastion, written in her precise economical hand:

> _"I have a location. Beneath Smokestack Row, sewer maintenance junction seven. My readings there have been elevated for three weeks and spiked twice in the last four days. I need to know what is physically present at that site. I cannot go myself — I am known at the Arcanum and my movements are observed. You are not. I will pay sixty gold for a detailed account of what you find, and a further forty if you return with something I can hold in my hands."_

No meeting. No in-person contact. She does not want to be seen with them yet.

## General Sewer Atmosphere

Read or paraphrase on entry and adapt as they descend:

> _The maintenance hatch drops them into the upper sewer channel — a low stone passage, ceiling barely eight feet, water running ankle-deep along a central groove. The smell is what it always is. But underneath it, something else: warm air, moving upward from somewhere below. And a hum. Low and constant, felt in the chest more than heard, like standing near a struck bell long after the tone has started to fade._

**Consistent environmental details as they descend:**

- The warmth increases with depth
- The hum becomes more physical — eventually concentration checks for spellcasters
- Bioluminescent fungus appears in Zone 2, casting faint blue-green light
- The stonework changes — original sewer construction gives way to newer, cleaner fitted stone with metal bracketing
- The smell shifts from rot to something electrical, like air before a lightning strike

**Vixen** — Scrap's fox — is uneasy from the moment they enter. She presses close to Scrap, ears flat, tail low. She does not bark. This is not fear of the dark; she is reading something the party cannot yet sense. Use her as an early warning system — she becomes more agitated the closer they get to active Lattice construction.

---

## Zone 1 — The Upper Channels

_Equivalent to the upper sewer sections in The Rat King's Ransom. Use that document for full detail. Summary below for continuity._

Standard sewer infrastructure. Narrow passages, maintenance alcoves, the occasional collapsed section. The first signs of wrongness: rats in unusual numbers, moving with unsettling coordination. Walls cracked from below rather than above.

**Key beat here:** The party finds the first piece of modified infrastructure — a section of wall where the original sewer stone has been reinforced with newer work. The joins are clean. This was done by someone who knew what they were doing. A DC 13 Investigation check reveals the reinforcement follows the channel's direction deliberately — it is structural support for something further in, not repair work.

**Encounter:** First wave of mutated rats (use Rat King's Ransom stat modifications — Giant Rat with force damage bite). Keep this fast. It is a warm-up, not the session's combat.

---

## Zone 2 — The Modified Tunnels

The party crosses a threshold — literally. A section of tunnel has been fitted with a new stone arch, cleaner and more precise than anything around it. Beyond it, the character of the sewer changes.

**Environment:**

- Ceiling rises to twelve feet
- Walls are fitted stone with metal bracketing etched with geometric runes
- Bioluminescent fungus clusters at regular intervals — not natural growth, deliberately cultivated to provide ambient light
- The hum is louder here, directional now — coming from ahead and below
- Temperature noticeably higher

**Encounters in Zone 2:**

_The Lurker_ — A single large creature occupying the main channel: a sewer crocodile that has been living too close to an active Lattice feed point for too long. It is wrong in visible ways — one eye replaced by something crystalline, scales partially translucent showing the musculature beneath, movements slightly too fast for its size. It is not mindless. It watches the party before it attacks, which is worse.

Use a Crocodile stat block (CR 1/2) with the following modifications:

- HP 45
- Speed 30ft land, 40ft swim
- Bite deals an additional 1d6 force damage
- Reaction: _Arcane Lurch_ — when reduced below half HP, the crocodile surges with stored magical energy; all creatures within 10ft make DC 13 Dex save or take 2d6 force damage

> **Scrap spotlight:** The crocodile, before it attacks, pauses near Vixen — not aggressively, but with the confused attention of something that has almost forgotten what normal animals feel like. Vixen holds completely still. The crocodile does not immediately move toward the party. Give Scrap a binary choice: _"It is looking at Vixen. You can pull her back, or you can hold still and see what it does."_ If Scrap holds still, the crocodile turns away from Vixen and toward the party — it cannot process her properly, she registers as something other than prey. It is a small moment of Vixen being relevant in a way that isn't combat.

_The Watchers_ — Two modified sewer workers, undead. These are not wandering undead — they were placed here. They patrol a specific section of Zone 2's tunnels in a fixed route, responding to intrusion. Their purpose is guard duty. They wear Arcanum construction worker insignia on their coats, which is the most disturbing thing about them.

Use Zombie stat blocks with the following addition:

- _Arcane Alarm:_ When destroyed, emits a pulse detectable to anyone attuned to Lattice notation within 500ft — this does not matter now, but note it for later
- They do not pursue beyond the end of Zone 2

> **DM Tip:** The construction insignia is the important detail here. Players who investigate the bodies find them. The Arcanum does not officially use undead labour. Someone decided these workers were more useful dead than alive. Let that sit.

## Zone 3 — The Construction Site

The tunnel opens into a space that was not here six months ago. A chamber thirty feet across and twenty feet high, carved from the bedrock below the sewer's original foundations. The walls are dressed stone, geometric runes covering every surface. The floor has been levelled and fitted with iron channels that run in parallel lines toward the far wall. Construction materials are stacked along the sides — stone blocks, metal rods, ceramic insulators of a kind used in Arcanum infrastructure work.

This room is actively being built. It is not finished.

**What the party finds:**

_Physical evidence for Felwin:_

- A surveyor's rod etched with Arcanum Infrastructure Office notation — this is the item Felwin asked for. DC 12 Investigation locates it among the materials.
- A construction ledger, partially complete — lists materials delivered, dates, quantities. No names, but the delivery chits use Arcanum institutional format. DC 14 Investigation identifies the format; DC 17 identifies the specific office — Verrath's division of the Deep Research Index.
- The ceramic insulators — three of them, unused. These are specialised components not available outside the Arcanum.

_Samson specifically:_ The rune notation on the walls matches what he saw in the Rat King's Ransom node chamber, if the party did that quest. If not, a DC 14 History check connects it to Arcanum architectural theory — and DC 17 surfaces the memory of Verrath's name attached to theoretical papers on ley amplification.

_Kyron specifically:_ The geometric rune patterns covering the walls have a quality that resonates uncomfortably with Clockwork Soul sorcery — the same underlying logic of imposed order on chaotic systems. He does not need to make a check to notice this. Turn to him directly: _"Kyron. The patterns on these walls — the logic of them. You recognise the principle, even if not the application. It feels like something you know from the inside."_ This is not a plot revelation. It is a disquieting recognition. Leave it unresolved.

**Encounter — The Foreman:**

The chamber is not empty. A creature that was once human occupies the far end — an overseer of some kind, now significantly modified by prolonged Lattice exposure. It is not undead. It is alive, in the technical sense: breathing, moving, aware. But its skin has taken on a faint crystalline quality, its eyes are the same purple-black as the node, and it responds to the party's presence not with alarm but with the focused attention of something that has been given a task and will not be interrupted.

Use a Mage stat block (CR 6) with the following modifications:

- Loses _Spellcasting_ — replaces it with _Lattice Channel:_ ranged spell attack +5, 60ft, deals 3d8 force damage on hit
- _Crystalline Skin:_ resistance to non-magical bludgeoning, piercing, slashing
- _Arcane Anchor:_ while within 10ft of any Lattice construction, has advantage on all saving throws
- On death: releases a burst of stored ley energy — 10ft radius, DC 14 Dex save, 3d6 force damage

This fight is the session's main combat encounter. The room's layout matters — the iron channels in the floor are difficult terrain if running across them, and the stacked construction materials provide cover. The Foreman will attempt to keep distance and use Lattice Channel. He does not flee.

---

## Zone 4 — The Anchor Chamber

Beyond the construction site, through a fitted stone doorway without a door — the frame is there, hinges installed, but the door itself has not yet been hung — the party reaches the anchor chamber.

This is not a new node. This one is older. It has been active for longer. The difference is visible.

**Environment:**

The chamber is roughly circular, fifteen feet across. The anchor sits at the centre — the same dark purple-black crystal as the node in Rat King's Ransom, but larger. Taller than a person. The radial channels cut into the floor glow faintly, running toward the walls and disappearing into the stone. The temperature here is uncomfortably high — like standing near a forge. The hum has become a tone, audible and consistent.

The fungus that was bioluminescent in Zone 2 does not grow here. Nothing grows here.

**What the party can learn:**

|Check|DC|Result|
|---|---|---|
|Arcana|12|The node is drawing ambient ley energy upward and inward simultaneously — it is both a siphon and a relay. It takes from the earth and passes it somewhere else.|
|Arcana|16|The relay direction is consistent across all the nodes the party has encountered — they are all feeding the same destination. The geometry points toward the Scholar's Descent district, where the Arcanum's Cradle Tower is located.|
|Investigation|13|The construction here is older than Zone 3. This node has been active for at least four months.|
|Investigation|17|The radial channels in this chamber are more extensive than the others — this is not a peripheral node. This is a primary relay point. The network routes through here.|
|History|15|The node's crystal composition matches theoretical descriptions of Weave-compression architecture — a branch of arcane engineering that the Arcanum officially classified as dangerous and suspended research on eleven years ago.|

**Tampering warning:** The node is heavily warded. Physical contact without the correct attunement triggers a DC 16 Constitution save — failure deals 4d10 force damage and knocks the creature prone. This should be communicated through the environment before anyone tries: the air near the node shimmers, small objects placed near it vibrate and skitter away, Vixen will not approach within ten feet.

**Ivor spotlight:** Along the chamber wall, partially obscured behind a Lattice channel bracket, is a small iron lockbox — not part of the construction, left here by someone. DC 14 Perception to notice it; DC 13 thieves' tools to open. Inside: a folded document in cipher, a small vial of something dark and viscous, and a Crownwatch sigil — not the City Guard sigil, the _Crownwatch_ sigil — belonging to someone who was here and left this behind, intentionally or otherwise. The cipher is beyond the party's current ability to break. The Crownwatch sigil means someone in the official city apparatus knows this place exists. Leave this entirely unresolved. It is a thread for much later.

> **DM Tip:** Do not explain the lockbox. Do not give the players the cipher key. The point is that someone else has been here, and the party now has evidence of it. Let them carry the uncertainty.

## Returning to the Surface

The party exits the way they came. The modified tunnels feel different on the way back — now that they know what they are, the rune bracketing on the walls reads as intentional rather than decorative. The hum is still there.

**What they have:**

- The surveyor's rod (Felwin's requested item)
- The construction ledger (supplementary evidence)
- The ceramic insulators if they took them
- The lockbox contents
- The sewer map from the vault in Session 15, now considerably more legible against what they have just seen

**Felwin's response** — she meets them at a neutral location of her choosing, not the Arcanum. She examines the surveyor's rod and the ledger. She is quiet for longer than is comfortable.

> _"This is Arcanum infrastructure notation. Current issue — not historical. Someone is using institutional resources to build this."_

Another pause.

> _"I have suspected the direction of this for some time. I did not have anything I could hold."_

She closes the ledger.

> _"There is a name that keeps appearing in the margins of things I cannot quite reach. I have been careful about saying it. I am going to be careful for a little longer."_

She pays them the hundred gold without ceremony. She tells them she will be in touch. She does not tell them what she intends to do with what they have brought her.

## Pacing Notes

Zone 1 — 20 minutes. Warm-up, atmosphere, first encounter.  
Zone 2 — 30–40 minutes. Two encounters, environment shifts, undead workers land.  
Zone 3 — 30–40 minutes. Construction site, main combat, evidence gathering.  
Zone 4 — 20–30 minutes. The anchor, investigation beats, lockbox, no combat.  
Exit and Felwin — 15 minutes.

Total estimated session: 2–2.5 hours of active content, comfortable for a full session with roleplay padding.

If running short: expand the Foreman fight, give the party more time in Zone 4 with investigation.  
If running long: compress Zone 1 to narration only, cut the undead workers and describe them as having been there recently (scorch marks, drag trails).
