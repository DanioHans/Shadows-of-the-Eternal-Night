
## Act 0: Prologue – Shadows Stir

**Level Range**: 3  
**Setting**: A remote village near a dark forest.

A necromancer conducts forbidden rituals in secret, reanimating the dead and testing the edges of the veil between life and death. The players stop him—but not before glimpses of a darker force behind his actions emerge. The necromancer references a “Pale Hand” and hints that death no longer behaves as it should.

**Narrative Purpose**:
- Introduce undead as a thematic core.
- Tease corruption and planar instability.
- Establish a baseline for divine casters and light magic, so their suppression later is felt.

---

## Act I: The Blackening of Hollowfall

**Level Range**: 4–6  
**Setting**: Small towns and the capital city of Hollowfall (name pre-cataclysm TBD).

The players are drawn into growing unrest across a once-stable kingdom. Reports of undead, shadowy figures, and divine silence increase, but leadership dismisses it. The players eventually arrive in **Hollowfall**, a grand and thriving city on the edge of the corrupted zone—just as it is **suddenly consumed in a night of death and darkness**.

They survive, barely. The city's fall is not gradual—it is overwhelming. They flee alongside a handful of survivors, powerless to stop the tide. This act ends not with triumph, but with failure, dread, and a burning desire to fight back.

**Narrative Purpose**:
- Deliver the **first major loss**—Hollowfall is taken.
- Create emotional investment in fighting Nagash’s forces.
- Establish urgency and vulnerability early in the campaign.

---

## Act II: Veil Breached

**Level Range**: 7–10  
**Setting**: Hollowfall ruins, nearby convergence zones, and the entry into the Shadowfell.

In the aftermath of Hollowfall’s fall, the players uncover a **Book of Nagash** in the ruins—alongside evidence of converging planes. This artifact reveals the veil has been breached. The undead no longer simply rise—they organize. Magic shifts. The gods falter.

The book contains secrets, riddles, and pathways—one of which offers a method to **enter the Shadowfell safely**, where the source of this corruption resides. The players may choose to enter immediately or first hunt down cults and gather allies, but ultimately, their path leads to the land of shadows.

**Narrative Purpose**:
- Begin the **book hunt** arc, establishing the books as keys to understanding (not defeating) Nagash.
- Introduce the **Shadow Corruption** mechanic through extended exposure.
- First possible encounter with a **minor BBEG** (e.g. necromancer general or a vampire lord).
- Potential early glimpse or intervention by **Nagash himself**, forcing the players to retreat.

---

## Act III: Shadows Ascendant

**Level Range**: 11–14  
**Setting**: Shadowfell, convergence sites, dead kingdoms.

The majority of the campaign now takes place in the **Shadowfell**. Here, the players encounter isolated bastions of resistance, corrupted gods, and the remains of ancient orders who failed to stop Nagash before. Each Book of Nagash they locate offers:
- Lore about Nagash's plans and past.
- Tempting powers at a cost.
- Clues about his ascension ritual and how to stop it.

Players face **zone-bound minor BBEGs**, corrupted lieutenants, and death-aligned entities. Divine magic suffers. Light falters. Each day in Shadowfell increases the risk of corruption unless mitigated through player ingenuity or sanctuary.

**Narrative Purpose**:
- Deepen the themes of choice and moral cost.
- Encourage exploration through the **book hunt** structure.
- Cement the sense that **Nagash’s victory is inevitable unless the players act boldly**.

---

## Act IV: The Fall of Light

**Level Range**: 15–17  
**Setting**: Shadowfell warzones, decaying Material Plane, shattered sanctuaries.

With most of the Nine Books uncovered or sealed, the players learn of Nagash’s final plan: a ritual to **merge the planes completely**, locking the universe into undeath. They race to stop the convergence, requiring them to break through convergence points, kill remaining generals, and disrupt the final components of the ritual.

At this stage, **corruption becomes a defining threat**. One or more players may be forced to make irreversible choices. Resurrection becomes nearly impossible. Planar travel becomes dangerous. The Material Plane teeters on collapse.

**Narrative Purpose**:
- Build to climax while ensuring players **feel the weight of their journey**.
- Emphasize desperation, sacrifice, and internal conflict.

---

## Act V: The Convergence

**Level Range**: 18–20  
**Setting**: Nagash’s fortress in the heart of Shadowfell.

Nagash enacts his final ritual. The players must infiltrate his fortress—passing through a gauntlet of death, betrayal, and arcane warping. Each step reveals how deeply his influence has spread. The final confrontation will test every choice they’ve made:
- Have they embraced or resisted corruption?
- Have they used the books? Destroyed them? Hidden them?
- Have they kept their divine connection or found new paths?

The battle against Nagash is **not just physical**—his essence has become entwined with the plane itself. His defeat may require a **sacrifice**, a **binding**, or **submission to his own laws**, rewritten by the players' hand.

**Narrative Purpose**:
- Deliver the emotional and mechanical culmination.
- Bring thematic closure to corruption, divinity, and resistance arcs.
- Let player choices throughout the campaign determine the ending.
