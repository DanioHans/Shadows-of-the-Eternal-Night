**DM Reminders**
- Keep it subtle; let them discover the escalation themselves.  
- A reflection might **look directly at a PC**, but only that player notices.  
- Use sensory cues — dripping echoes, double footsteps, or delayed speech.  
- When they rest or lose time, emphasize the feeling that their “others” are gaining ground.  
- Treat the countdown as a *soft time limit* for the investigation: every long rest or major delay advances the reflection phase.

## Key Beats for Session Flow
1. **Opening Scene – The Fountain Reflection**  
   - Describe the shadows fading into fog; slight ripples on the water mirror.  
   - Establish tension and paranoia immediately.

2. **Exploration Segment**  
   - Let them pick 1–2 POIs to visit.  
   - Give meaningful discoveries (rune patterns, survivor journals, etc.).  
   - Sprinkle mirror incidents (a reflection lingers, glass hums, etc.).

3. **Rising Distortion**  
   - NPCs repeat words the players said minutes ago.  
   - Sounds begin to echo before they’re spoken.  
   - Optional quick hazard or illusion puzzle if pacing drags.

4. **Approaching the Finale Hook**  
   - As dusk deepens, have the *mirrors act ahead of them* — their copies walking toward the southern marsh.  
   - The fountain water begins turning black again; bell toll syncs with footsteps.

5. **Session Cliffhanger**  
   - The reflections pause at the fountain’s edge, turn to face the party.  
   - The world stutters — one second of silence, then whispers:  
     > “You’re late.”  
   - End session here, setting up entry into the **Nightmare Realm** (Session 8).

---

## DM Tone Notes
- Keep pacing *dreamlike but accelerating*; each scene should feel slightly shorter or faster than the last.  
- Highlight environmental unease over combat.  
- Reinforce that something intelligent — their own reflections — is herding them toward the fountain.  
- Use the countdown to maintain urgency without enforcing a visible timer.
