# Duskwatch Minidungeon – “Echoes Beneath the Well”
*A collapsed dream-space formed from a parasitic echo entity feeding on the fears and memories of Duskwatch.*

The minidungeon represents the **deepest layer of the nightmare-projection**, a place where the Echo-Born anomaly draws its strength.  
It is not a physical dungeon beneath the town, but a **collapsed mental landscape** anchored to the **well** — where the projection is strongest.

This space uses the thoughts, memories, fears, and authority-figures of Duskwatch’s villagers and scouts as raw material.

The parasite manipulates **emotion**, not spells; the structure of this dungeon is upheld by remnants of a **failed high-level Arcanum illusion** used long ago in an attempt to stabilize a different magical anomaly.  
The parasite consumed parts of that illusion, reshaping it into this distorted dream-realm.

---

# 🜁 Entry – The Well

The minidungeon begins when the party enters the **Nightmare Realm** and approaches the **well**, or when the anomaly pulls them in during moments of instability.

### Entry Sequence
- The well becomes impossibly deep.  
- The water turns mirror-like and then vanishes.  
- Buildings around the square “bend” inward as if pulled toward the well.  
- The cobblestones crack in a slow spiral.  
- Sound distorts; reflections linger too long.  
- A dull pulse emanates from the darkness below.

Stepping forward causes the ground to **fall away like paper**, dropping the party into the dream-dungeon.

---

# 🜁 Structure Overview

The dream-dungeon has **three encounters**, each representing a theme:

### 1. **Echoes of the Self**  
PCs confront the shadows that have followed them through Duskwatch.

### 2. **Echoes of Authority**  
Distorted illusions of Crownwatch, Cradle Tower, and undead imagery flood the space — a manifestation of the villagers’ fear of judgment, not literal prophecy.

### 3. **The Heart of the Anomaly**  
A circular arena where the Hollow Dreamer fights to maintain its hold on the village.

# 🜁 Encounter 1 — Echoes of the Self

The players find themselves in a narrow, shifting alleyway formed from broken fragments of Duskwatch buildings.

### Encounter Concept  
Their **shadows finally catch up** — the ones seen at the fountain.

These are not perfect copies, but distorted impressions of the PCs based on:
- villagers' memories of them,  
- fragments the parasite consumed,  
- and the parasite’s own incomplete interpretation of their identities.

### Appearance
- Movement is frame-skipped, like watching themselves in a broken mirror.  
- Shadows are slightly too tall or too thin.  
- Eyes reflect scenes from Duskwatch.  
- Each shadow “glitches” when struck or when a PC succeeds a Wis save.

### Mechanics (Guidelines)
- CR ~1/2–1 versions of each PC with altered abilities.  
- On **failed Wis save**, a shadow gains an advantage die (d4/d6) to next attack or effect.  
- On **successful Wis save**, a shadow loses reactions or suffers a “glitch” penalty.

**Goal:**  
Symbolic confrontation.  
Not deadly, but unnerving and emotionally charged.

### Transition
When the last shadow falls, the walls crack like glass and collapse into blackness.

---

# 🜁 Encounter 2 — Echoes of Authority

The party lands in a **vast liminal space** — half street, half training yard, half collapsing dream.  
The parasite manifests imagery not of truth, but of **collective fear**:

### Elements Present
- **Crownwatch silhouettes**: too tall, armor melted and fused.  
- **Undead fragments**: half-bones, half-memories, not fully formed.  
- **Crumbling Caer Hollow architecture**: rooftops falling, smoke rising.  
- **A distant, impossible silhouette of the Cradle Tower**:  
  projected like a nightmare impression, not a real vision.

### Why These Appear
Villagers fear:
- Crownwatch discipline,  
- border undead raids,  
- Arcanum scrutiny,  
- the overwhelming presence of the Cradle Tower.

The parasite replays those fears without understanding meaning.

### Encounter Style
A **skill-and-survival gauntlet**, not a full combat:
- Wis saves to resist fear distortions  
- Dex saves for falling debris  
- Str saves when “specters” try to pull them down  
- Psychic damage from Crownwatch illusions that swing ghostly halberds

**The illusions never bleed.**  
They shatter like glass when bypassed or “killed.”

### Collapse Transition
The sky cracks open.  
The silhouette of the Cradle Tower breaks apart.  
The ground folds into a perfect circle.

The party falls into the final arena.

# 🜁 Encounter 3 — Heart of the Anomaly
*A separate note will hold the full statblock and mechanics.*

The final arena is where the Echo-Born entity, known to the villagers as the **Hollow Dreamer**, attempts to defend its existence.

Arena traits:
- Floating circular stone platform  
- Broken Duskwatch architecture drifting in the void  
- Ribbon-like “memory tendrils” surrounding the arena  
- Whispers echoing earlier scenes from the village  
- Terrain shifts reflecting collective fear

# 🜁 Exit

When the Hollow Dreamer is defeated:
- the arena ruptures inward,  
- the projection collapses,  
- dream-fragments dissolve like ash,  
- and the party awakens in Duskwatch near the well.

The pulse is gone.  
The village begins to stir.
