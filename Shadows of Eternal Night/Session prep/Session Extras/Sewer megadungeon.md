## General Atmosphere

On entry, read or paraphrase:

> _The maintenance hatch drops them into the upper sewer channel — a low stone passage, ceiling barely eight feet, water running ankle-deep along a central groove. The smell is what it always is. But underneath it, something else: warm air, moving upward from somewhere below. And a hum. Low and constant, felt in the chest more than heard, like standing near a struck bell long after the tone has started to fade._

**Persistent environmental progression as they descend:**

|Depth|Temperature|Sound|Light|Stonework|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|Zone 1|Cool, damp|Distant hum, barely felt|Lanterns only|Original sewer — worn, cracked from below|
|Zone 2|Noticeably warmer|Hum directional, coming from ahead|Bioluminescent fungus begins|Fitted stone, metal bracketing with geometric runes|
|Zone 3|Hot, like near a forge|Hum constant, hard to ignore|Fungus dense, then absent near the node|Dressed stone, runes floor to ceiling|
|Zone 4|Uncomfortably hot|A tone, audible and consistent|Nothing grows here|Older than Zone 3. The node predates the construction.|

**Vixen (Scrap's fox):** Uneasy from entry. Ears flat, tail low, presses close. Does not bark. Becomes more agitated the closer the party gets to active Lattice construction. Goes completely still at the Zone 4 threshold. Will not enter Zone 4.

## Zone 1 — The Upper Channels

**Ceiling height:** 8ft  
**Floor:** Ankle-deep water in central channel groove  
**Stonework:** Original sewer construction — worn limestone, cracked from below (not above)  
**Estimated rooms:** 5–8 before Zone 2 transition  
**Tone:** Mundane but wrong. Standard sewer, except the rats are too coordinated and the cracks go the wrong way.

### Unique Rooms (must include)

#### U1-A — Entry Chamber

_Small room, ~3×3 squares_

The room directly beneath the maintenance hatch from Smokestack Row. Iron rungs set into the wall. The hatch above is heavy — DC 12 Athletics to open from below. A maintenance log on a rusted hook lists inspection dates; the last entry is four months ago. Water stains on the walls show the channel has been running higher than usual.

- **Fixed contents:** maintenance log (flavour only), iron rungs, hatch
- **No encounter here**

#### U1-B — The Modified Wall

_Corridor or alcove, ~2×4 squares_

A section of tunnel where original sewer stone has been cut away and replaced with cleaner, newer work. The joins are precise. This was done by someone who knew what they were doing.

- **DC 13 Investigation:** The reinforcement follows the channel's direction deliberately — structural support for something further in, not repair work. The mortar is fresh. Months old, not years.
- **DC 11 Perception:** Small geometric scratch-marks on the newer stones — the same basic forms as the rune bracketing they will see in Zone 2, but rougher, like preliminary notation.
- **No encounter here**

#### U1-C — Rat Encounter Room

_Medium room or wide corridor, ~4×5 squares_

The first combat. The rats move with unsettling coordination — no swarming chaos, just purposeful. They come from a single direction and retreat when below half numbers, back the way they came.

- **Encounter:** 2× Giant Rat (modified) — Bite deals +1d4 force damage. On a hit, the rat does not release; costs the target a bonus action to shake free.
- **Pace:** Keep this fast. It is a warm-up, not the session's main event.
- **After combat:** A DC 10 Perception check notices all the dead rats are facing the same direction — toward the south, toward the deeper tunnels.

## Zone 1 → Zone 2 Transition

**The Stone Arch**

A section of tunnel has been fitted with a new arch — cleaner and more precise than anything around it. The stonework beyond it is different: newer, deliberate, fitted rather than laid. The ceiling rises noticeably (from 8ft to 12ft) the moment they cross the threshold.

- **This is a fixed, hard transition point. Mark it on your map.**
- **DC 11 Arcana or Investigation:** The arch itself is load-bearing for something — it is not decorative. The keystoneis etched with a symbol that matches nothing in Zone 1.
- **Atmosphere shift:** The hum becomes directional here. It is coming from ahead and below.

## Zone 2 — The Modified Tunnels

**Ceiling height:** 12ft  
**Floor:** Water mostly channelled to sides — walkable dry centre in most rooms  
**Stonework:** Fitted stone, metal bracketing etched with geometric runes  
**Light:** Bioluminescent fungus in clusters — faint blue-green, deliberately cultivated at regular intervals  
**Estimated rooms:** 6–9 before Zone 3 transition  
**Tone:** Someone built this. The wrongness is intentional.

### Unique Rooms (must include)

#### U2-A — The Crocodile Channel

_Large room or wide corridor, ~5×7 squares. Must include water channel running lengthwise._

The main sewer channel widens here. Water runs 2–3ft deep along a central groove wide enough for the creature. The room feels watched before anything happens.

**The Lurker:** A sewer crocodile that has been living too close to an active Lattice feed point for too long. One eye replaced by something crystalline. Scales partially translucent, showing the musculature beneath. Movements slightly too fast for its size. It is not mindless — it watches the party before it attacks.

- **Stat block:** Crocodile (CR 1/2) with modifications:
    - HP 45
    - Speed 30ft land, 40ft swim
    - Bite: +1d6 force damage
    - Reaction — _Arcane Lurch:_ When reduced below half HP, all creatures within 10ft make DC 13 Dex save or take 2d6 force damage

> **Scrap spotlight:** Before attacking, the crocodile pauses near Vixen — not aggressively, but with confused attention. Give Scrap a binary choice: _pull her back, or hold still and see what it does._ If Scrap holds still, the crocodile turns away from Vixen and toward the party — it cannot process her properly. She registers as something other than prey. The croc turns away first. Let that moment breathe.

#### U2-B — Undead Patrol Corridor

_Long corridor or looping passage, ~3×8 squares (or split across two rooms). Must connect back on itself to form a patrol loop._

Two modified sewer workers, undead. They were placed here — not wandering, not lost. They patrol a fixed route and respond to intrusion. Their coats bear Arcanum construction worker insignia.

- **Stat block:** Zombie (standard) with addition:
    - _Arcane Alarm:_ On destruction, emits a pulse detectable to anyone attuned to Lattice notation within 500ft. Note for future sessions — not relevant now.
    - They do not pursue beyond the end of Zone 2.

> **The insignia is the important detail.** DC 11 Investigation on the bodies finds it. The Arcanum does not officially use undead labour. Someone decided these workers were more useful dead. Let that sit without comment.

## Zone 2 → Zone 3 Transition

**The Descent Passage**

The fitted stone tunnel narrows slightly and slopes downward — noticeably, not steeply. The temperature rises sharply over 20–30 feet. The hum has become constant. Spellcasters may begin to feel it as mild pressure behind the eyes.

- **This is a fixed transition. Mark the slope on your map.**
- **For spellcasters:** DC 12 Constitution. On a failure, disadvantage on Concentration checks until they leave Zone 3. (This is the first mechanical signal that the Lattice is doing something to the environment.)
- **Atmosphere:** The fungus stops here. There is nothing living in the passage beyond this point. The stonework below is older than Zone 2, not newer.

## Zone 3 — The Construction Site

**Ceiling height:** 20ft  
**Floor:** Levelled, fitted. Iron channels run parallel across most rooms toward the south wall.  
**Stonework:** Dressed stone. Geometric runes on every surface — floor, walls, ceiling.  
**Light:** No fungus. The runes themselves emit faint luminescence — just enough to see.  
**Estimated rooms:** 3–5 (smaller zone — the space is purposeful, not sprawling)  
**Tone:** Someone is building something here and they are not finished. The wrongness here is architectural.

> **Iron channels as terrain:** The parallel iron channels running across the floors are raised approximately 2 inches. Running across them is difficult terrain. They run roughly north–south in most rooms. Moving parallel to them (east–west) is unaffected.

### Unique Rooms (must include)

#### U3-A — The Main Construction Chamber

_Large room, exactly ~6×4 squares (30×20ft). This is the session's centrepiece combat room._

Carved from bedrock below the original sewer foundations — this space was not here six months ago. Thirty feet across, twenty feet high. Construction materials stacked along north and west walls (cover). Iron channels in parallel lines crossing the floor. Runes on every surface.

**The Foreman** occupies the far (south) end. He was once human. He is alive, technically. His skin has taken a faint crystalline quality. His eyes are the same purple-black as the node. He does not react to the party's entrance with alarm — he focuses on them with the attention of something that has been given a task and will not be interrupted.

- **Stat block:** Mage (CR 6) with modifications:
    
    - Loses _Spellcasting_ — replaced with _Lattice Channel:_ ranged spell attack +5, 60ft, 3d8 force damage on hit
    - _Crystalline Skin:_ resistance to non-magical bludgeoning, piercing, slashing
    - _Arcane Anchor:_ advantage on all saving throws while within 10ft of any Lattice construction
    - _On death:_ 10ft radius burst, DC 14 Dex save, 3d6 force damage
- **Tactics:** The Foreman stays south, uses iron channels and distance. He does not flee. He does not speak unless a character addresses him — and even then, his responses are functional rather than emotional. He has a job.
    
- **Terrain:** Stacked materials (north/west walls) provide half cover. Iron channels are difficult terrain east-west. The room is wide enough that ranged combat is viable — use it against the party.
    

> **Void shard opportunity:** The Foreman's Lattice Channel attacks are the first natural occasion where absorbing a spell would be tactically appealing — and also the most dangerous place to experiment. If Samsons holds the shard toward an incoming attack, it absorbs, and he gets the first warning. See [[Void Shard]].

#### U3-B — Evidence Room

_Small adjoining room or alcove off U3-A, ~3×3 squares. Can be a side chamber or the same room — your call._

Construction materials here are more carefully organised. This is where documentation lives.

- **Surveyor's rod** — DC 12 Investigation. Etched with Arcanum Infrastructure Office notation. This is Felwin's requested item.
- **Construction ledger** — DC 14 Investigation. Lists materials delivered, dates, quantities. No names, but institutional format. DC 14 identifies it as Arcanum format. DC 17 identifies the specific office as Verrath's division of the Deep Research Index.
- **Ceramic insulators ×3** — visible without a check, if the party looks around. Specialised components not available outside the Arcanum.

> **Kyron:** The rune notation on the walls matches what he saw in the node chamber (or: DC 14 History to connect it to Arcanum architectural theory on ley amplification). One specific sequence near the far corner: DC 0 for Kyron — he recognises it. Not from a book. From the inside. Do not answer where from. Leave it unresolved. **Reward:** Advantage on one Arcana check in Zone 4.

## Zone 3 → Zone 4 Transition

**The Doorway Without a Door**

A fitted stone doorway — frame present, hinges installed, door not yet hung. The frame is warm to the touch. Beyond it, the air is noticeably hotter.

- **This is a fixed transition. The missing door is intentional detail — mark it on your map.**
- **Vixen:** She slows here. If Scrap is paying attention, he notices before anyone else.
- **Atmosphere:** The rune density on the walls drops suddenly past this threshold. Whatever is beyond does not need notation on the walls. The stonework is older. The hum becomes a tone.

## Zone 4 — The Anchor Chamber

**Ceiling height:** 15ft  
**Shape:** Roughly circular  
**Diameter:** ~6 squares (15ft)  
**Stonework:** Old. Older than Zone 3, older than the sewers. This was here before the rest.  
**Temperature:** Uncomfortably hot  
**Light:** The node itself emits a faint purple-black luminescence. Nothing else grows here.  
**Tone:** This room is the point. Everything else led here.

**This zone is a single room. No random table. No wandering encounter. No additional rooms.**

> _Nothing grows here._

### U4-A — The Anchor Chamber

_Single room, circular, ~6 squares diameter._

The anchor sits at the centre — the same dark purple-black crystal as any node the party has previously seen, but taller than a person. Radial channels cut into the floor run toward the walls and disappear into the stone. They glow faintly.

**Fixed contents:**

- The anchor node (see below)
- Radial floor channels — not difficult terrain, but visually striking
- The lockbox (see Ivor spotlight)
- Nothing else. The room's emptiness is part of what makes it feel wrong.

**The Node:**

|Check|DC|Result|
|---|---|---|
|Arcana|12|The node draws ambient ley energy upward and inward simultaneously — siphon and relay both. It takes from the earth and passes it somewhere else.|
|Arcana|16|The relay direction is consistent with all nodes encountered — they all feed the same destination. The geometry points toward the Scholar's Descent district. Cradle Tower.|
|Investigation|13|This construction is older than Zone 3. The node has been active for at least four months.|
|Investigation|17|The radial channels here are more extensive than any other node. This is not peripheral. The network routes through here.|
|History|15|The crystal composition matches theoretical descriptions of Weave-compression architecture — a branch of arcane engineering the Arcanum officially classified as dangerous and suspended research on eleven years ago.|

> **Kyron:** Advantage on one Arcana check here (from Zone 3 rune recognition). He gets it once — player's choice when to spend it.

**Tampering warning:**

The node is heavily warded. Physical contact without correct attunement:

- DC 16 Constitution save
- Failure: 4d10 force damage, knocked prone
- The environment warns first: air shimmers near the node, small objects placed near it vibrate and skitter away, Vixen will not approach within 10ft

> **Void shard opportunity:** If the ward's 4d10 discharge triggers and Samsons uses the shard to absorb it, it works — and warnings escalate. See [[Void Shard]].

**The Lockbox — Ivor Spotlight:**

Partially obscured behind a Lattice channel bracket on the chamber wall.

- **DC 14 Perception** to notice it
- **DC 13 Thieves' Tools** to open (unlocked is also fine if Ivor doesn't have tools — it is not heavily secured)
- **Contents:**
    - A folded document in cipher (unbreakable with current resources — thread for later)
    - A small vial of something dark and viscous (unknown — do not identify it this session)
    - A Crownwatch sigil (not City Guard — _Crownwatch_)

> **Do not explain the lockbox. Do not give the players the cipher key.** Someone else has been here. The party now has evidence of it. Let them carry the uncertainty.

**Scrap / Vixen:**

Vixen goes completely still at the threshold of this room. Not frightened — still, the way animals go still in the presence of something too large to process as a threat. She will not cross into the room. Give Scrap a moment to register this before anyone else speaks.
