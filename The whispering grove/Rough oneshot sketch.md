# The Whispering Grove

> [!info] Oneshot Overview **Level:** 1–3 (designed for new players) **Players:** 3–5 **Estimated Duration:** 3–4 hours **Tone:** Mysterious, wonder-driven, with a compassionate core **Theme:** Not everything strange is dangerous — sometimes the monster is just lonely

---

## Synopsis

A village on the edge of an ancient forest has been experiencing strange phenomena — livestock wander into the woods and return changed, coated in crystalline growths, behaving oddly. The party is asked to investigate. Deep in the forest they discover a realm of amethyst and violet fog, where the trees themselves have turned to crystal and whispers drift through the air. At the heart of it all is a purple dragon with the power to shapeshift and merge with the land — not malicious, but lonely, calling creatures to keep it company without understanding the harm it causes. The party must decide how to resolve the situation: through empathy, cleverness, or force.

---

## Act 1 — The Village of Thornwell

_Estimated time: 30–45 minutes_

### Setting the Scene

The party arrives at **Thornwell**, a small farming village nestled against the treeline of the **Wychwood** — an old-growth forest that the villagers have always respected but never feared. Until recently.

> [!note] Read Aloud _The village of Thornwell sits where tilled fields meet tangled forest. Smoke curls from chimneys, chickens scratch in the dirt, and everything looks... ordinary. Almost. At the edge of the village closest to the treeline, you notice a pen of goats. One of them catches your eye — patches of its fur glint in the sunlight, as if dusted with crushed gemstone. It stares at the forest with unblinking, violet eyes._

### Key NPC — Elder Maren

**Elder Maren** (she/her) is the village elder, a practical woman in her sixties with calloused hands and no patience for superstition — which is why the current situation frightens her. She doesn't believe in curses, but she can't explain what's happening either.

**What she knows:**

- Over the past two weeks, animals have been wandering into the forest at night and returning by morning — but changed. Crystalline patches on their hide, strange behaviour, glassy eyes.
- A goatherd named **Pim** (he/him) followed one of his goats in three days ago and hasn't returned.
- The forest has always been dense but normal. Recently, villagers report a faint purple glow visible from the treeline at night, and some claim they hear whispering on the wind — though they can't make out the words.
- Nobody has entered the forest to investigate properly. The villagers are afraid.

**What she wants:** Someone to find Pim and figure out what's happening to the animals. She offers what the village can spare — 50 gp pooled together, plus supplies and a place to rest.

> [!tip] DM Note — Teaching Moment This is a great place to introduce **skill checks** to new players. If they examine the crystallized goat: **Nature DC 10** reveals the crystals are amethyst, and they're growing, not painted on. **Arcana DC 12** reveals faint transmutation magic — something is slowly changing the animals. **Animal Handling DC 10** reveals the goat isn't distressed — it seems content, almost peaceful, but it keeps staring at the forest.

### Entering the Forest

The transition from normal forest to the dragon's domain should be gradual. Describe it in stages as they walk:

1. **First 10 minutes of travel:** Normal forest. Birds, rustling leaves, dappled light. Then the birdsong stops.
2. **After 20 minutes:** The bark on some trees has a faint violet sheen. The air smells faintly sweet, like lavender and petrichor. Tiny amethyst crystals grow in clusters around tree roots.
3. **After 30 minutes:** The fog rolls in — a low, violet haze that clings to the ground. The whispers begin. They're soft, indistinct, almost like someone humming a tune just out of hearing. The trees ahead are no longer wood at all — they're translucent crystal, catching and refracting light in purple and lavender. The party has entered the **Whispering Grove**.

> [!note] Read Aloud _The fog is warm against your ankles. The trees ahead are unlike anything you've seen — their trunks are smooth, translucent crystal, veined with deep violet. Light filters through them in soft purple beams. The ground is carpeted in moss that glitters with embedded gemstone. And the whispers — they're everywhere now. Not words, not yet, but a feeling. Welcome. Come closer. You are not alone here._

## Act 2 — The Crystal Forest

_Estimated time: 60–90 minutes_

This is the exploration and discovery phase. The party moves through the Whispering Grove, encountering its wonders and dangers. This act contains one **combat encounter**, one **puzzle**, and opportunities for roleplay and investigation.

### The Whispers

Throughout Act 2, the whispers are a constant presence. They grow clearer the deeper the party goes. Use them to:

- Hint at the dragon's emotions (loneliness, curiosity, a desire for company)
- Warn players of upcoming dangers ("_careful... the sharp ones... they don't listen to me anymore..._")
- React to player actions (if someone touches a crystal tree, the whispers sigh contentedly)

> [!tip] DM Note — Teaching Moment The whispers are a great way to give hints without breaking immersion. If the players seem stuck or unsure what to do, have the whispers gently guide them. This also teaches the new DM that **the world can communicate with the players** — not every piece of information has to come from an NPC with a quest marker.

### Encounter — The Shardlings

_Combat encounter — medium difficulty for level 1–3_

As the party pushes deeper into the crystallized forest, they enter a clearing where the amethyst growth is especially thick. Clusters of crystal jut from the ground at sharp angles. Something moves between them.

> [!note] Read Aloud _The clearing ahead is dense with crystal formations — jagged spires of amethyst rising from the earth like broken teeth. Between them, something shifts. At first you think it's a trick of the light refracting through the crystal, but then a shape detaches from the nearest cluster. It's vaguely humanoid, roughly the size of a child, made entirely of interlocking crystal shards. Its head swivels toward you with a sound like wind chimes. Then two more pull themselves free from the formations behind it._

**Shardlings** — use the **Needle Blight** stat block (MM p. 32) with the following flavour changes:

- **Appearance:** Humanoid figures made of jagged amethyst crystal, 3 feet tall
- **Needle attack → Shard Burst:** Same mechanics, reflavoured as launching crystal splinters
- **Claws attack → Crystal Slam:** Same mechanics, reflavoured as slamming with crystalline fists
- No changes to AC, HP, or damage — keep it simple

**Number of Shardlings:**

- 3 players → 3 shardlings
- 4 players → 4 shardlings
- 5 players → 5 shardlings

**Tactics:** The shardlings are not intelligent. They are fragments of the forest's magic that have become unstable — the dragon's power animating raw crystal without intent or control. They attack whatever is closest and don't retreat. The whispers during the fight sound distressed: "_no... stop... I didn't mean... they weren't supposed to..._"

> [!tip] DM Note — Teaching Moment This is the **combat tutorial**. Walk through initiative, attack rolls, damage, AC, and HP tracking step by step. For brand new players, consider the following:
> 
> - Roll initiative together as a group the first time, explaining what it means
> - On the first player's turn, walk through the full sequence: move, action, bonus action (if applicable)
> - Remind players they can do things other than attack — shove, dodge, help, hide
> - If a player goes down (drops to 0 HP), use it as a teaching moment for death saves — but with only 3–5 shardlings this is unlikely at level 2–3
> - After combat, prompt them: "What do you do with the remains?" — the crystal shards could be valuable (worth 5 gp as a handful of semiprecious stones, giving an early taste of loot)

### Finding Pim

After the combat, the party can find **Pim** nearby. He's alive but in a dreamy, trance-like state, sitting with his back against a crystal tree, stroking a crystallized goat that lies contentedly in his lap.

**Pim** (he/him) is a young goatherd, maybe 19. He's not injured or restrained — he just... doesn't want to leave. The whispers are louder near him, and he seems to understand them.

**What Pim knows (if they can get him to focus):**

- He followed his goat into the forest and got lost in the fog
- The whispers calmed him down. He stopped being scared. He felt welcome.
- He's seen "her" — a woman with violet eyes and silver-purple hair who tends to the crystal garden deeper in the forest. She spoke to him kindly and told him he could stay as long as he liked.
- He hasn't eaten in three days but doesn't feel hungry. He feels... perfect.

**Snapping Pim out of it:** A sharp stimulus — a slap, cold water, a loud noise — requires no check. But he'll be disoriented and frightened when the trance breaks, and he'll want to go back. **Persuasion DC 12** to convince him to stay with the group or head back to the village.

> [!tip] DM Note Pim is a mirror for what the dragon does — it doesn't hurt people, it _enchants_ them. This is important context for Act 3. The dragon is not evil; it just doesn't understand that trapping someone in comfortable numbness is still trapping them.

### Puzzle — The Singing Stones

Between the combat clearing and the dragon's glade, the party encounters a barrier — a semicircle of five tall crystal pillars blocking the path forward. The fog is thickest here, and the whispers have become almost musical, overlapping in harmonics.

> [!note] Read Aloud _Five pillars of deep purple crystal stand in a crescent before you, each one roughly seven feet tall, humming with a low, resonant tone. The fog pools between them like water, and beyond them you can see a faint golden-violet glow — the heart of the forest. But the fog between the pillars is solid. When you reach toward it, your hand meets resistance, like pushing against a warm, yielding wall. The whispers here are layered, almost like a chord — but something is off. Discordant. One of the pillars hums at a different pitch than the others._

**The Puzzle:** Each pillar produces a tone. Four are in harmony; one is discordant. The "wall" of fog won't part until all five pillars resonate in harmony.

**How to solve it:**

- **Perception DC 10:** Identifies which pillar sounds different from the others (the second from the left)
- **Investigation DC 12 or Arcana DC 12:** Notices that each pillar has a faintly glowing rune at its base. Four runes glow steady violet. The discordant pillar's rune flickers between violet and red.
- **The fix:** The discordant pillar has a crack running through it where a shard of dark, non-amethyst rock is lodged — like a splinter in the crystal. Removing it (a simple action, no check required, or **Strength DC 10** if you want to make them work for it) causes the pillar to shift tone, harmonize with the others, and the fog-wall parts.

**Alternative solutions the DM should accept:**

- A **bard or any character with a musical instrument** could try to match the correct pitch — **Performance DC 13** to harmonize the pillar by singing or playing the right note
- **Brute force** — hitting the discordant pillar hard enough (dealing 10+ damage in one hit) shatters it, which drops the barrier but also alerts the dragon and makes her wary (affects the tone of the Act 3 meeting — she'll be defensive rather than welcoming)
- **Dispel Magic or similar** if anyone has it — automatically works

> [!tip] DM Note — Teaching Moment This teaches new players that **problems have multiple solutions** and that the DM should reward creative thinking. If a player suggests something not listed here that makes sense, let it work. The puzzle also teaches the new DM how to set DCs — notice the progression: easy Perception to notice the problem, medium Investigation to understand it, and then the solution itself is either simple (pull the shard) or requires a moderate check. Layering difficulty like this makes puzzles feel fair.

## Act 3 — The Heart of the Grove

_Estimated time: 45–60 minutes_

### Meeting the Dragon

Beyond the barrier, the forest opens into a vast glade. This is the heart of the Whispering Grove — the most beautiful and most unsettling place the party has seen.

> [!note] Read Aloud _The glade is breathtaking. Crystal trees arch overhead to form a natural cathedral, their branches interlocking like stained glass in shades of violet, lavender, and deep indigo. The ground is soft moss studded with tiny amethyst flowers. In the center, a shallow pool reflects the purple canopy above like a mirror. And kneeling beside it, trailing her fingers through the water, is a woman._
> 
> _She's striking — silver-purple hair falling past her shoulders, skin with a faint luminous sheen, and eyes the colour of deep amethyst. She wears a simple dress that seems woven from fog itself. She looks up as you enter, and her expression cycles through surprise, curiosity, and something like hope._
> 
> _"Oh," she says softly. "You came. I've been... I've been hoping someone would come."_

This is **Iridessa**, the dragon in humanoid form. She uses the **Dryad** stat block (MM p. 121) for her humanoid form's statistics if combat becomes necessary in this shape, though her true form is much more powerful.

### Roleplaying Iridessa

**Her personality:** Gentle, earnest, slightly awkward. She speaks in a halting, careful way, as if she's not used to conversation. She is genuinely kind but profoundly lonely and doesn't understand why what she's doing is wrong.

**Her story (revealed through conversation):**

- She is a young purple dragon — not ancient or powerful by dragon standards, but her innate magic allows her to shapeshift and to merge with the land around her, transforming it into an extension of herself.
- She's been alone for a very long time. Other dragons are territorial and solitary; she found that unbearable.
- She discovered that by extending her magic into the forest, she could call creatures to her — make them feel safe, content, at peace. She thought she was giving them a gift.
- She doesn't realize the crystallization is harmful, or that the contentment she gives is a form of enchantment that robs creatures (and Pim) of their free will. If confronted with this, she's horrified — genuinely, not performatively.
- The shardlings were an accident. Raw magic that hardened into something she couldn't control. She's embarrassed by them.

### The Choice

This is the climax, and it should feel like one — not through a boss fight, but through a meaningful decision. The party needs to resolve the situation. Here are the likely paths:

**Path 1 — Empathy and Persuasion** The party explains the harm she's causing and helps her understand. This requires good roleplay and a **Persuasion DC 13** (lower if they brought Pim and can show her what the enchantment does to people, or if they are particularly compassionate in their approach). If they succeed, Iridessa is devastated but agrees to release the enchantment and stop calling creatures. She asks, plaintively, what she's supposed to do instead. The party might offer to visit, or suggest she seek out other dragons, or help her find a community — this is open-ended and there's no wrong answer.

**Path 2 — The Compromise** The party negotiates — maybe she can keep the crystal forest (it's beautiful and not inherently harmful) but she has to stop the enchantment and let the animals and Pim go. **Persuasion DC 11.** She agrees more easily to this because she doesn't have to give up everything. The forest remains transformed, becoming a local wonder rather than a threat.

**Path 3 — Force (the forest fights back)** If the party is hostile, threatens her, or attacks, Iridessa panics. She doesn't fight directly — she flees into the crystal, merging with the forest, and the grove itself turns hostile.

**Final combat (only if Path 3):**

- 4–6 **Shardlings** (Needle Blight stat blocks) erupt from the crystal formations
- The environment becomes hazardous: at initiative count 20, crystal spikes erupt in a random 5-foot area — any creature there makes a **DEX save DC 12** or takes **1d6 piercing damage**
- The goal isn't to kill the dragon (they can't reach her while she's merged with the forest) but to survive long enough to either destroy the central crystal in the pool (**AC 15, 20 HP**) which forces her to separate from the forest — or to call out to her and attempt Persuasion mid-combat (**DC 15**, harder because trust has been broken)
- If they destroy the central crystal, the forest begins to revert. Iridessa re-emerges in humanoid form, weakened and weeping. The enchantment fades. She doesn't fight. The tone is melancholy, not triumphant.

> [!tip] DM Note — Teaching Moment This act teaches the most important DM lesson: **the players will surprise you**. Have a plan for multiple outcomes but hold them loosely. If the party does something you didn't anticipate, that's not a failure — that's D&D working as intended. The dragon doesn't have to die. The forest doesn't have to be destroyed. The "best" ending is whatever ending the table finds satisfying.

## Resolution and Wrap-Up

However the confrontation resolves, the party returns to Thornwell. Describe the aftermath:

- **If they resolved it peacefully:** The crystallization on the animals fades over the next few days. Pim recovers fully, though he sometimes stares at the treeline with a wistful expression. The Wychwood retains some of its purple beauty — a local wonder, not a threat. Elder Maren pays the party and invites them to stay for a feast.
- **If they used force:** The crystal forest shatters and fades, leaving behind normal (if slightly glittery) woodland. The animals return to normal. Pim snaps out of it abruptly and is confused and frightened. The village is grateful but the mood is subdued. Maren pays them and thanks them quietly.

**Loot and Rewards:**

- 50 gp from Elder Maren (divided among the party)
- Amethyst shards collected from the forest or combat: 10–25 gp worth of semiprecious stones
- If they befriended Iridessa: she gives one character a **Shard of the Grove** — a palm-sized amethyst crystal that is warm to the touch and glows faintly in darkness (functions as a unique trinket; no mechanical benefit, purely narrative)
- XP: enough to reach level 2 if they started at level 1, or level 3 if they started at level 2 (adjust as needed for the DM's campaign plans — since this is a oneshot, leveling doesn't matter much, but it's good practice for the new DM to understand XP/milestone rewards)
