#### **Arrival at the Clearing**

- **Scene Description:**  
    The players step through the activated Fey Gate, leaving the corrupted forest behind. They arrive in a **small clearing** bathed in an ethereal glow. The air feels **cool and safe**, untouched by Emberblight’s corruption.
    
    - The clearing is surrounded by **towering golden trees** with leaves that shimmer and hum faintly.
    - **Dancing wisps**—tiny, glowing spheres of light—float lazily through the air, casting soft, multicolored light across the clearing.
- **Nyssa’s Commentary:**  
    Nyssa seems relieved, her form glowing brighter as she says:  
    _“This place is a sanctuary, a remnant of the grove’s untouched magic. But the gateway ahead is our only path forward.”_
    

#### **Notable Features in the Clearing**

1. **The Fey Gateway (Exit Point):**
    
    - On the far side of the clearing, a **second gateway** stands, its structure simpler than the Fey Gate they entered, occasionally the gateway flickers with a fiery red color.
    - It hums faintly, with **wisps occasionally flickering near it**, as though drawn to its energy.
    - **Nyssa’s Warning:**  
        _“Once we pass through that gateway, we’ll be close to Vaelthir. Rest here if you must, for we may not find such peace again.”_
2. **The Dancing Wisps:**
    
    - The wisps hover near the center of the clearing, occasionally zipping to and fro, leaving faint trails of light.
    - They seem curious about the group, drawing closer when approached.
3. **The Camping Spot:**
    
    - A **soft patch of grass**, untouched by corruption, offers an ideal place to rest.
    - A **small Fey spring** bubbles quietly nearby, its water clear and cool. Drinking from it restores **1d6 HP** once per player.

---

#### **Interacting with the Wisps**

- **Behavior:**  
    The wisps are playful and curious but wary of strong emotions. When players approach, they respond in kind.
    
    - Friendly interaction: The wisps **dance joyfully** around the player.
    - Hostile behavior: The wisps **scatter**, creating faint illusions like mocking laughter or shadowy images.
- **The Wisp Game ([[The Riddle Ring]]):**  
    The wisps offer a **game of trickery and cleverness** in exchange for a boon:
    
    - The wisps form into a glowing ring and hum a song-like phrase:  
        _“Play with us and prove your wit, and we’ll gift you strength to make darkness quit.”_
    - **Challenge:** The wisps ask a series of **riddles** or set up a Fey trick (e.g., making the players guess which of them holds a glowing gem).
- **Reward for Success:**  
    If the players win, the wisps gift the party a **Fey Charm**, granting:
    
    - **Boon of Unity:** During the final fight, one player can use the charm to give all allies **advantage on their next attack or saving throw**.
- **Failure Consequences:**  
    If the players lose or act rudely, the wisps scatter and give no boon. Nyssa says:  
    _“Fey do not take kindly to arrogance, but they rarely punish failure outright. Consider this lesson learned.”_
    

---

#### **Rest and Reflection (Camping Spot)**

- **Nyssa’s Role:**  
    During the rest, Nyssa encourages the group to reflect on their journey so far. She shares:
    
    - More about **Vaelthir’s fall** and her hopes for either his redemption or his end.
    - Subtle hints about the **Emberblight’s true nature** (if they’ve uncovered clues).
- **Player Interaction:**  
    Encourage players to discuss their feelings about:
    
    - The mission’s stakes.
    - The morality of redeeming or destroying Vaelthir.
    - Their evolving trust in each other.

---

#### **Transition to the Final Battle**

Once rested, Nyssa leads the group to the **Fey Gateway**, saying:  
_“Beyond this path lies Vaelthir’s domain. Be ready, for his corruption will not let us pass easily.”_  
The players step through, arriving near Vaelthir’s lair, setting up the final confrontation.
