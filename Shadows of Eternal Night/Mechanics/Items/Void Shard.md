# Void Shard

**Type:** Magical item — fragment  
**Current holder:** Samsons  
**Identified as:** A capacitor. Engineered carefully and precisely, designed to hold magical energy of an unspecified kind in an unspecified quantity for an unspecified period of time. It is not a complete object. It is a fragment — part of something larger, the architecture of which Identify could gesture at but not fully describe.  
**Physical description:** Dark purple-black crystal, palm-sized, carried in a small lantern clasp on Samsons's belt. It gives no light.

---

## Mechanic — Spell Absorption

The shard can absorb spells. If Samsons (or any party member) holds the shard toward an incoming spell, or Samsons actively channels a spell through it as an intentional action, the shard absorbs the spell entirely — the spell does not fire or take effect. It is stored inside the shard.

The shard gives no outward indication of how full it is. It does not glow brighter, grow heavier, or visibly change — the warnings are sensory and subtle.

**DM tracking:** Note each absorbed spell below, its damage value, and its effect. This information is used to calculate the burst if the shard breaks.

---

## Warning Progression

Warnings escalate as more spells are stored. Deliver these in the moment of absorption.

**First absorption:**
> *"You feel the spell pulled inward — not dissipated, but caught. The shard is warm in its clasp. Something about it doesn't feel entirely empty."*

**Second or third absorption:**
> *"The shard hums against your hip. When you look at it, the interior catches the light differently than it used to — there's a quality of pressure inside it, like a word someone is about to say."*

**Fuller shard (four or more absorptions, or high stored damage):**
> *"Wild, chaotic magic emanates from within the crystal. Whatever is in there is not resting."*

The players should feel, through this progression, that something is building. The explosion should be surprising in timing but not feel arbitrary — they were warned.

---

## Breaking Condition (TBD)

If the shard breaks, it releases all stored spells simultaneously in a burst centred on the shard's location. Damage and effects from every absorbed spell fire at once. Very likely to kill one or multiple players depending on what has been stored.

**Possible trigger conditions — choose or combine when dramatically appropriate:**
- Samsons takes a critical hit while the shard is on his person
- *Dispel Magic* or *Antimagic Field* is applied directly to the shard
- A stored damage threshold is reached (suggested: 30–40 total stored damage)
- A specific external condition determined later

Decide the breaking condition once the shard is fuller and you can calibrate against what has actually been stored.
